# Mosapride

**Generic Name:** Mosapride Citrate

**Usage:**

Mosapride is used to treat symptoms of functional dyspepsia (FD), a condition characterized by upper abdominal discomfort and pain.  It is specifically indicated for patients with symptoms like epigastric pain, postprandial fullness, early satiation, and nausea.  It's important to note that Mosapride is not a treatment for the underlying cause of FD, but rather aims to alleviate the symptoms.  It's often used in cases where other treatments have not been effective.  Prescription and usage should always be guided by a medical professional.


**Side Effects:**

Mosapride is generally well-tolerated, but side effects can occur.  These can include:

* **Common:** Diarrhea (most frequent side effect), headache, dizziness.
* **Less Common:** Constipation, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, rash, increased heart rate (tachycardia).
* **Rare but serious:** Prolonged QT interval (can lead to potentially fatal heart arrhythmias). This risk is heightened in patients with pre-existing heart conditions or those taking certain medications.

It's crucial to report any unusual or concerning side effects to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Mosapride is a prokinetic agent. This means it increases the motility (movement) of the gastrointestinal tract.  It achieves this primarily by acting as a selective serotonin 5-HT4 receptor agonist.  By stimulating these receptors, it enhances the contractions of the stomach and intestines, helping food move more efficiently through the digestive system and thereby alleviating symptoms of FD like bloating, fullness, and pain.
