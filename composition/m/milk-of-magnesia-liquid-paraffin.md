# Milk of Magnesia + Liquid Paraffin

**Generic Name:** Magnesium Hydroxide + Mineral Oil (Liquid Paraffin)

**Usage:** This combination is used as a laxative to relieve occasional constipation.  Magnesium hydroxide acts as a stimulant laxative, drawing water into the intestines, while mineral oil lubricates the stool, making it easier to pass.  It's typically used for short-term relief and not for ongoing daily use.  Always follow the dosage instructions on the product label.

**Side Effects:**  Common side effects can include:

*   Abdominal cramping or discomfort
*   Diarrhea
*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Gas
*   Rectal leakage (with excessive use)

Less common but more serious side effects can include:

*   Dehydration (due to diarrhea) – particularly in elderly or debilitated individuals
*   Electrolyte imbalance (due to magnesium absorption) – particularly in individuals with kidney problems
*   Aspiration pneumonia (if mineral oil is inhaled into the lungs) –  this is more likely if taken immediately before lying down.
*   Allergic reactions (rare)

**How it Works:**

*   **Magnesium Hydroxide:** This is a saline laxative. It works by drawing water into the intestines, increasing the bulk and softness of the stool, stimulating bowel movements.

*   **Mineral Oil (Liquid Paraffin):** This is a lubricating laxative. It coats the stool, making it easier to pass through the intestines.  It also softens the stool by preventing water absorption.

The combination uses both mechanisms for a more effective and quicker relief of constipation.
