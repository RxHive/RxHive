# Metoclopramide + Paracetamol

**Generic Name:** Metoclopramide + Paracetamol (This combination is not standardized across all countries.  The specific brand name will vary.)

**Usage:** This combination medication is typically used to treat symptoms associated with nausea, vomiting, and mild to moderate pain.  The metoclopramide helps to relieve nausea and vomiting by increasing the movement of the stomach and intestines, while the paracetamol (acetaminophen) reduces fever and pain.  Prescriptions are usually for short-term use for conditions like:

* Post-operative nausea and vomiting
* Migraine headaches with associated nausea and vomiting
* Gastroenteritis (stomach flu) with pain and vomiting.

**How it Works:**

* **Metoclopramide:** This is a dopamine antagonist and prokinetic agent. It works by blocking the action of dopamine in the brain, reducing nausea and vomiting signals. It also increases the motility (movement) of the gastrointestinal tract, helping to speed up the emptying of the stomach.
* **Paracetamol (Acetaminophen):** This is an analgesic (pain reliever) and antipyretic (fever reducer).
