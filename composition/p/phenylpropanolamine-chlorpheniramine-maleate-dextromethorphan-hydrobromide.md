# Phenylpropanolamine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate + Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide

**Generic Name:** Phenylpropanolamine + Chlorpheniramine Maleate + Dextromethorphan Hydrobromide

**Usage:** This combination drug is a decongestant, antihistamine, and cough suppressant.  It's typically used to treat the symptoms of the common cold or flu, specifically:

* **Nasal congestion:** Phenylpropanolamine reduces congestion by constricting blood vessels in the nasal passages.
* **Runny nose and sneezing:** Chlorpheniramine blocks the action of histamine, a substance released by the body during an allergic reaction, reducing these symptoms.
* **Cough:** Dextromethorphan suppresses the cough reflex in the brain.

* **Dextromethorphan:** Dizziness, drowsiness, nausea, vomiting, constipation.

**Allergic reactions** such as rash, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing are also possible.

**How it Works:**

* **Phenylpropanolamine:** Acts as a sympathomimetic amine, mimicking the effects of adrenaline. This causes vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels), reducing nasal congestion.
* **Chlorpheniramine:** Acts as an antihistamine, competitively blocking histamine receptors. This reduces the symptoms of allergy and the common cold related to histamine release.
* **Dextromethorphan:** Acts as a central nervous system depressant, suppressing the cough reflex in the brain.
