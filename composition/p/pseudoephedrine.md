# Pseudoephedrine

**Generic Name:** Pseudoephedrine

**Usage:**

Pseudoephedrine is a decongestant used to relieve stuffy noses and sinuses caused by the common cold, hay fever (allergic rhinitis), and other upper respiratory allergies. It's available in various forms, including tablets, capsules, and liquid solutions. It works by narrowing blood vessels in the nasal passages, reducing swelling and congestion.  It is important to note that pseudoephedrine is a controlled substance in many countries due to its use in the illicit manufacture of methamphetamine.  Therefore, purchase restrictions may apply.  Always follow the directions on the packaging and consult a pharmacist or doctor if you have questions.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of pseudoephedrine can include:

* Nervousness
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Insomnia (difficulty sleeping)
* Difficulty urinating
* Increased heart rate
* High blood pressure
* Anxiety
* Restlessness
* Tremors
* Dry mouth
* Nausea
* Vomiting

More serious, though less common, side effects can include:

* Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia)
* Stroke
* Seizures
* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, itching, difficulty breathing)


**How it Works:**

Pseudoephedrine is a sympathomimetic amine.  This means it mimics the effects of the sympathetic nervous system, a part of the autonomic nervous system that controls the "fight or flight" response.  It works primarily by binding to alpha-adrenergic receptors in the blood vessels of the nasal passages. This binding causes vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels), which reduces swelling and congestion, thereby improving nasal breathing.
