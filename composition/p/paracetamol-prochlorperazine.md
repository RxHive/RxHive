# Paracetamol + Prochlorperazine

**Generic Name:** Paracetamol + Prochlorperazine

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to relieve moderate to severe pain and nausea, often associated with conditions such as migraine headaches, postoperative pain, and other painful conditions accompanied by vomiting. It's crucial to only use this medication as prescribed by a doctor.

**How it Works:**

* **Paracetamol (Acetaminophen):** This is an analgesic (pain reliever) and antipyretic (fever reducer). Its exact mechanism isn't fully understood, but it's believed to reduce pain by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins in the central nervous system.

* **Prochlorperazine:** This is an antiemetic (anti-vomiting medication) and neuroleptic (antipsychotic). It works by blocking dopamine receptors in the brain's chemoreceptor trigger zone, which is responsible for initiating the vomiting reflex.  It also has some sedative properties, contributing to the relief of nausea and pain.
