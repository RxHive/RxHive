# Pioglitazone + Glimepiride

**Generic Name:** Pioglitazone + Glimepiride

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to treat type 2 diabetes. It works by improving the body's response to insulin and increasing insulin production.  It's typically prescribed when diet and exercise alone aren't enough to control blood sugar levels.  The doctor will determine the appropriate dosage based on individual needs and response.  This is not a first-line treatment and is usually used after other diabetes medications have been tried.

**How it Works:**

* **Pioglitazone:**  This is a thiazolidinedione (TZD). It works by making the body's cells more sensitive to insulin.  Insulin is a hormone that helps glucose (sugar) from the blood enter cells for energy. By improving insulin sensitivity, pioglitazone helps lower blood sugar levels.

* **Glimepiride:** This is a sulfonylurea.  It stimulates the pancreas to release more insulin.  By increasing insulin production, glimepiride helps lower blood sugar levels.

The combination of pioglitazone and glimepiride provides a dual approach to blood sugar control: improving insulin sensitivity and increasing insulin secretion.
