# Polyethylene Glycol + Propylene Glycol

Generic Name: Polyethylene Glycol (PEG) + Propylene Glycol (PG) Mixture (Note:  There is no single "generic name" for a mixture of these two.  The specific ratios and additives will vary significantly between products)

Usage:  PEG and PG mixtures are used in a wide variety of applications, depending on the specific ratio.  Common uses include:

* **Cosmetics and Personal Care:** As humectants (attract and retain moisture), solvents, and carriers for other ingredients in lotions, creams, shampoos, and other products.  PG often provides a smooth feel and aids in preserving the product. PEG can also act as an emulsifier, helping oil and water mix.
* **Pharmaceuticals:**  As solvents, excipients (inactive ingredients that help with formulation), and carriers in various drug formulations (ointments, creams, solutions). PG can also be used as a penetration enhancer to help drugs be absorbed more readily.
* **Food Industry:** As solvents, humectants, and stabilizers in food products.  (Note:  Regulations regarding use in food vary by country.)
* **Industrial Applications:**  In lubricants, antifreeze, and other industrial processes.


Side Effects:  The side effects depend heavily on the concentration, route of administration (topical, oral, etc.), and individual sensitivities.  Generally, PEG and PG are considered relatively safe in low concentrations. However, potential side effects include:

* **Topical:** Irritation, allergic contact dermatitis (rash, itching, redness), burning sensation.
* **Oral:** Diarrhea (especially with higher concentrations of PEG), nausea, vomiting.  In rare cases, more severe gastrointestinal problems can occur.
* **Inhalation:** Irritation of the respiratory tract, coughing.


How it Works:

* **Polyethylene Glycol (PEG):**  PEGs are a group of polymers with varying molecular weights.  Their properties vary depending on their molecular weight.  Lower molecular weight PEGs are more water-soluble and can act as humectants, solvents, and emulsifiers. Higher molecular weight PEGs have thicker, more viscous properties.
* **Propylene Glycol (PG):**  PG is a humectant (attracts moisture), solvent, and preservative. It helps to keep products moist and prevents microbial growth.  It can also enhance the penetration of other substances through the skin.

The combination of PEG and PG leverages their individual properties to create a mixture with specific characteristics tailored to the intended application.
