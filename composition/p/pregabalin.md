# Pregabalin

**Generic Name:** Pregabalin

**Usage:**

Pregabalin is primarily used to treat:

* **Epilepsy:**  It's used as an adjunctive therapy (meaning it's used in addition to other anti-seizure medications) to treat partial seizures in adults with epilepsy.  It may also be used to treat certain types of nerve pain.
* **Fibromyalgia:** It's used to relieve the pain and fatigue associated with fibromyalgia.
* **Diabetic Peripheral Neuropathy:** It's used to treat the nerve pain caused by diabetic neuropathy.
* **Postherpetic Neuralgia:** It's used to treat the nerve pain that can persist after a shingles infection.
* **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD):** It's used to treat the symptoms of GAD, such as excessive worry and anxiety.

**How it Works:**

Pregabalin works by binding to specific proteins in the brain and spinal cord called alpha2-delta (α2δ) subunits of voltage-gated calcium channels. By binding to these proteins, pregabalin reduces the release of neurotransmitters, including substance P and glutamate, which are involved in the transmission of pain signals and nerve impulses. This reduction in neurotransmitter release helps to reduce pain, seizures, and anxiety symptoms.
