**How it Works:**

* **Propranolol:**  A beta-blocker, it works by blocking the effects of adrenaline (epinephrine) and noradrenaline (norepinephrine) on the heart and blood vessels. This lowers heart rate, blood pressure, and reduces the force of heart contractions. In migraines, it may help by reducing the dilation of blood vessels in the brain.
* **Flunarizine:** A calcium channel blocker, it reduces the influx of calcium ions into nerve cells. This stabilizes cell membranes, potentially reducing the frequency and severity of migraine attacks.  Its exact mechanism in migraine prevention isn't fully understood.

* **What are the risks of combining these drugs?** The combined side effects of drowsiness, dizziness, and hypotension (low blood pressure) are significantly increased.  The risk of depression also increases.  Regular monitoring is essential.
