### Propranolol

**Generic Name:** Propranolol

**Usage:** Propranolol is a beta-blocker primarily used to treat:

*   High blood pressure (hypertension): It slows the heart rate and relaxes blood vessels, lowering blood pressure.
*   Angina (chest pain): It reduces the heart's workload, lessening chest pain.
*   Migraine headaches: It can help prevent migraine attacks.
*   Essential tremor: It reduces the shaking associated with this condition.
*   Certain heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias).
*   Hyperthyroidism: It helps manage symptoms related to overactive thyroid.
*   Performance anxiety (off-label use): It can help reduce symptoms of anxiety related to public speaking or performance situations.


**Dosage:**  Dosage varies greatly depending on the condition being treated, the individual's health, and other medications.  It is available in oral forms (tablets, capsules) and sometimes intravenous (IV) formulations, though IV administration is usually done in hospital settings.  Dosage must be determined by a physician.  Common dosages range from 10mg to 80mg or more per day, divided into multiple doses.  Children's dosages are significantly lower and strictly determined by a physician based on weight and condition.


**Side Effects:**

*   Common: Dizziness, lightheadedness, fatigue, nausea, diarrhea, constipation, sleep disturbances.
*   Less Common but Serious:  Slowed heart rate (bradycardia), low blood pressure (hypotension), shortness of breath, wheezing (bronchospasm â€“ particularly in patients with asthma or COPD), worsening of heart failure, hallucinations, depression.


**How it Works:** Propranolol blocks the effects of adrenaline (epinephrine) and noradrenaline (norepinephrine) on the heart and blood vessels, reducing heart rate, blood pressure, and the force of heart contractions.


**Precautions:**

*   Contraindicated in patients with certain heart conditions (e.g., severe bradycardia, certain types of heart block), severe lung disease, and some metabolic disorders.
*   Should be used cautiously in patients with diabetes, liver or kidney disease, or those who are pregnant or breastfeeding.
*   Interactions:  Can interact with many other medications, including calcium channel blockers, insulin, and some antidepressants.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist about potential interactions.


### Flunarizine

**Generic Name:** Flunarizine

**Usage:** Flunarizine is primarily used to prevent migraine headaches. It's less commonly used for other conditions like vertigo (dizziness).


**Dosage:** Dosage varies and is determined by a physician based on the condition and individual response.  It's typically taken orally once daily.  Children's use is generally not recommended.


**Side Effects:**

*   Common: Drowsiness, weight gain, fatigue, dizziness.
*   Less Common but Serious:  Neurological symptoms (e.g., depression, movement disorders), galactorrhea (excessive milk production), gynecomastia (breast enlargement in men).


**How it Works:** Flunarizine works by blocking calcium channels in brain blood vessels, helping to prevent the dilation of blood vessels that can trigger migraines.  Its precise mechanism of action isn't entirely understood.


**Precautions:**

*   Contraindicated in patients with Parkinson's disease, depression, or certain other neurological conditions.
*   Should be used cautiously in older adults and patients with liver or kidney disease.
*   Interactions: Can interact with other medications, including some antidepressants and sedatives.
