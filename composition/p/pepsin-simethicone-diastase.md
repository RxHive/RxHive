# Pepsin + Simethicone + Diastase

**Generic Name:** Pepsin + Simethicone + Diastase (This is a combination product, so there isn't one single generic name for the entire combination.)

**Usage:** This combination of medications is typically used to relieve symptoms associated with indigestion and bloating.  It's often found in over-the-counter (OTC) antacid preparations.  Specifically:

* **Pepsin:** Aids in protein digestion.  It may be helpful for individuals with insufficient natural pepsin production.
* **Simethicone:** Relieves gas and bloating by reducing the surface tension of gas bubbles in the digestive tract, allowing them to coalesce and be more easily expelled.
* **Diastase:**  A pancreatic enzyme (amylase) that helps digest carbohydrates (starches).  It might be included to assist in the digestion of complex carbohydrates that may contribute to discomfort.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects are generally mild and infrequent, especially with OTC dosages. However, possible side effects include:

* **Pepsin:**  Rarely, can cause irritation to the mouth or esophagus if taken without sufficient liquid.  
* **Simethicone:**  Generally very well-tolerated.  Rarely, some individuals may experience mild constipation or diarrhea.
* **Diastase:**  Rarely, might cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to pancreatic enzymes.


**How it Works:**

* **Pepsin:**  A proteolytic enzyme (breaks down proteins) found naturally in the stomach.  It helps break down proteins into smaller peptides and amino acids, improving digestion.
* **Simethicone:** A defoaming agent. It doesn't break down gas but facilitates its release by making gas bubbles smaller and easier to expel.
* **Diastase:** An amylase enzyme that breaks down complex carbohydrates (starches) into simpler sugars, improving carbohydrate digestion.  This can lessen the bloating and discomfort associated with incomplete starch digestion.
