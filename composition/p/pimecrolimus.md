# Pimecrolimus

**Generic Name:** Pimecrolimus

**Usage:**

Pimecrolimus is a topical (applied to the skin) medication used to treat mild to moderate atopic dermatitis (eczema) in people aged 2 years and older.  It's not a cure for eczema, but it helps reduce inflammation and itching associated with the condition. It's typically used for maintenance therapy after the initial flare-up has been controlled by other treatments, or for long-term management of mild to moderate eczema.  It's important to note that it's not indicated for the treatment of acute severe eczema flare-ups.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding application frequency and areas to treat.

**How it Works:**

Pimecrolimus is a calcineurin inhibitor.  It works by suppressing the activity of certain immune cells (T lymphocytes) in the skin that contribute to the inflammation and itching associated with eczema. By reducing the activity of these cells, it helps to reduce the inflammation and alleviate the symptoms of atopic dermatitis.  It does not directly kill bacteria or viruses.
