# Polidocanol

**Generic Name:** Polidocanol

**Usage:** Polidocanol is used primarily as a sclerosing agent. This means it's injected into veins or hemorrhoids to cause them to shrink and eventually disappear.  Specific uses include:

* **Treatment of varicose veins:**  It's injected directly into the affected vein to cause inflammation and subsequent scarring, closing off the vein.
* **Treatment of hemorrhoids:**  Injected into hemorrhoids to reduce their size and alleviate symptoms.
* **Treatment of spider veins:** Similar to varicose veins, it's injected to close off smaller, superficial veins.
* **Local anesthetic:**  In some formulations, it can act as a local anesthetic, although this is less common than its sclerosing use.


**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the site of injection and the individual's response. Common side effects include:

* **Pain or burning at the injection site:** This is often temporary and mild.
* **Swelling or bruising at the injection site:** Usually resolves within a few days.
* **Redness or discoloration of the skin:** This is typically temporary.
* **Itching or irritation at the injection site:**  May be relieved with topical creams.
* **Allergic reactions:** Although rare, allergic reactions are possible.  Symptoms can range from mild rash to severe anaphylaxis.  Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.
* **Blood clots (thrombosis):** A rare but serious complication, particularly with larger veins.
* **Skin discoloration (hyperpigmentation):** This can be a long-term effect in some cases.
* **Nerve damage:** In rare cases, injection can cause damage to nearby nerves.


**How it Works:** Polidocanol works by causing irritation and inflammation within the targeted vein or hemorrhoid. This inflammation leads to scarring and sclerosis (hardening) of the vessel walls, causing the vein or hemorrhoid to close off and eventually be absorbed by the body.  The exact mechanism isn't fully understood, but it involves damaging the endothelial cells lining the blood vessel.
