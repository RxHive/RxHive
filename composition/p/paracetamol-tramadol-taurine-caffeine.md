* **Generic Name:** Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
* **Usage:** Analgesic (pain reliever) and antipyretic (fever reducer).

**2. Tramadol:**

* **Generic Name:** Tramadol
* **Usage:** Opioid analgesic for moderate to severe pain.
* **Side Effects:** Constipation, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, drowsiness, lightheadedness, respiratory depression (especially at high doses), potential for dependence and withdrawal symptoms.
* **How it Works:** Binds to opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord, inhibiting pain signals and increasing pain threshold. Also inhibits serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake.

* **Side Effects:** Generally well-tolerated, but potential for diarrhea, nausea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.
* **How it Works:**  Its mechanisms are not fully understood, but it's involved in various cellular processes, including osmoregulation, antioxidation, and neurotransmission.

**4. Caffeine:**

* **Generic Name:** Caffeine
* **Usage:** Central nervous system stimulant.
* **Side Effects:** Anxiety, restlessness, insomnia, increased heart rate, headache, digestive upset.
* **How it Works:** Blocks adenosine receptors in the brain, leading to increased alertness and reduced fatigue.
