### Side Effects

The side effects listed below represent potential side effects of the *individual* components, not necessarily the combined effect. The combined side effects could be more complex or varied:

**Pseudoephedrine:**

* Nervousness, anxiety, insomnia, headache, increased heart rate, high blood pressure.
* In rare cases, more serious cardiovascular effects.


**Cetirizine:**

* Drowsiness, dry mouth, fatigue, headache.


**Nimesulide:**

* Stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.
* Liver damage (rare but serious).
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).


**Serious but Less Common Side Effects:** Any of the above can occur more severely.  Seek immediate medical attention if experiencing serious side effects like difficulty breathing, chest pain, severe allergic reactions, or significant changes in liver function.


### How it Works

* **Pseudoephedrine:**  A sympathomimetic amine. It works by mimicking the effects of adrenaline, causing vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels) in the nasal passages, thereby reducing swelling and congestion.

* **Cetirizine:** A second-generation antihistamine.  It competitively blocks histamine receptors, preventing histamine from binding and reducing allergy symptoms like runny nose and sneezing.

* **Nimesulide:** A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID). It inhibits cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, reducing the production of prostaglandins, which are involved in pain, inflammation, and fever.


### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  People with certain heart conditions, high blood pressure, glaucoma, hyperthyroidism, or prostate problems should avoid pseudoephedrine.  Those with known allergies to any component should avoid the medication. Nimesulide is contraindicated in those with liver impairment.

* **Drug Interactions:** These medications could interact with other drugs. Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies being taken.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Consult your doctor before using any of these medications during pregnancy or breastfeeding.  Nimesulide, especially, should be avoided during these periods.

* **Children and Elderly:**  Dosage adjustments are required for children and the elderly for all three medications.

* **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking these medications, as alcohol can worsen side effects.
