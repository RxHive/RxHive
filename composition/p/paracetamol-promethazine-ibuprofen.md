* **Generic Name:** Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
* **Usage:** Relieves fever and mild to moderate pain.
* **Side Effects:**  Rarely serious at recommended doses.  Overdose can cause severe liver damage.  Allergic reactions are possible.

**Promethazine:**

* **Generic Name:** Promethazine
* **Usage:** Antihistamine with sedative properties; used to treat nausea, vomiting, and allergic reactions.  Can be used to enhance the analgesic effects of other medications.
* **Side Effects:** Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision.  More serious side effects are possible, especially with higher doses or in sensitive individuals.  Can cause extrapyramidal symptoms (involuntary movements) in some cases, especially in children.
* **How it Works:** Blocks the action of histamine, a substance released during allergic reactions, and has a sedating effect on the central nervous system.


**Ibuprofen:**

* **Generic Name:** Ibuprofen
* **Usage:** Relieves fever, pain, and inflammation.
* **Side Effects:**  Stomach upset, nausea, heartburn, dizziness.  Higher doses or long-term use may increase the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding, kidney problems, and cardiovascular events. Allergic reactions are possible.
* **How it Works:**  Reduces fever and inflammation by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins.  It reduces pain through a similar mechanism.
