# Propoxycaine
### Generic Name
Propoxycaine

### Usage
Propoxycaine is a local anesthetic, specifically a type of ester-linked anesthetic.  It was used primarily in dentistry, often in combination with procaine, to provide anesthesia during dental procedures. Its advantage lay in its rapid onset of action and relatively longer duration compared to procaine alone.  It was uniquely available in cartridge form for dental use.  Importantly, propoxycaine is no longer available in the United States, having been removed from the market in 1996.

### How it Works
Propoxycaine works by blocking the transmission of nerve impulses.  It achieves this by binding to and blocking voltage-gated sodium channels in nerve cell membranes.  These sodium channels are essential for the propagation of nerve signals, so by blocking them, propoxycaine prevents the transmission of pain signals to the brain.  Its mechanism also involved increasing the fluidity of cell membranes, potentially contributing to its anesthetic action.
