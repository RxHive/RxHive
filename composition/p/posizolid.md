# Posizolid
### Generic Name
Posizolid

### Usage
Posizolid is an oxazolidinone antibiotic with bactericidal activity primarily targeting gram-positive bacteria.  While initially investigated for the treatment of tuberculosis, clinical trials were discontinued, suggesting limited efficacy in this area.  Further research is needed to fully define its clinical applications.  Currently, it's not widely used clinically due to the discontinued trials and lack of subsequent approvals.

### How it Works
Posizolid's mechanism of action involves the selective inhibition of bacterial protein synthesis.  It achieves this by binding to the bacterial 23S ribosomal RNA of the 50S ribosomal subunit.  This binding prevents the formation of the 70S initiation complex, a crucial element in bacterial translation.  By disrupting this process, Posizolid effectively halts bacterial protein production, leading to bacterial cell death.
