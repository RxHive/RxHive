# Pleconaril
### Generic Name
Pleconaril

### Usage
Pleconaril is an antiviral medication primarily investigated for its activity against picornaviruses.  These viruses include rhinoviruses (the most common cause of the common cold) and enteroviruses, which can cause a range of illnesses such as viral meningitis, encephalitis, and other upper respiratory tract infections.  While it showed promise in treating these conditions, its use has been limited due to concerns about efficacy, the development of viral resistance, and drug interactions.  Currently, an intranasal form of pleconaril is under review, primarily targeting rhinoviral infections.  These infections, while usually resolving on their own, can be more persistent and problematic in individuals with weakened immune systems.

Different formulations (e.g., oral, intranasal) would have distinct dosage recommendations.  This information should not be used for self-medication; consult a healthcare professional for guidance.

### Side Effects
Common side effects reported during clinical trials included:

* Headache
* Diarrhea
* Nausea

Less common, but potentially more serious side effects may also occur.  It's crucial to report any unexpected or concerning side effects to a doctor immediately.


### How it Works
Pleconaril works by targeting a specific area within picornaviruses.  It binds to a hydrophobic pocket in viral protein 1, a key component of the virus's capsid (outer shell). This binding action prevents the virus from releasing its RNA (the genetic material needed for replication). In the case of rhinoviruses, pleconaril also interferes with the virus's ability to attach to and infect host cells. By blocking these critical steps in the viral life cycle, pleconaril inhibits viral replication and spread.

### Precautions
Pleconaril has shown potential interactions with oral contraceptives.  Women using oral contraceptives should discuss this potential interaction with their doctor before starting pleconaril treatment.  Further,  limited data is available on its safety during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult their physician before taking pleconaril.  Individuals with underlying health conditions should also inform their doctor before initiating treatment.  As with all medications, pleconaril should be used as prescribed and only under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
