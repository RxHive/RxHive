# Pyrazinamide + Isoniazid + Rifampicin + Vitamin B

* **Isoniazid:** Peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage, numbness, tingling), liver problems (hepatitis), gastrointestinal upset, drug-induced lupus (a type of autoimmune disease).
* **Rifampicin:** Liver problems (hepatitis), gastrointestinal upset, discoloration of body fluids (urine, tears, sweat), flu-like symptoms, allergic reactions.
* **Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine):**  Generally well-tolerated, but high doses can cause nausea and other gastrointestinal issues.


**How it Works:**

* **Pyrazinamide:**  Its exact mechanism isn't fully understood, but it is believed to inhibit bacterial growth and kill Mycobacterium tuberculosis.  It's particularly effective in the sterilizing phase of TB treatment.
* **Isoniazid:** Inhibits the synthesis of mycolic acids, which are essential components of the mycobacterial cell wall. This disrupts the bacterial cell wall and leads to bacterial death.

* **Vitamin B6:** Counteracts the effects of isoniazid on vitamin B6 metabolism, reducing the risk of peripheral neuropathy.
