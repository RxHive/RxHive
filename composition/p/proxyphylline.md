# Proxyphylline

### Usage
Proxyphylline is a bronchodilator, meaning it helps to relax and open the airways in the lungs. ### How it Works
Proxyphylline, like theophylline, is a methylxanthine derivative.  It works primarily by relaxing the smooth muscles that surround the airways in the lungs. This is achieved, at least in part, by inhibiting certain enzymes (phosphodiesterases) that break down cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) and/or cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP). Higher levels of cAMP and/or cGMP lead to muscle relaxation, widening the airways and making breathing easier.
