# Proparacaine + Chlorbutol
### Generic Name
Proparacaine Hydrochloride + Chlorbutol

### Usage

Proparacaine hydrochloride and chlorbutol is a combination ophthalmic (eye) anesthetic used primarily for producing topical anesthesia of the eye and surrounding structures.  This means it numbs the eye temporarily. Its primary use is to provide pain relief during brief eye procedures, such as:

* **Tonometry:** Measuring the pressure inside the eye (intraocular pressure).
* **Gonioscopy:** Examining the angle where the iris meets the cornea.
* **Removal of foreign bodies from the eye:**  Such as dust, small particles, or splinters.
* **Fitting of contact lenses:** To ease discomfort during the fitting process.
* **Other minor eye examinations or procedures:** Where temporary numbing is required.

Chlorbutol acts as a preservative in this combination, preventing microbial growth and extending the shelf life of the proparacaine.  It does not possess anesthetic properties itself.


### Dosage

Dosage should always be determined and administered by a qualified ophthalmologist or healthcare professional.  Self-medication is strongly discouraged.  There is no established pediatric dosage separate from adult dosage for this specific combination, as it's used for brief procedures and not ongoing treatment. Typically, one or two drops of the solution are instilled into the eye(s) 1-2 minutes before the procedure. ### Side Effects

* **Common Side Effects:** Burning or stinging sensation upon application (temporary), transient blurred vision, slight redness or irritation of the eye.
* **Less Common but Serious Side Effects:** Although rare, allergic reactions, such as swelling of the eyelids or face, itching, or difficulty breathing, can occur.  Corneal damage (rare but serious) is possible with prolonged or repeated use.  


If any adverse reactions occur, discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention.


### How it Works

Proparacaine hydrochloride is a topical anesthetic that works by blocking the transmission of nerve impulses in the cornea and other eye tissues. This prevents pain signals from reaching the brain, creating a temporary numbing effect.  Chlorbutol, as mentioned, is a preservative and doesn't actively participate in the anesthetic effect.


### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  Individuals with a known hypersensitivity (allergy) to proparacaine hydrochloride, chlorbutol, or any other components of the formulation should avoid using this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  While interactions with other medications are rare, it's essential to inform your ophthalmologist about all medications (including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements) you are currently taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The safety of this medication during pregnancy and breastfeeding hasn't been fully established.  Consult your doctor before use if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Other Precautions:** Avoid touching the tip of the dropper to the eye or any surface to prevent contamination.  Do not use if the solution is discolored or contains particulate matter.  Prolonged or repeated use should be avoided as it can increase the risk of corneal damage.


### FAQs

* **Q: How long does the numbing effect last?** A: The numbing effect usually lasts for a short period, typically 15-20 minutes.
* **Q: Can I use this medication for extended periods?** A: No, prolonged use can lead to corneal complications. It's designed for short-term use during specific procedures.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I use this medication if I wear contact lenses?** A: You should remove your contact lenses before application and wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting them.  Always consult your ophthalmologist or optometrist.
* **Q: What should I do if I accidentally get too much in my eye?** A:  Rinse your eye with lukewarm water and contact your healthcare provider if irritation persists.
