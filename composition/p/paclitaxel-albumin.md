**Side Effects:**

Side effects can vary greatly depending on the individual and the dosage. Common side effects include:

* **Neuropathy (nerve damage):** Tingling, numbness, pain, or weakness in the hands and feet. This is a significant and potentially debilitating side effect.
* **Myelosuppression (bone marrow suppression):** Leading to decreased white blood cell count (increasing infection risk), red blood cell count (anemia), and platelet count (increased bleeding risk).
* **Fatigue:**  Extreme tiredness.
* **Nausea and vomiting:**  Often managed with anti-emetic medications.
* **Hair loss (alopecia):**  Temporary hair thinning or loss.
* **Diarrhea:**  Can range from mild to severe.
* **Mouth sores (mucositis):** Painful sores in the mouth.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Rare but possible, ranging from mild skin reactions to severe anaphylaxis.


**How it Works:**

Paclitaxel is a chemotherapy drug that works by interfering with cell division.  It does this by stabilizing microtubules, which are essential components of the cell's internal structure.  By stabilizing these microtubules, paclitaxel prevents the cell from properly dividing and ultimately leads to cell death.

The albumin-bound formulation (like Abraxane) differs from traditional paclitaxel in that the drug is bound to albumin nanoparticles. This improves drug delivery, allowing for higher concentrations in tumors and potentially reducing some side effects associated with the traditional formulation.  Specifically, it may decrease the need for premedication to reduce hypersensitivity reactions and improve its effectiveness.
