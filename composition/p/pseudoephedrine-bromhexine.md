# Pseudoephedrine + Bromhexine

**Generic Name:** Pseudoephedrine + Bromhexine (combination product - brand names vary)

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to treat symptoms of the common cold and other upper respiratory tract infections.  Pseudoephedrine is a decongestant that helps relieve stuffy nose and sinus congestion. Bromhexine is an expectorant that helps loosen and thin mucus in the airways, making it easier to cough up.  Therefore, this combination aims to address both nasal congestion and chest congestion simultaneously.  It's important to note that this is often an OTC combination, but dosages and specific formulations vary by country and brand.  Always follow the instructions on the product label.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual and dosage. Common side effects may include:

* **From Pseudoephedrine:** Nervousness, dizziness, headache, insomnia, increased heart rate, high blood pressure, difficulty urinating.  Rarely, more serious cardiovascular events can occur.
* **From Bromhexine:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).

**How it Works:**

* **Pseudoephedrine:** This is a sympathomimetic amine that acts on alpha-adrenergic receptors in the nasal passages. This causes vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels), reducing swelling and congestion.
* **Bromhexine:** This is an expectorant that reduces the viscosity (thickness) of mucus in the respiratory tract.  It does this by altering the structure of mucopolysaccharides in the mucus, making it easier to cough up.
