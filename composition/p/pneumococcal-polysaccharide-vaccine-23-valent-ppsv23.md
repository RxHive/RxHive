# Pneumococcal Polysaccharide Vaccine 23 Valent (PPSV23)
### Generic Name
Pneumococcal Polysaccharide Vaccine 23-valent (PPSV23)

### Usage
PPSV23 is a vaccine used to prevent pneumococcal disease caused by 23 different types (serotypes) of *Streptococcus pneumoniae* bacteria.  Pneumococcal disease can range from mild ear infections and sinusitis to severe conditions like pneumonia, bloodstream infections (bacteremia), and meningitis.  The vaccine is primarily used in adults aged 65 years and older and in adults and children aged 2 years and older with certain underlying medical conditions that increase their risk of severe pneumococcal disease.  These conditions may include chronic heart disease, lung disease, diabetes, alcoholism, or immune deficiencies.  The vaccine can also be used in other situations as directed by a healthcare provider.

### Dosage

**Adults:** The dosage for adults is typically 0.5 mL given as a single intramuscular (IM) or subcutaneous (SubQ) injection. The recommended schedule varies depending on age, vaccination history (prior PPSV23 or PCV13 vaccinations), and underlying health conditions.  Consult the detailed recommendations provided by your doctor or the CDC.  Generally, for adults aged 65 and older, a single dose is recommended, unless previous PPSV23 vaccination occurred prior to age 65. In such cases, another dose might be given. If PCV13 (pneumococcal conjugate vaccine) is given along with PPSV23, specific time intervals between administrations should be followed.

**Children:** PPSV23 is not typically recommended for children under 2 years old. For children aged 2 years and older,  the dose is 0.5 mL, administered as a single IM or SubQ injection.  Vaccination should ideally follow completion of the recommended pneumococcal conjugate vaccine (PCV13) series, with a minimum interval of 8 weeks between PCV13 and PPSV23. Revaccination schedules are more complex and vary based on health status; professional guidance is essential.

**Dosage Adjustments:** No dosage adjustments are typically needed based on kidney or liver function.  However, always consult your physician for specific guidance.

### How it Works

PPSV23 is a polysaccharide vaccine.  It contains purified capsular polysaccharides from 23 different pneumococcal serotypes.  When injected, these polysaccharides trigger the body's immune system to produce antibodies against these specific serotypes.  These antibodies help protect against infection by these types of *Streptococcus pneumoniae*. The vaccine does not provide complete protection against all pneumococcal serotypes, meaning some strains can still cause disease despite vaccination.

### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  The vaccine is contraindicated for individuals with a history of severe allergic reactions (like anaphylaxis) to any component of the vaccine or a previous dose of pneumococcal polysaccharide vaccine.
* **Interactions:** While specific drug interactions are rare, inform your physician about all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, before receiving the vaccine.
* **Warnings:** Use caution in individuals with bleeding disorders, severe cardiovascular or pulmonary disease, or those receiving anticoagulant therapy.  The vaccine should be administered with care in such cases. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should discuss the risks and benefits of vaccination with their healthcare provider.  
* **Vulnerable Groups:** Older adults, people with weakened immune systems, and those with chronic health conditions are at higher risk of severe pneumococcal disease and should be vaccinated.


### FAQs

* **Q: How long does the protection from PPSV23 last?**  A: The duration of protection varies, but it generally provides several years of protection.  Booster doses may be recommended based on age and health status.
* **Q: Can I get the flu shot at the same time as PPSV23?**  A:  It is generally recommended to administer the vaccines separately, with a sufficient time interval to avoid potential interactions or masking of vaccine side effects.  Consult your physician for specific recommendations.
* **Q: What should I do if I develop a high fever after the vaccination?** A:  High fever is a relatively uncommon but possible reaction.  Consult your doctor for further guidance, especially if the fever is accompanied by other concerning symptoms.
* **Q:  Is PPSV23 safe for people with allergies?**  A: While most individuals with common allergies can safely receive PPSV23, severe allergies to vaccine components are a contraindication.  Discuss any allergies with your healthcare provider before vaccination.
* **Q:  How is PPSV23 stored?**  A:  Follow storage guidelines provided with the vaccine. Generally, it should be stored at refrigerator temperatures and protected from direct sunlight.
