**How it Works:**

Perindopril erbumine works by inhibiting the angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE).  ACE converts angiotensin I to angiotensin II, a potent vasoconstrictor (a substance that narrows blood vessels). By blocking ACE, perindopril erbumine prevents the formation of angiotensin II, leading to:

* **Vasodilation (widening of blood vessels):** This lowers blood pressure.
* **Reduced aldosterone release:** Aldosterone causes the body to retain sodium and water, increasing blood volume and pressure. Reducing aldosterone helps lower blood pressure.
* **Improved heart function:** In heart failure, it helps improve the heart's ability to pump blood.
