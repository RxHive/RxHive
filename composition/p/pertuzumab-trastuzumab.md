# Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab

**Generic Name:** Pertuzumab and Trastuzumab (combination)

**Usage:**  Pertuzumab and trastuzumab are used together in combination with docetaxel chemotherapy to treat HER2-positive metastatic breast cancer in adults. This combination is often used in patients whose cancer has progressed after treatment with trastuzumab and an anthracycline (like doxorubicin or epirubicin). It can also be used as an adjuvant therapy (after surgery) in certain cases.  The specific usage will depend on the individual patient's situation and the oncologist's recommendation. **How it Works:**

* **Trastuzumab (Herceptin):** This is a monoclonal antibody that targets the HER2 protein, which is overexpressed in many HER2-positive breast cancers. By binding to HER2, trastuzumab blocks the signals that stimulate cancer cell growth and survival.
* **Pertuzumab (Perjeta):** This is another monoclonal antibody that also targets HER2, but it binds to a different part of the protein than trastuzumab.  By binding to a different site, pertuzumab prevents HER2 from dimerizing (forming pairs) with other HER2 proteins or other receptor tyrosine kinases. This dimerization is crucial for cancer cell growth and survival.  The combined action of pertuzumab and trastuzumab blocks HER2 signaling pathways more effectively than either drug alone.
* **Docetaxel (Taxotere):**  This is a chemotherapy drug that works by interfering with cell division, preventing cancer cells from multiplying.

The combination of these three drugs attacks the cancer cells from multiple angles, increasing the effectiveness of the treatment.
