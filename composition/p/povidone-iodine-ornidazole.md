# Povidone Iodine + Ornidazole

**Generic Name:** Povidone-Iodine + Ornidazole

**Usage:** This combination is typically used for treating infections of the vagina (vaginitis) and other areas caused by a mix of bacteria, fungi, and protozoa.  It's often prescribed for bacterial vaginosis (BV), trichomoniasis, and candidiasis (yeast infections), though the specific efficacy depends on the infecting organisms.  The povidone-iodine component provides broad-spectrum antimicrobial action, while ornidazole targets anaerobic bacteria and protozoa.  **It's crucial to consult a doctor for diagnosis and prescription, as self-treating can be dangerous.**

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual and the specific formulation. Common side effects might include:

* **Local irritation:** Burning, itching, redness, or swelling at the application site.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Rash, hives, itching, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (seek immediate medical attention if these occur).
* **Gastrointestinal upset:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain (more likely with oral formulations, if available).
* **Metallic taste:**  A temporary metallic taste in the mouth.
* **Headache:** Although less common.

**How it Works:**

* **Povidone-iodine:** This is an antiseptic and disinfectant. It works by releasing iodine slowly, which kills a wide range of microorganisms (bacteria, fungi, viruses, and protozoa) by interfering with their metabolic processes and damaging their cell structures.
* **Ornidazole:** This is an antiprotozoal and antibacterial agent. It's effective against anaerobic bacteria (bacteria that thrive in oxygen-poor environments) and some protozoa, such as *Trichomonas vaginalis* (the cause of trichomoniasis). It works by disrupting the DNA of the target organisms, preventing their replication and leading to their death.
