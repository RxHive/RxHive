# Phensuximide
### Generic Name
Phensuximide

### Usage
Phensuximide is an anticonvulsant medication, specifically from the succinimide class.  Its primary use was in the treatment of absence seizures (also known as petit mal seizures), a type of seizure characterized by brief lapses of consciousness.  It was considered an alternative to ethosuximide, another succinimide anticonvulsant.  However, it's important to note that phensuximide is no longer commercially available.

### Dosage
Because phensuximide is no longer manufactured, specific dosage information is not readily available through current pharmaceutical channels.  Dosage recommendations, if found in older medical literature, would vary based on the patient's age, weight, and the severity of their seizures.  It was typically administered orally.  Any information found regarding past dosages should not be used without explicit guidance from a qualified healthcare professional.

### How it Works
The precise mechanism by which phensuximide works to control absence seizures isn't fully understood. However, it is believed to interact with the brain's neuronal systems involved in generating the characteristic three-per-second spike-and-wave discharges seen in absence seizures.  Some research suggests it may achieve this by influencing the levels of cyclic AMP and cyclic GMP in brain tissue.
