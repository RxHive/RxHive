### How it Works
Pirazolac belongs to the heterocyclic acetic acid subgroup of NSAIDs.  It works primarily by inhibiting prostaglandin synthetase, an enzyme involved in the production of prostaglandins.  Prostaglandins are substances in the body that contribute to inflammation and pain. By reducing prostaglandin production, Pirazolac helps to decrease inflammation and alleviate pain associated with conditions like ankylosing spondylitis and rheumatoid arthritis.  Studies suggest it may also influence the activity of neutrophils (a type of white blood cell) and the production of certain inflammatory mediators, further contributing to its anti-inflammatory effects.
