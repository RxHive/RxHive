# Pitofenone
### Generic Name
Pitofenone

### Usage
Pitofenone is an antispasmodic medication primarily used to relieve pain and spasms affecting smooth muscles.  It's often found in combination with other drugs like fenpiverinium bromide and metamizole sodium.  The primary application is for the short-term management of severe pain stemming from smooth muscle spasms, particularly when other treatments have proven ineffective.  This medication is currently available in certain regions such as Eastern Europe and India.

### Dosage

**Adults:** The recommended oral dose for pain and spasms is 50-75 mg.

**Children and Adolescents:** Pitofenone is not recommended for use in individuals under 18 years of age.

**Dosage Adjustments:**  The manufacturer's labeling does not provide specific dosage adjustments for patients with hepatic (liver) or renal (kidney) impairment.

### Side Effects

Common side effects may include:

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Skin rashes
* Itching
* Dry mouth

While less frequent, serious side effects are possible.  If any adverse effects occur, it's crucial to seek medical advice immediately.

### How it Works

Pitofenone works by suppressing and relaxing spasms in the smooth muscles of organs like the intestines, stomach, and urinary bladder, thus alleviating associated pain.  The precise mechanism of action may involve interaction with specific receptors within these muscles, though detailed pharmacological studies may not be widely available in English literature.

### Precautions

**Contraindications:** Pitofenone is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergy to pitofenone or any components of the combination product it's included in.

**Warnings:**  This medication is not recommended for children and adolescents under 18. Its use is restricted to short-term treatment of severe pain caused by smooth muscle spasms only when other therapies fail.

**Pregnancy and Lactation:** Avoid using Pitofenone during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

**General Considerations:**  Oral formulations should be taken after meals with water.
