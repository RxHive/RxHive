**Paracetamol (Acetaminophen):**

* **Generic Name:** Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
* **Usage:**  Relieves mild to moderate pain and fever.
* **Side Effects:**  At recommended doses, generally mild.  High doses can cause liver damage.  Allergic reactions are possible (though rare).
* **How it Works:**  Reduces fever by acting on the hypothalamus, the body's temperature regulating center.  Its pain-relieving mechanism is not fully understood but is thought to involve inhibition of cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes in the central nervous system.

**Codeine:**

* **Generic Name:** Codeine
* **Usage:** Relieves moderate to severe pain and can suppress cough.  Often used in combination with paracetamol for enhanced pain relief.
* **Side Effects:**  Constipation, drowsiness, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and respiratory depression (especially at high doses).  Can be habit-forming.
* **How it Works:** Binds to opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord, reducing pain signals and potentially suppressing the cough reflex.

**Ibuprofen:**

* **Generic Name:** Ibuprofen
* **Usage:** Relieves mild to moderate pain, fever, and inflammation.
* **Side Effects:**  Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, heartburn, stomach pain), headache, dizziness, fluid retention.  Can increase the risk of bleeding in some individuals.  Long-term use can damage the kidneys and liver.
* **How it Works:**  Inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, reducing the production of prostaglandins, which are involved in pain, inflammation, and fever.
