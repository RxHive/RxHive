# Phenylpropanolamine + Bromhexine

**Generic Name:** Phenylpropanolamine + Bromhexine

**Usage:** This combination drug was used primarily as an over-the-counter (OTC) decongestant and expectorant.  Phenylpropanolamine acted as a decongestant to reduce nasal congestion, while bromhexine acted as an expectorant to loosen and thin mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to cough up.  **However, it's crucial to note that phenylpropanolamine has been largely removed from the market in many countries due to safety concerns (see side effects).**  This combination is therefore rarely available anymore.  If you have a similar medication, check the current formulation.

**Side Effects:**

* **Phenylpropanolamine:**  The most serious side effect is an increased risk of stroke and other cardiovascular events, particularly in individuals with pre-existing hypertension or other cardiovascular conditions. Other potential side effects include increased blood pressure, headache, dizziness, nervousness, insomnia, and palpitations.
* **Bromhexine:**  Common side effects are generally mild and may include nausea, vomiting, gastrointestinal upset, and allergic reactions (such as skin rash or itching).


**How it Works:**

* **Phenylpropanolamine:** This sympathomimetic amine acts on alpha-adrenergic receptors in the blood vessels of the nasal passages, causing vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels). This reduces swelling and congestion.
* **Bromhexine:** This mucolytic agent reduces the viscosity (thickness) of mucus in the respiratory tract.  The exact mechanism isn't fully understood but it's believed to affect the chemical structure of mucus, making it less sticky.
