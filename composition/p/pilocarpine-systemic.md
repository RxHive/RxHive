# Pilocarpine (Systemic)
### Generic Name
Pilocarpine (Systemic)

### Usage
Pilocarpine is primarily used to treat dry mouth (xerostomia), a common side effect of radiation therapy for head and neck cancers.  It's also employed to manage the dry mouth symptoms associated with Sjögren's syndrome, an autoimmune disorder affecting moisture-producing glands.  Pilocarpine works by stimulating the production of saliva and other bodily secretions.

### Dosage

**Adults:**

* **Xerostomia (Radiation-Induced):** The initial dose is typically 5 mg three times daily (TID).  The dose may be adjusted upwards based on individual response and tolerance, with a usual range of 15-30 mg per day.  However, the maximum single dose should not exceed 10 mg.

* **Xerostomia (Sjögren's Syndrome):**  A common starting dose is 5 mg four times daily (QID).

**Children:** The safety and effectiveness of pilocarpine for children have not been established.  It is not recommended for pediatric use.

**Dosage Adjustments:**

* **Hepatic Impairment:**  Dosage adjustments are necessary for patients with liver problems.  Those with mild impairment (Child-Pugh score 5-6) may not require adjustment, while those with moderate impairment (Child-Pugh score 7-9) should start with a lower dose (5 mg twice daily, BID) and titrate upwards.  Pilocarpine is generally not recommended for patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh score 10-15).

* **Renal Impairment:**  The manufacturer's labeling doesn't provide specific dosage adjustments for patients with kidney problems.  However, careful monitoring is advised.

The medication is typically administered orally as a tablet.


### Side Effects

**Common Side Effects (Occurring in >10% of patients):**

* Chills
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Flushing
* Nausea
* Urinary frequency
* Weakness
* Rhinitis (runny nose)
* Diaphoresis (excessive sweating)


**Less Common but Potentially Serious Side Effects (Occurring in 1-10% or less of patients):**  These include, but are not limited to:  palpitations, tachycardia (rapid heart rate), diarrhea, vomiting, skin rashes, blurred vision, and many others.  A complete list is provided in the original document.

**Important Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list of all possible side effects.  If you experience any adverse effects, especially those that are severe or persistent, contact your doctor or healthcare provider immediately.

### How it Works

Pilocarpine is a parasympathomimetic agent, meaning it mimics the effects of the parasympathetic nervous system.  It works by binding to muscarinic receptors, which are found on various glands and smooth muscles throughout the body. This binding action stimulates increased secretions from glands like the salivary glands (resulting in increased saliva production) and sweat glands. It also affects the tone of smooth muscles in the gastrointestinal and urinary tracts.

### Precautions

* **Contraindications:** Pilocarpine should not be used in individuals with hypersensitivity to the drug or its components, uncontrolled asthma, or clinical situations where miosis (constriction of the pupil) is undesirable (such as acute iritis and angle-closure glaucoma).

* **Interactions:** Pilocarpine can interact with other medications. It may decrease the effectiveness of certain anticholinergic drugs (like cimetropium) and may have its effects increased by acetylcholinesterase inhibitors and beta-blockers.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Warnings:**  Use caution in patients with significant cardiovascular disease, cholelithiasis (gallstones), biliary tract disease, moderate hepatic impairment, a history of nephrolithiasis (kidney stones), or controlled asthma/COPD. Increased risk of cognitive or psychiatric disturbances and nephrolithiasis has been noted.

* **Pregnancy and Lactation:**  Pilocarpine is classified as Category C during pregnancy, meaning its safety has not been fully established.  Breastfeeding mothers should discuss the risks and benefits with their doctors before using this medication.

* **General Considerations:** Avoid taking pilocarpine with high-fat meals.  Maintain adequate hydration as dehydration is a potential side effect.


### FAQs

* **Q: How long does it take for pilocarpine to work?** A: The onset of action can vary, but generally, you may notice an increase in saliva production within a short time after taking the medication.

* **Q: Can I stop taking pilocarpine suddenly?** A: It's best not to stop taking pilocarpine abruptly without consulting your doctor.  They may recommend a gradual reduction in dosage to minimize potential withdrawal effects.

* **Q: How should I store pilocarpine?** A: Store pilocarpine at room temperature, away from moisture and heat, and keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.

* **Q: Are there any long-term effects associated with pilocarpine?** A: Long-term effects are generally uncommon with appropriate use and monitoring.  However, prolonged use may lead to some of the side effects listed above becoming more prominent. Regular checkups with your doctor are important to monitor your progress.
