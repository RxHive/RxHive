# Sofosbuvir

**Generic Name:** Sofosbuvir

**Usage:** Sofosbuvir is an antiviral medication used to treat chronic hepatitis C virus (HCV) infection.  It's typically used in combination with other antiviral drugs, depending on the genotype of the HCV and other factors determined by a physician.  It's not used to treat other viral infections.

**How it Works:** Sofosbuvir is a nucleotide analog inhibitor of the HCV NS5B polymerase.  In simpler terms, it interferes with the enzyme the virus uses to replicate itself.  By inhibiting this enzyme, it prevents the virus from making copies of its genetic material, thus slowing down and ultimately reducing the viral load in the body.  It is crucial to understand that Sofosbuvir is usually part of a combination therapy, not a standalone treatment.  The combination targets different parts of the viral lifecycle to maximize effectiveness.
