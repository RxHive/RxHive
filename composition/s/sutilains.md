# Sutilains
### Generic Name
Sutilains

### Usage
Sutilains is a topical enzymatic debriding agent derived from the bacterium *Bacillus subtilis*.  Its primary use was in the treatment of wounds, specifically for the debridement (removal of dead or damaged tissue) of necrotic (dead) tissue in conditions such as:

* **Burns:**  Sutilains helped to remove the dead, eschar tissue from burn wounds, promoting healing.
* **Decubitus ulcers (pressure sores):**  The enzyme helped to clean and prepare these wounds for healing by removing the necrotic tissue.
* **Other wounds:** Sutilains could be used for other types of chronic wounds where necrotic tissue needed to be removed to facilitate healing.

### How it Works
Sutilains contains proteolytic enzymes, which are biological catalysts that break down proteins. In the context of wound debridement, these enzymes selectively target and digest the dead, necrotic tissue in wounds.  This process helps to remove the non-viable tissue, leaving healthy tissue intact and promoting wound healing.  This process aided in reducing the risk of infection by eliminating a breeding ground for bacteria.
