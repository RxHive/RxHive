# Sulfadicramide
### Generic Name
Sulfadicramide

### Usage
Sulfadicramide is a sulfonamide antibiotic.  Historically, it was primarily used topically as an ointment to treat superficial bacterial infections of the eye.  However, due to the rise of bacterial resistance and the availability of more effective and safer alternatives, its clinical use has significantly decreased and it has been discontinued in most markets.  It's crucial to understand that its use is now largely obsolete.

### Side Effects
While the frequency of side effects would vary depending on historical use and individual response (information is limited due to the drug's discontinuation), sulfonamides as a class are known to cause a range of adverse effects. These include:

*   Allergic reactions (ranging from mild rash to severe, potentially life-threatening reactions like Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
*   Kidney problems (including kidney stones and damage)
*   Blood disorders (such as anemia, agranulocytosis)
*   Liver damage
*   Gastrointestinal issues (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
*   Photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight)

**Important Note:**  Because Sulfadicramide is largely unavailable, any experience with potential side effects should be reported immediately to a healthcare professional.

### How it Works
Sulfadicramide, like other sulfonamides, works by interfering with the bacterial synthesis of folic acid.  Folic acid is essential for bacterial growth and reproduction.  Sulfadicramide blocks the incorporation of para-aminobenzoic acid (PABA) into dihydrofolic acid, a crucial step in folic acid production.  By inhibiting folic acid synthesis, the drug prevents bacterial cell growth and ultimately leads to bacterial death.
