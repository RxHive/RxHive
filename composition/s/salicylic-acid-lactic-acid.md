# Salicylic Acid + Lactic Acid

**Generic Name:** Salicylic Acid + Lactic Acid (combination)  *Note: There isn't a single generic name for this combination, as the specific ratios vary between products.*


**Usage:**

This combination is commonly used topically to treat acne, improve skin texture, and reduce hyperpigmentation (dark spots). Salicylic acid is a beta-hydroxy acid (BHA) that exfoliates the skin by dissolving dead skin cells, unclogging pores, and reducing inflammation. Lactic acid is an alpha-hydroxy acid (AHA) that also exfoliates, but it hydrates the skin more and can be gentler on sensitive skin. **Side Effects:**

Common side effects can include:

* **Irritation:** Redness, burning, stinging, dryness, peeling, or flaking are possible, particularly with higher concentrations or during initial use.
* **Sensitivity to sunlight:**  Both acids can increase sun sensitivity.  Sunscreen use is crucial.
* **Allergic reaction:** Although rare, some individuals may experience an allergic reaction.
* **Increased dryness:**  Overuse can lead to excessive dryness and dehydration.
* **Purging:** An initial worsening of acne (purging) can occur as the skin sheds dead cells. This is usually temporary.


**How it Works:**

* **Salicylic Acid:**  This oil-soluble BHA penetrates the pores, dissolving sebum (oil) and dead skin cells that clog pores and contribute to acne. It also has anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling.
* **Lactic Acid:** This water-soluble AHA exfoliates the skin's surface by loosening the bonds between dead skin cells, promoting cell turnover. It also helps to improve hydration and skin tone, reducing the appearance of hyperpigmentation.

The combination of these acids offers synergistic effects, with salicylic acid addressing pore congestion and lactic acid improving skin texture and hydration, resulting in clearer, smoother, and brighter skin.
