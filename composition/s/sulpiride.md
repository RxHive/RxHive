# Sulpiride

**Generic Name:** Sulpiride

**Usage:**

Sulpiride is an atypical antipsychotic medication primarily used to treat:

* **Schizophrenia:**  It helps manage positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking) and, to a lesser extent, negative symptoms (flat affect, social withdrawal, avolition).
* **Depression:**  It's sometimes used in combination with antidepressants to treat depression, particularly in cases where antidepressants alone are ineffective.
* **Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS):** In some cases, it can be used to treat or help prevent NMS, a rare but serious reaction to certain antipsychotic drugs.
* **Nausea and Vomiting:**  Although less common now, sulpiride has been used in the past to treat nausea and vomiting, especially in certain gastrointestinal conditions.  This use is less prevalent due to the availability of other, safer antiemetic medications.


**Side Effects:**

Sulpiride, like other antipsychotics, can cause a range of side effects, some common and others less so. These can include:

* **Common:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, headache, insomnia, restlessness, anxiety, dry mouth, constipation, weight gain, galactorrhea (breast milk production), gynecomastia (breast development in men), menstrual irregularities.
* **Less Common but Serious:**  Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) - a rare but life-threatening condition characterized by high fever, muscle rigidity, altered consciousness, and autonomic instability; tardive dyskinesia (TD) - involuntary movements, particularly of the face and mouth, which can be irreversible; extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) - muscle stiffness, tremors, restlessness; prolonged QT interval (on ECG), increasing the risk of heart rhythm problems.
* **Other Potential Side Effects:**  Low blood pressure (hypotension), changes in blood sugar levels, allergic reactions.

* **Can I drink alcohol while taking Sulpiride?**  Alcohol can increase the sedative effects of sulpiride and should generally be avoided.
* **Can I drive while taking Sulpiride?**  Drowsiness is a common side effect.  Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how the medication affects you.
* **What should I do if I miss a dose?** Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is close to the time for your next dose.  Never double the dose.
* **Is Sulpiride safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding?**  The safety of sulpiride during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established. Consult your doctor immediately if you are pregnant, plan to become pregnant, or are breastfeeding.
