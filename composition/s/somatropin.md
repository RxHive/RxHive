# Somatropin
### Generic Name
Somatropin

### Usage
Somatropin is a synthetic version of human growth hormone (hGH).  Its primary use is to treat growth hormone deficiency (GHD) in children and adults.  GHD can result from various causes, including genetic conditions, brain tumors, or damage to the pituitary gland.  In children, this deficiency leads to significantly impaired growth. In adults, it can manifest as reduced muscle mass, increased body fat, and other metabolic abnormalities.

Somatropin is also used to treat Turner syndrome (a genetic condition affecting females), chronic kidney disease in children on dialysis, Prader-Willi syndrome (a genetic disorder impacting growth and appetite), and short bowel syndrome in children.  It may also be used off-label in some cases to treat muscle wasting associated with certain illnesses, though its effectiveness in these contexts is under ongoing research.  It's crucial to remember that using somatropin for purposes beyond those approved by a healthcare professional is risky and potentially harmful.


### Dosage

Dosage of somatropin is highly individualized and depends on several factors, including the patient's age, weight, condition being treated, and response to therapy.  A physician will determine the appropriate dosage. It's never to be self-administered or adjusted without explicit medical guidance.

**Children:**  Dosage is typically calculated based on body weight or body surface area. It's usually administered daily via subcutaneous injection (under the skin). The precise dosage will be determined by the physician based on the child's growth rate and response to treatment.

**Adults:**  Dosage varies depending on the specific condition and individual needs.  Similar to children, administration is typically via subcutaneous injection, and dosage is adjusted according to individual responses and ongoing monitoring.

**IV Administration:**  Somatropin is not typically administered intravenously. Subcutaneous injection is the standard route.  

**Forms:** Somatropin is available in various forms, including injection pens and pre-filled syringes for subcutaneous administration.  It's not available as an oral medication because it is broken down in the digestive system.


### Side Effects

Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Muscle pain
* Joint pain
* Swelling in the hands or feet
* Increased blood sugar levels
* Increased risk of carpal tunnel syndrome

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Increased risk of pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
* Slipped capital femoral epiphysis (SCFE) in children (a condition where the thigh bone slips at the hip)
* Benign intracranial hypertension (increased pressure in the brain)


If any significant or concerning side effects occur, immediately consult a healthcare provider.


### How it Works

Somatropin mimics the effects of naturally occurring human growth hormone. It binds to growth hormone receptors on cells throughout the body, stimulating growth and metabolism.  In children, it promotes bone growth and linear growth. In adults, it can help increase muscle mass, reduce body fat, and improve metabolic parameters.  The exact mechanisms involved are complex and involve interaction with various intracellular signaling pathways.


### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  Somatropin is contraindicated in individuals with active cancer, proliferative retinopathy (a complication of diabetes), and certain critical illnesses.

* **Drug Interactions:** Somatropin can interact with various medications, including corticosteroids and insulin.  It is crucial to inform your physician of all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements, being taken.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of somatropin use during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Use should be carefully considered and only undertaken under the strict supervision of a physician.

* **Children:**  Close monitoring of growth and development is necessary in children receiving somatropin.  Regular check-ups are crucial.

* **Older Adults:**  Older adults may be more susceptible to certain side effects.  Careful monitoring is recommended.
