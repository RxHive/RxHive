# Somatropin

**Generic Name:** Somatropin

**Usage:**

Somatropin is a synthetic form of human growth hormone (HGH).  Its use is strictly regulated and requires a prescription.  It's approved for several conditions, including:

* **Growth hormone deficiency (GHD) in children and adults:** This is the most common use.  GHD can be caused by various factors, leading to impaired growth and development.  Somatropin helps stimulate growth and development in these individuals.
* **Turner syndrome:** A genetic condition affecting females, characterized by short stature.
* **Chronic kidney disease:** In children with chronic kidney disease, somatropin can improve growth.
* **Prader-Willi syndrome:** A genetic disorder causing intellectual disability, short stature, and obesity.  Somatropin can improve growth and body composition.
* **Idiopathic short stature (ISS):**  Short stature with no identifiable underlying medical cause (use is controversial and may not be approved in all regions).
* **HIV-associated wasting:**  Somatropin may help improve body composition and lean muscle mass in adults with HIV wasting syndrome (use is limited and often debated).
* **Adult-onset GHD:**  To improve body composition, muscle strength, and bone density in adults with diagnosed GHD.


**Side Effects:**

Side effects can vary depending on the dosage and individual response.  Common side effects include:

* **Fluid retention:** Swelling in the hands, feet, and ankles.
* **Joint pain:** Arthralgia.
* **Muscle pain:** Myalgia.
* **Carpal tunnel syndrome:** Due to fluid retention.
* **Hyperglycemia:** Elevated blood sugar levels.
* **Increased insulin resistance.**
* **Gynecomastia (breast enlargement) in males.**
* **Headaches.**
* **Increased risk of developing sleep apnea.**


**Rare but serious side effects:**

* **Slipped capital femoral epiphysis (SCFE):** A condition where the head of the femur slips out of the hip socket (primarily in children).
* **Intracranial hypertension (high pressure within the skull):** Symptoms can include headaches, vision changes, and nausea.
* **Leukemia (rare, but increased risk has been linked in some studies).**


**How it Works:**

Somatropin mimics the effects of naturally occurring human growth hormone.  It binds to receptors on various cells throughout the body, stimulating growth and cell reproduction. This leads to:

* **Increased protein synthesis:** Building and repairing tissues.
* **Increased bone growth:** Leading to increased height in children.
* **Increased lipolysis:** Breaking down fats for energy.
* **Decreased fat mass:**  Leading to improved body composition.
* **Increased muscle mass:** Promoting lean body mass.
