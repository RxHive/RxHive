# Salicylic Acid
### Generic Name
Salicylic Acid

### Usage

Salicylic acid is a versatile medication primarily used topically to treat various skin conditions. Its main applications include:

* **Acne:** Salicylic acid helps unclog pores by loosening the bonds between dead skin cells, preventing acne breakouts.  It's a common ingredient in many over-the-counter acne treatments.

* **Psoriasis:** This medication helps to shed excess skin cells, reducing the characteristic scaly patches associated with psoriasis.

* **Seborrheic dermatitis (dandruff):** Salicylic acid is frequently found in anti-dandruff shampoos, where it helps to remove excess skin cells from the scalp.

* **Warts and calluses:** Its keratolytic properties make it effective in removing warts and calluses by breaking down the thickened skin.  

* **Other hyperkeratotic skin disorders:** Conditions involving thickened skin may benefit from the exfoliating action of salicylic acid.


### Dosage

Dosage varies greatly depending on the specific condition being treated, the formulation (e.g., cream, lotion, gel, shampoo, plaster), and the concentration of salicylic acid.  Always follow the instructions on the product label or as directed by a healthcare professional.  The following is for informational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice.

**Adults:**

* **Acne:** Topical application of various formulations (0.5-10%) as a thin layer to the affected area.

* **Hyperkeratotic skin disorders (e.g., calluses, corns):**  Gels (6%) may be applied and covered with a bandage at night. Shampoos (6%) are applied to wet hair, lathered, and rinsed; repeated as needed.

* **Psoriasis:** Topical application of various formulations (3-6%) as a thin layer to affected areas.

* **Wart removal:** Topical application of various formulations (5-40%) one to two times daily for up to 12 weeks.

### Side Effects

Common side effects are generally mild and localized to the application site. They may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation
* Redness
* Dryness or peeling of the skin
* Itching


Less common, but potentially more serious side effects include:

* Skin irritation (including blistering or rash)
* Allergic reactions (in rare cases)
* Salicylism (a condition caused by high levels of salicylic acid in the body, characterized by symptoms like tinnitus, dizziness, and hearing loss). This is far more likely with oral ingestion than topical application.


If you experience any unusual or persistent side effects, consult a doctor or other healthcare professional immediately.


### How it Works

Salicylic acid is a keratolytic agent, meaning it breaks down the bonds between dead skin cells. This action helps to exfoliate the skin, removing the top layer of dead cells and unclogging pores. This exfoliation helps to treat acne and reduces the scaling associated with conditions like psoriasis and seborrheic dermatitis.  It also possesses some anti-inflammatory properties.


### Precautions

* **Allergy:** Do not use salicylic acid if you are allergic to it or any of its components.

* **Pregnant or breastfeeding women:** Consult a doctor before using salicylic acid, especially during pregnancy and breastfeeding, as there is limited safety data available.

* **Children:** Use caution when applying to children, especially young children.  Always follow dosage instructions provided by a healthcare professional.

* **Other medications:**  Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all medications you are using, as interactions are possible, especially with other topical acne treatments. Concurrent use may increase skin irritation and dryness.

* **Wounds or broken skin:** Avoid applying to open wounds or broken skin.

* **Large areas of skin:** Use with caution on large areas of skin as absorption may increase.


### FAQs

* **Q: Can I use salicylic acid on my face every day?** A:  Not necessarily.  The frequency of use depends on the product and your skin's tolerance. Start with less frequent applications and increase gradually if tolerated.  Always follow the product instructions.

* **Q: How should I store salicylic acid products?** A: Store according to the label instructions, typically at room temperature away from moisture and direct sunlight.

* **Q: What should I do if I accidentally ingest salicylic acid?** A: Contact a poison control center or your doctor immediately.

* **Q: Is salicylic acid safe for long-term use?** A: Long-term safety depends on the concentration used, the area treated, and individual tolerance.  Always follow the product instructions and consult a doctor if you have concerns.
