# Sodium Metavanadate
### Generic Name
Sodium Metavanadate

### Usage
Sodium metavanadate is **not** a medicine used for treating human medical conditions.  It's an inorganic chemical compound primarily used in industrial settings and research.  It's not approved for therapeutic use in humans and shouldn't be considered a pharmaceutical drug.  Its applications involve uses as a catalyst in certain industrial processes, as a reagent in chemical synthesis, and in some specialized research applications, particularly in biological studies investigating the role of vanadium.  However, its use in these contexts requires stringent safety precautions due to its toxicity.

### Dosage
There is no established dosage for Sodium Metavanadate for human therapeutic use as it is not a medicine. Any handling of this chemical requires strict adherence to laboratory safety protocols and regulations.  Exposure should be minimized.


### Side Effects
Because sodium metavanadate is not intended for human consumption, a detailed list of side effects in a therapeutic context is not applicable. However, it's crucial to understand that it is toxic.  Exposure can lead to various health problems, including:

* **Irritations:** Skin, eye, and respiratory tract irritation.
* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.
* **Damage to organs:** Liver and kidney damage, nervous system effects.
* **Long-term exposure:**  May lead to more severe and chronic health problems.

Immediate medical attention should be sought if exposure occurs.


### How it Works
