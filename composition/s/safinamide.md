# Safinamide

**Generic Name:** Safinamide

**Usage:**  Safinamide is used to treat Parkinson's disease.  Specifically, it's used as an adjunctive therapy (meaning it's used *in addition* to levodopa) to improve the motor symptoms (like tremors, rigidity, and slow movement) of Parkinson's disease in patients experiencing "off" periods (fluctuations in the effectiveness of their medication).  It may also be used as monotherapy in some cases, but this is less common.

**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, safinamide can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them.  Common side effects include:

* **Nausea:** This is a frequently reported side effect.
* **Dizziness:** Can be a significant issue, especially in the beginning of treatment.
* **Sleepiness/Somnolence:** Increased drowsiness is possible.
* **Constipation:**  Difficulty with bowel movements.
* **Headache:**  A common, usually mild side effect.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling tired and lacking energy.
* **Insomnia:** Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.
* **Falls:** An increased risk of falls has been reported.
* **Orthostatic hypotension (low blood pressure upon standing):** This can lead to dizziness or fainting.

**Less common, but more serious side effects may include:**  Hallucinations, confusion, abnormal dreams, liver problems (rare).  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience serious side effects.

**How it Works:** Safinamide's exact mechanism of action isn't fully understood, but it's believed to work through multiple pathways:

* **MAO-B Inhibition:** It's a selective, reversible inhibitor of monoamine oxidase B (MAO-B), an enzyme that breaks down dopamine in the brain.  By inhibiting MAO-B, safinamide helps increase dopamine levels, which can improve motor symptoms in Parkinson's disease.
* **Glutamate Modulation:**  Research suggests it may also modulate glutamate release, a neurotransmitter involved in motor control. This action might contribute to its effectiveness in reducing "off" periods.
