# Sodium Bicarbonate + Glycerin

**Generic Name:** Sodium Bicarbonate + Glycerin (This is not an official generic name as it's a combination of two ingredients.  Individual components have their own generic names: Sodium Bicarbonate and Glycerol)

* **Skin soothing and moisturizing:** Glycerin's humectant properties attract and retain moisture, while sodium bicarbonate can help neutralize acidity and potentially soothe irritated skin. This combination might be found in lotions, creams, or pastes for conditions like mild eczema or dry skin.
* **Oral hygiene:** Some toothpastes or mouthwashes may contain both ingredients. Sodium bicarbonate is a mild abrasive and can help whiten teeth and neutralize acids in the mouth. Glycerin adds a smooth texture and moisturizing effect.
* **Other uses (less common):**  Some less common applications may exist depending on the specific product, such as in certain laxatives (though glycerin is the primary active ingredient in this case).

**How it Works:**

* **Sodium Bicarbonate:** Acts as a weak base, neutralizing acids. This can help soothe skin irritation caused by acidic substances. Its mild abrasive properties can also assist in removing surface debris.
* **Glycerin (Glycerol):**  A humectant, meaning it draws moisture from the air and binds it to the skin, leading to increased hydration and improved skin texture. It also acts as a lubricant and emollient, softening and protecting the skin.

The combined effect of these two ingredients offers a soothing, moisturizing, and potentially mildly cleansing action on the skin.
