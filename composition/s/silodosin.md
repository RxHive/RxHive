# Silodosin

**Generic Name:** Silodosin

**Usage:** Silodosin is used to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), also known as an enlarged prostate.  It helps to relax the muscles in the prostate and bladder neck, making urination easier.  It is specifically a selective alpha1-adrenergic receptor blocker.

**Side Effects:**  The most common side effects of silodosin include:

* **Retrograde ejaculation:** This means that semen enters the bladder instead of being expelled through the penis during orgasm.  This is usually harmless but can affect fertility.
* **Dizziness:** This can be particularly pronounced when standing up suddenly (orthostatic hypotension).
* **Headache:** A common side effect that usually resolves on its own.
* **Nasal congestion:** Stuffiness or a runny nose.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling tired or weak.
* **Somnolence (sleepiness):** Feeling unusually drowsy.
* **Hypotension (low blood pressure):**  Can lead to dizziness or fainting.
* **Blurred vision:**  Temporary impairment of vision.
* **Diarrhea:** Loose or watery stools.


**How it Works:** Silodosin is a selective alpha1-adrenergic receptor blocker.  In the prostate and bladder neck, alpha1-adrenergic receptors cause the muscles to contract. By blocking these receptors, silodosin relaxes these muscles, reducing the pressure on the urethra and improving urine flow.  It is more selective for alpha1A-adrenergic receptors found in the prostate than other alpha1-adrenergic receptor subtypes found elsewhere in the body, thus minimizing side effects in other areas.
