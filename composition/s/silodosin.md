# Silodosin
### Generic Name
Silodosin

### Usage
Silodosin's primary use is in the symptomatic treatment of benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), also known as enlarged prostate.  BPH is a common condition in older men, causing urinary symptoms like frequent urination, weak urine stream, and difficulty starting urination.  While not officially approved for this use, silodosin has also shown potential in helping expel distal ureteral calculi (kidney stones).  This off-label use requires careful medical supervision.

### Dosage
**Adult Dosage:** For BPH, the usual dose is 8 mg orally once daily, taken with food.  For off-label use in distal ureteral calculi expulsion, the dose is also 8 mg once daily until the stone is passed (typically within 3-18 days).  Dosage adjustments are necessary for individuals with kidney problems:

* **Creatinine clearance (CrCl) > 50 mL/min:** No adjustment needed.
* **CrCl 30-50 mL/min:** Reduce the dose to 4 mg once daily.
* **CrCl < 30 mL/min:** Silodosin is contraindicated.

For individuals with mild to moderate liver impairment, no dosage adjustment is typically required. However, silodosin is contraindicated in those with severe liver impairment.

**Pediatric Dosage:**  The safety and efficacy of silodosin in children have not been established. It is not recommended for use in children.

**Administration:** Silodosin is available in capsule form. The capsules can be opened, and the contents sprinkled onto a spoonful of cool applesauce (not hot).  The applesauce should be swallowed immediately and followed with 8 ounces of cool water.  Do not chew the contents. Dividing the capsule contents is not recommended.

### Side Effects
**Common Side Effects (>10%):**

* Retrograde ejaculation (semen flows backward into the bladder instead of out through the penis)

**Less Common Side Effects (1-10%):**

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Insomnia
* Orthostatic hypotension (a sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing)
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Increased prostate-specific antigen (PSA) levels
* Weakness
* Nasal congestion
* Rhinorrhea (runny nose)
* Sinusitis


**Rare but Serious Side Effects (<1%, Post-marketing, and/or Case Reports):**

* Hepatic insufficiency (liver failure)
* Hypersensitivity reactions (allergic reactions)
* Increased liver enzyme levels
* Intraoperative floppy iris syndrome (a complication during eye surgery)
* Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
* Pharyngeal edema (swelling of the throat)
* Priapism (prolonged, painful erection)
* Pruritus (itching)
* Purpura (purple spots on the skin)
* Skin rash (including toxic epidermal necrolysis)
* Swollen tongue
* Syncope (fainting)
* Urticaria (hives)

If any adverse effects occur, contact a healthcare professional immediately.

### How it Works
Silodosin is a selective alpha-1A adrenoceptor antagonist.  Alpha-1A receptors are found in the prostate and bladder.  By blocking these receptors, silodosin relaxes the smooth muscle in the prostate and bladder neck, improving urine flow and easing the symptoms of BPH.


### Precautions
**Contraindications:**

* Allergy to silodosin or any ingredient in the formulation.
* Concurrent use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., clarithromycin, itraconazole, ketoconazole, ritonavir).
* Severe renal impairment (CrCl < 30 mL/min).
* Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class C).

**Warnings:**

* Silodosin may increase the risk of intraoperative floppy iris syndrome during eye surgery.
* Silodosin can cause orthostatic hypotension and syncope; patients should be advised to change positions slowly.
* Use caution in patients with mild to moderate hepatic or renal impairment and in elderly patients.


**Drug Interactions:** Silodosin can interact with many other medications.  Discuss all medications, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, with your doctor or pharmacist before taking silodosin.  Important interactions involve medications affecting CYP3A4 enzymes (involved in drug metabolism) and other drugs that affect blood pressure.

**Q: How should I store silodosin?**
A: Store silodosin at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.


**Q: Can I stop taking silodosin abruptly?**
A:  No, it's generally recommended to discontinue silodosin gradually as directed by your doctor to minimize potential withdrawal effects.


**Q: Will silodosin affect my ability to drive or operate machinery?**
A: Silodosin may cause dizziness or drowsiness. Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how it affects you.


**Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?**
A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not take two doses at once.
