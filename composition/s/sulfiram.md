# Sulfiram

**Generic Name:** Disulfiram

**Usage:** Disulfiram is used as a deterrent for alcohol use in individuals with alcohol use disorder. It works by inhibiting the enzyme aldehyde dehydrogenase, leading to an accumulation of acetaldehyde in the body when alcohol is consumed. This accumulation causes unpleasant side effects, discouraging further alcohol intake.  It is crucial to note that disulfiram is only effective when used in conjunction with a comprehensive treatment plan that includes counseling and support.  It is *not* a standalone cure for alcoholism.

**Side Effects:** Side effects of disulfiram can range from mild to severe. Common side effects include:

* **Metallic taste in the mouth:** This is a very frequent side effect.
* **Nausea and vomiting:** These can be significant, especially after alcohol consumption.
* **Headache:**  A common complaint.
* **Fatigue:**  Feeling tired and lacking energy.
* **Drowsiness:**  Increased sleepiness.
* **Rash:** Skin reactions can occur.


Severe side effects, though less common, can include:

* **Severe allergic reactions:**  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience symptoms like difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat.
* **Hepatotoxicity (liver damage):** This is a serious side effect that requires monitoring.
* **Peripheral neuropathy:** Nerve damage in the extremities.
* **Psychotic reactions:**  Rare but potentially serious mental health issues.
* **Cardiovascular effects:**  In rare instances, disulfiram can affect the heart.


**How it Works:**  Disulfiram inhibits the enzyme aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH), which is crucial in the metabolism of alcohol. When a person taking disulfiram consumes alcohol, the acetaldehyde (a toxic byproduct of alcohol metabolism) builds up in the body. This accumulation causes unpleasant and potentially dangerous symptoms such as flushing, nausea, vomiting, headache, and palpitations. This unpleasant reaction acts as a deterrent to alcohol consumption.
