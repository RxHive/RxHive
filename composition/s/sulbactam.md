# Sulbactam

**Generic Name:** Sulbactam

**Usage:** Sulbactam is a beta-lactam antibiotic used in combination with ampicillin (e.g., Unasyn) or amoxicillin (e.g., Augmentin) to treat bacterial infections.  It's typically used for infections that are resistant to ampicillin or amoxicillin alone.  These infections might include:

* Infections of the respiratory tract (e.g., pneumonia, bronchitis)
* Infections of the urinary tract
* Skin and soft tissue infections
* Intra-abdominal infections
* Gynecological infections (e.g., pelvic inflammatory disease)

**How it Works:** Sulbactam is a beta-lactamase inhibitor.  Many bacteria produce beta-lactamases, enzymes that break down beta-lactam antibiotics (like ampicillin and amoxicillin) rendering them ineffective. Sulbactam inhibits these beta-lactamases, protecting the ampicillin or amoxicillin from degradation, allowing them to effectively kill the bacteria.  Essentially, it acts as a shield for the other antibiotic.
