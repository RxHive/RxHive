# Sildenafil

**Generic Name:** Sildenafil

**Usage:** Sildenafil is used to treat erectile dysfunction (ED) in men and pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) in both men and women.  For ED, it is taken as needed, usually about 30-60 minutes before sexual activity.  For PAH, it is taken regularly as prescribed by a doctor.  It's crucial to follow the dosage instructions provided by your healthcare professional.  The dosage will vary depending on the condition being treated and the individual's health.

**Side Effects:**  The most common side effects of sildenafil include headache, flushing (redness of the face, neck, or chest), nasal congestion, upset stomach, dizziness, and visual disturbances (such as blurred vision, changes in color vision, or increased sensitivity to light).  More serious, though less common, side effects can include:

* **Sudden decrease or loss of vision:**  Stop taking sildenafil and seek immediate medical attention if you experience this.
* **Sudden decrease or loss of hearing:** Stop taking sildenafil and seek immediate medical attention if you experience this.
* **Prolonged erection (priapism):** This is a painful condition and requires immediate medical attention.
* **Heart attack or stroke:**  Sildenafil can increase the risk of these events, especially in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions.
* **Low blood pressure:** This can be particularly dangerous in individuals taking other medications that lower blood pressure.


**How it Works:**  For erectile dysfunction, sildenafil works by inhibiting an enzyme called phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE5).  This enzyme breaks down cyclic GMP, a molecule that plays a crucial role in achieving and maintaining an erection. By inhibiting PDE5, sildenafil allows cyclic GMP levels to remain elevated, leading to increased blood flow to the penis and facilitating an erection in response to sexual stimulation.

For pulmonary arterial hypertension, sildenafil works by relaxing blood vessels in the lungs, reducing blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries and improving blood flow to the lungs.
