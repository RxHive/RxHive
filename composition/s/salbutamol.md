# Salbutamol

**Generic Name:** Salbutamol

**Usage:**

Salbutamol is a short-acting beta2-agonist (SABA) bronchodilator primarily used to treat and prevent bronchospasm in people with reversible airway obstruction, such as:

* **Asthma:**  It relieves symptoms like wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing.  It's used for both acute attacks (rescue inhaler) and preventing exercise-induced bronchospasm (prophylactically).
* **Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD):**  It helps relieve symptoms of COPD, such as shortness of breath.
* **Other conditions:**  It may be used to treat other conditions involving bronchospasm, as directed by a physician.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* Tremor (shaking)
* Headache
* Muscle cramps
* Nervousness
* Dizziness
* Increased heart rate (tachycardia)
* Insomnia

Less common, but more serious side effects can occur and require immediate medical attention:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis):  Symptoms include swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat; difficulty breathing; hives; rapid heartbeat; and dizziness.
* Paradoxical bronchospasm:  Worsening of wheezing or shortness of breath after using the medication.
* Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmias)
* High blood pressure


**How it Works:**

Salbutamol works by relaxing the muscles around the airways in the lungs.  It does this by binding to beta2-adrenergic receptors in the bronchial smooth muscle. This binding triggers a series of intracellular events that ultimately lead to smooth muscle relaxation, resulting in widening of the airways and improved airflow.
