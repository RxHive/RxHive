# Solifenacin

**Generic Name:** Solifenacin succinate

**Usage:** Solifenacin is used to treat overactive bladder (OAB).  OAB is a condition characterized by urgency, frequency, and urge incontinence (leakage of urine).  It is typically prescribed for adults.  The dosage and frequency of administration will vary depending on individual needs and response.


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, solifenacin can cause side effects.  Some common side effects include:

* **Dry mouth:** This is a very common side effect.
* **Constipation:**  Difficulty passing stools.
* **Dry eyes:**  May lead to discomfort or blurry vision.
* **Dizziness:** Feeling lightheaded or unsteady.
* **Headache:**  A common side effect.
* **Blurred vision:**  Impaired vision.
* **Nasal congestion:**  Stuffy nose.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling tired or weak.
* **Increased risk of urinary retention:** Difficulty emptying the bladder. This is particularly important for patients with pre-existing bladder or urinary conditions.


**Less common but more serious side effects include:**

* **Allergic reactions:** Rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience these.
* **Severe constipation:**  This can require medical intervention.
* **Glaucoma:**  Increased pressure in the eye.
* **Heat stroke:** Especially in hot or humid conditions.
* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.

This list is not exhaustive, and individuals may experience other side effects. It's crucial to consult a doctor or pharmacist if you experience any concerning side effects.


**How it Works:** Solifenacin is an antimuscarinic drug.  It works by blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates the bladder muscle. By reducing the activity of the bladder muscle, solifenacin helps to reduce the frequency of bladder contractions and thus decreases the symptoms of overactive bladder.
