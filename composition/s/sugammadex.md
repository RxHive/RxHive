# Sugammadex

**Generic Name:** Sugammadex

**Usage:** Sugammadex is used to reverse the neuromuscular blockade induced by rocuronium and vecuronium, two muscle relaxants commonly used during surgery and other medical procedures requiring paralysis.  It helps patients regain the ability to breathe on their own more quickly after the use of these muscle relaxants.  It is administered intravenously (IV).

* **Vomiting:** Throwing up.
* **Headache:** Pain in the head.
* **Hypotension (low blood pressure):**  A drop in blood pressure.
* **Pain at the injection site:** Discomfort where the injection was given.
* **Allergic reactions:** These can range from mild to severe and include rash, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.  Rare but serious allergic reactions can occur.


Less common, but more serious side effects can also occur and require immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:** Sugammadex works by selectively binding to rocuronium and vecuronium, effectively removing them from the neuromuscular junction.  This allows the neuromuscular transmission to recover and enables the patient to regain muscle function, including breathing.  It's a specific antagonist, meaning it targets and neutralizes the muscle relaxant without affecting other muscle functions.
