# Sulphacetamide
### Generic Name
Sulfacetamide

### Usage

Sulfacetamide is an antibiotic belonging to the sulfonamide class.  Its primary use is in the topical treatment of bacterial infections of the eye and surrounding structures. This includes conditions like bacterial conjunctivitis (pink eye), blepharitis (inflammation of the eyelids), and other superficial ocular infections.  While less common, it might also be used in treating certain skin infections.  It's crucial to understand that sulfacetamide is *not* effective against viral or fungal infections.  A proper diagnosis from a healthcare professional is essential before using this medication.


### Dosage

Dosage for sulfacetamide varies greatly depending on the specific formulation (ointment, solution, etc.) and the condition being treated.  Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist.  There are no established pediatric or adult IV formulations of sulfacetamide; it's administered topically only.

* **Ophthalmic Solutions (Eye Drops):**  Typically, one to two drops are instilled into the affected eye(s) every 2 to 4 hours, or as directed by a physician.  The frequency may be adjusted based on the severity of the infection and the patient's response.

* **Ophthalmic Ointments:** A small ribbon of ointment is applied to the affected eye(s) usually two to four times daily.  Again, the doctor's instructions should be followed precisely.

* **Topical Creams/Lotions (for skin infections):**  Dosage will vary depending on the concentration and the area to be treated.  Consult your physician or pharmacist for specific instructions.


**Important Note:** Never exceed the prescribed dosage.  Improper use may lead to reduced effectiveness or increased risk of side effects.


### Side Effects

Common side effects of sulfacetamide, especially with ophthalmic use, may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation upon application
* Temporary blurry vision
* Mild eye irritation
* Allergic reactions (itching, redness, swelling)

Less common, but more serious side effects, although rare, can include:

* Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) - characterized by difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue.
* Stevens-Johnson syndrome (a rare but serious skin reaction)
* Toxic epidermal necrolysis (another severe skin reaction)

If any serious side effects occur, seek immediate medical attention.


### How it Works

Sulfacetamide works by interfering with the production of folic acid, a vital nutrient for bacterial growth. By inhibiting folic acid synthesis, sulfacetamide prevents bacteria from replicating and ultimately helps to clear the infection.  This mechanism of action is specific to bacteria; it doesn't affect human cells in the same way.


### Precautions

* **Allergies:**  Individuals with a known allergy to sulfonamides should not use sulfacetamide.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  While generally considered safe for topical use during pregnancy and breastfeeding, it's always best to consult a doctor before using it.
* **Interactions with other medications:**  Sulfacetamide may interact with certain medications. Inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are currently taking.
* **Contact lenses:**  Remove contact lenses before applying sulfacetamide and wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting them.
* **Driving and operating machinery:**  Temporary blurry vision may occur after application; avoid driving or operating machinery until vision clears.

### FAQs

* **Q: Can I use sulfacetamide for ear infections?** A: No, sulfacetamide is specifically formulated for eye and skin infections, not ear infections.

* **Q: How should I store sulfacetamide?** A: Store it as directed on the label, usually at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: Can I use sulfacetamide if I have a viral conjunctivitis?** A: No. Sulfacetamide is ineffective against viral infections.  Your doctor needs to determine the cause of your conjunctivitis before recommending treatment.

* **Q: Is sulfacetamide safe for children?** A: It can be safe for children under appropriate medical supervision.  A doctor should determine the correct dosage and monitor the child for any adverse effects.  Always follow the doctor's instructions carefully.
