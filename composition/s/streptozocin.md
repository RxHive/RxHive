# Streptozocin
### Generic Name
Streptozocin

### Usage
Streptozocin is primarily used to treat metastatic islet-cell carcinoma of the pancreas, specifically in cases where the disease is symptomatic or progressing.  While its main application is in pancreatic cancer, it's also sometimes used off-label for other conditions such as metastatic adrenocortical carcinoma and gastrointestinal neuroendocrine tumors.  These off-label uses often involve combination therapies with other drugs and should only be considered under the guidance of a qualified oncologist.


### Dosage
Streptozocin is administered intravenously (IV).  Dosage varies considerably depending on the specific treatment schedule and the patient's body surface area (BSA), calculated using their height and weight.  There is no single standard regimen.

**For metastatic pancreatic islet-cell carcinoma:** Several schedules exist, including:

* **Daily Schedule:** 500 mg/m²/day for 5 consecutive days every 6 weeks.
* **Weekly Schedule:** 1000 mg/m² once weekly, with potential escalation to 1500 mg/m² if needed.
* **Other Schedules (Off-label):**  Various combinations and dosages are used in conjunction with other chemotherapeutic agents.  These are highly individualized and determined by the treating physician based on the patient's response and tolerance.

**For other cancers (off-label):**  Dosages are even more variable and depend heavily on the specific cancer type, other medications used, and the patient's overall health.


**Pediatric Dosage:** Streptozocin is not recommended for use in children.

**Dosage Adjustments:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary based on factors such as renal or hepatic impairment, or the development of side effects.  For renal impairment, there's no universally accepted adjustment protocol, but some guidelines suggest dose reduction based on creatinine clearance.  Hepatic impairment also warrants careful monitoring and potential dose adjustments.  Obesity may influence dosage calculations; however, guidance for this is still evolving.

### Side Effects
Streptozocin can cause various side effects, some common and others less frequent but potentially more serious.  It's crucial to report any adverse effects to a healthcare professional immediately.

**Common Side Effects:**

* Nausea and vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Mild bone marrow suppression (anemia)
* Increased blood glucose levels


**Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**

* Severe bone marrow suppression (leukopenia, thrombocytopenia)
* Renal toxicity (azotemia, anuria, proteinuria, renal tubular acidosis)
* Hepatotoxicity (elevated liver enzymes, liver dysfunction)
* Neurologic effects (confusion, lethargy, depression)
* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling)


### How it Works
Streptozocin is an alkylating agent, meaning it damages DNA within cancer cells.  This damage disrupts their ability to replicate and grow, ultimately leading to cell death.  The drug's mechanism involves alkylation and cross-linking of DNA strands, potentially also modifying proteins and inhibiting enzymes crucial for DNA synthesis.


### Precautions
Several precautions should be observed when using Streptozocin:

* **Renal Impairment:**  Patients with pre-existing kidney problems are at increased risk of renal toxicity. Careful monitoring of kidney function is essential.
* **Hepatic Impairment:**  Patients with liver disease may experience heightened hepatotoxicity. Close monitoring of liver function is vital.
* **Bone Marrow Suppression:**  Streptozocin can suppress bone marrow function, increasing susceptibility to infections. Regular blood tests to monitor blood counts are necessary.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Streptozocin is categorized as pregnancy category D, meaning it poses a risk to the fetus.  Breastfeeding is not recommended while taking this medication.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Streptozocin can interact with other medications, potentially increasing the risk of side effects or diminishing the effectiveness of other drugs.  It's crucial to disclose all medications to the prescribing physician.
* **Other Precautions:**  Patients should be closely monitored for signs of renal and hepatic toxicity, neurologic effects, and allergic reactions.  Supportive care measures may be needed to manage side effects.
