# S-Amlodipine + Bisoprolol

**Generic Name:** Amlodipine/Bisoprolol

**Usage:**

Amlodipine/Bisoprolol is a combination medication used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It combines a calcium channel blocker (amlodipine) and a beta-blocker (bisoprolol).  This combination is often prescribed when one medication alone isn't sufficient to control blood pressure.  It's primarily used for patients who haven't achieved adequate blood pressure control with either amlodipine or bisoprolol individually.


**Side Effects:**

Side effects can vary depending on the individual and the dosage.  Common side effects include:

* **Amlodipine related:** Headache, dizziness, flushing, swelling in ankles or feet (peripheral edema), fatigue, nausea.  Rarely, more serious side effects like liver problems or allergic reactions can occur.
* **Bisoprolol related:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, fatigue, nausea, shortness of breath, cold hands and feet, sleep disturbances.  Less common, but more serious side effects include bradycardia (slow heart rate), heart block, worsening of heart failure, and bronchospasm (in patients with asthma or COPD).

It's crucial to note that this is not an exhaustive list.  Always consult a doctor or pharmacist for a complete list of potential side effects and to report any unusual symptoms.


**How it Works:**

* **Amlodipine:** Amlodipine relaxes blood vessels, making it easier for the heart to pump blood. This lowers blood pressure.
* **Bisoprolol:** Bisoprolol slows down the heart rate and reduces the force of the heart's contractions. This also helps to lower blood pressure.  It also helps to protect the heart.

By working together, amlodipine and bisoprolol provide a more comprehensive approach to blood pressure control than either drug alone.
