# Sodium Tauroglycocholate
### Generic Name
Sodium Tauroglycocholate

### Usage
Sodium tauroglycocholate is a digestive enzyme primarily used in combination with pancreatin.  This combination is employed to treat a range of digestive issues stemming from insufficient pancreatic enzyme production or impaired bile acid function.  Specific conditions treated include:

* **Indigestion:**  Characterized by discomfort or pain in the upper abdomen after eating.
* **Flatulence:** Excessive gas build-up in the digestive tract, leading to bloating and discomfort.
* **Anorexia:** Loss of appetite, often related to digestive problems.
* **Dyspepsia:**  A general term for indigestion-related symptoms such as heartburn, nausea, bloating, and abdominal pain.
* **Hepatic and Pancreatic Insufficiency:**  Conditions where the liver or pancreas don't function adequately, leading to poor digestion.
* **Cystic Fibrosis of the Pancreas:** A genetic disorder affecting the pancreas' ability to produce digestive enzymes.
* **Post-operative Digestive Upsets:** Digestive problems occurring after surgery.
* **Convalescence:** The period of recovery after an illness or surgery, where digestive support may be needed.

It's crucial to understand that sodium tauroglycocholate is typically part of a combination therapy and not used alone to treat these conditions.

### Side Effects
While generally well-tolerated, sodium tauroglycocholate (in combination products) can cause some side effects. Common side effects include:

* Nausea
* Abdominal cramps
* Diarrhea
* Constipation

Less common, but more serious side effects may include allergic reactions (such as rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing).  If any serious or unexpected side effects occur, seek immediate medical attention.


### How it Works
Sodium tauroglycocholate functions as a bile salt. Bile salts, including tauroglycocholate, are crucial for fat digestion.  They emulsify fats, breaking them down into smaller droplets that are more easily accessible to pancreatic lipases (enzymes that digest fats). This improved fat digestion leads to better nutrient absorption and reduced symptoms associated with insufficient pancreatic enzyme activity or bile acid deficiency.  Furthermore, sodium tauroglycocholate assists in the breakdown of proteins and carbohydrates, though its primary role is in fat digestion.
