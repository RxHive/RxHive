# Sodium aminosalicylate

**Generic Name:** Sodium aminosalicylate (PAS)

**Usage:**

Sodium aminosalicylate is an anti-infective medication primarily used in the treatment of tuberculosis (TB), particularly in combination with other anti-tuberculosis drugs.  It's less commonly used now due to the availability of more effective and better-tolerated medications.  It may still be considered in certain situations, such as:

* **Treatment of drug-resistant TB:** When other first-line TB drugs are ineffective.
* **Combination therapy:** Used alongside other anti-tuberculosis drugs to improve treatment efficacy and reduce the risk of drug resistance.
* **Treatment of Crohn's disease:** Although less frequent, it may be used in specific cases of Crohn's disease, often in conjunction with other therapies.  Its use in Crohn's is diminishing due to newer treatments.


**Side Effects:**

Sodium aminosalicylate can cause a range of side effects, varying in severity from mild to serious.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, loss of appetite. These are often dose-related and can be mitigated by taking the medication with food.
* **Hypersensitivity reactions:** Rashes, itching, hives, fever.  More serious allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis, are rare but possible.
* **Hematologic effects:**  Rare instances of anemia, leukopenia (low white blood cell count), and thrombocytopenia (low platelet count) have been reported.
* **Hepatotoxicity:** Liver damage is a rare but serious potential side effect, requiring close monitoring of liver function tests.
* **Renal effects:** Crystaluria (crystals in the urine) and kidney stones are possible, especially with high doses or inadequate hydration.
* **Neurological effects:** Headaches, dizziness, and peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage) have been reported.

The exact mechanism of action of sodium aminosalicylate in treating tuberculosis is not fully understood.  It's believed to exert its effects by:

* **Inhibition of bacterial growth:** It interferes with the synthesis of bacterial components essential for the growth and survival of *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*.
* **Anti-inflammatory effects:**  In the context of Crohn's disease, it may help reduce inflammation in the gut.
