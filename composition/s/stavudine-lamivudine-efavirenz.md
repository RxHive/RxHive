# Stavudine + Lamivudine + Efavirenz

**Generic Name:** Stavudine + Lamivudine + Efavirenz (This combination is not typically available as a single fixed-dose combination pill.  Each component is usually administered separately or in other combinations.)

**Usage:**  This combination of drugs is used to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection.  It's a highly active antiretroviral therapy (HAART) regimen, meaning it combines multiple drugs to suppress viral replication.  **Crucially, it's important to note that this specific triple combination is not a standard or commercially available formulation.  Prescribing will be based on individual patient needs, viral load, and resistance profiles.  Doctors will often prescribe these three drugs individually or in different combination pills.**

**Side Effects:** Side effects vary depending on the individual drug and can be numerous.  Here's a breakdown based on the components:

* **Stavudine (d4T):**  Peripheral neuropathy (pain, numbness, tingling in hands and feet), pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas), lactic acidosis (a buildup of lactic acid in the blood, a serious condition), anemia, and myopathy (muscle weakness).
* **Lamivudine (3TC):**  Headache, nausea, diarrhea, abdominal pain, fatigue, and rash.  Less common but serious side effects include pancreatitis and lactic acidosis (though less frequently than with stavudine).
* **Efavirenz (EFV):**  Dizziness, drowsiness, nightmares, vivid dreams, rash, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and depression.  Severe skin reactions can occur. Central nervous system effects are common.


**How it Works:**

* **Stavudine (d4T):** A nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). It works by incorporating into the DNA chain of the HIV virus, thereby halting its replication.
* **Lamivudine (3TC):** Also an NRTI.  It works similarly to stavudine, inhibiting reverse transcriptase and preventing the virus from replicating.
* **Efavirenz (EFV):** A non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI). It works by binding to a different site on the reverse transcriptase enzyme than NRTIs, further inhibiting viral replication.
