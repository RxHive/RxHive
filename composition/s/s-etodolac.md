# S-Etodolac

**Generic Name:** Etodolac

**Usage:**  Etodolac is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve the symptoms of osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and other forms of inflammatory or painful conditions.  It's typically prescribed for the short-term management of acute pain as well.  It's important to note that etodolac does *not* cure these conditions; it only manages the symptoms.

**Side Effects:** Like all NSAIDs, etodolac can cause a range of side effects, some mild and some serious.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:**  Nausea, vomiting, heartburn, indigestion, constipation, diarrhea, abdominal pain, stomach ulcers (in rare cases, potentially leading to bleeding or perforation).
* **Central Nervous System:** Dizziness, headache, drowsiness, insomnia.
* **Cardiovascular:** Fluid retention, high blood pressure (hypertension), increased risk of heart attack and stroke (though the risk is generally considered lower than with some other NSAIDs).
* **Skin:** Rash, itching.
* **Other:**  Kidney problems (especially in patients with pre-existing kidney disease), liver problems (rare), ringing in the ears (tinnitus).


**How it Works:** Etodolac works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemicals in the body that contribute to inflammation, pain, and fever.  By reducing prostaglandin production, etodolac helps to alleviate the symptoms of inflammatory conditions.

Specifically, concurrent use with other NSAIDs, blood thinners (anticoagulants), or certain heart medications can increase risks.

* **What should I do if I experience side effects?**  Contact your doctor immediately if you experience severe or concerning side effects, such as severe stomach pain, black or bloody stools, difficulty breathing, or signs of allergic reaction.
