# Sodium Fusidate

**Generic Name:** Sodium Fusidate

**Usage:** Sodium fusidate is an antibiotic used to treat bacterial skin infections, such as impetigo, infected eczema, and other superficial wounds.  It's usually applied topically (directly to the skin) as a cream, ointment, or solution.  Oral formulations are less common and may be used in specific situations where topical treatment isn't sufficient, often under the guidance of a specialist.

**Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Skin irritation, redness, itching, burning at the application site.  These are usually mild and temporary.
* **Uncommon:**  Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling), increased skin sensitivity to sunlight (photosensitivity).
* **Rare:**  More serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), systemic side effects (affecting the whole body) if absorbed in significant amounts (more likely with oral formulations).

**Important Note:**  If you experience any unusual or severe side effects, stop using the medication and seek medical attention immediately.


**How it Works:**

Sodium fusidate is a bacteriostatic antibiotic, meaning it inhibits the growth of bacteria rather than killing them directly. It works by interfering with the bacteria's protein synthesis, preventing them from producing essential proteins necessary for their survival and reproduction.  This primarily targets gram-positive bacteria, which are common culprits in skin infections.
