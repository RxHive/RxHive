# Sorafenib

**Generic Name:** Sorafenib

**Usage:**

Sorafenib is a targeted therapy used to treat several types of cancer.  Its specific uses include:

* **Advanced renal cell carcinoma (kidney cancer):**  Used when the cancer has spread to other parts of the body or cannot be surgically removed.
* **Advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (liver cancer):** Used in patients who are not candidates for surgery, liver transplant, or other systemic therapies.
* **Differentiated thyroid cancer (DTC) that has spread or cannot be surgically removed:**  Used in patients who have already received radioactive iodine treatment.


**Side Effects:**

Sorafenib can cause a wide range of side effects, some common and some serious.  It's crucial to discuss any concerns with your doctor. Common side effects may include:

* **Hand-foot skin reaction (HFSR):** Painful, red, and swollen palms and soles of the feet.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling unusually tired.
* **Diarrhea:** Loose, watery stools.
* **High blood pressure (hypertension):** Elevated blood pressure readings.
* **Nausea and vomiting:** Feeling sick to the stomach and throwing up.
* **Rash:** Skin irritation or eruptions.
* **Hair thinning (alopecia):** Hair loss.
* **Weight loss:** Unintentional decrease in body weight.
* **Increased risk of bleeding or bruising:** Due to its effects on blood vessels.


Serious side effects, though less common, can include:

* **Heart failure:** Difficulty breathing and swelling in the legs and ankles.
* **Bleeding:** Excessive bleeding from any site.
* **Blood clots:** Formation of blood clots in the veins or arteries.
* **Kidney problems:** Reduced kidney function.
* **Liver problems:** Elevated liver enzymes.
* **Severe skin reactions:** Stevens-Johnson syndrome or toxic epidermal necrolysis (rare but potentially life-threatening).


This is not an exhaustive list of all possible side effects.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist for complete information.


**How it Works:**

Sorafenib is a multi-kinase inhibitor.  This means it blocks the activity of several different types of kinases, enzymes involved in cell growth and division. Specifically, it inhibits Raf kinases (specifically BRAF and CRAF) and vascular endothelial growth factor receptor (VEGFR) and platelet-derived growth factor receptor (PDGFR)  kinases.  By blocking these kinases, sorafenib prevents cancer cells from growing and spreading, and it also reduces the formation of new blood vessels that supply tumors with nutrients and oxygen (angiogenesis).

* **What are the long-term effects of sorafenib?** Long-term effects vary widely among individuals.  Your doctor can discuss potential long-term risks and benefits with you.
