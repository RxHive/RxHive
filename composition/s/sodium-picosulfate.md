# Sodium Picosulfate
### Generic Name
Sodium Picosulfate

### Usage
Sodium picosulfate is primarily used to treat constipation.  It's a stimulant laxative, meaning it works by stimulating the muscles in the intestines to increase bowel movements.  This makes it effective in relieving infrequent or difficult bowel movements.  It's not typically used for long-term management of constipation; rather, it's intended for occasional relief.

### Dosage

**Adults:** For constipation, the usual adult dose is 5mg orally, taken once daily in the evening. This dose can be increased to 10mg if needed, but 10mg is the maximum daily dose.

**Children:** Dosage for children varies significantly by age:

* **Children under 4 years old:** The recommended dose is 0.25 mg per kilogram of body weight per day.  Always consult a doctor for precise dosage calculation.
* **Children aged 4-10 years old:** The recommended dose is 2.5-5mg once daily in the evening.
* **Children over 10 years and adolescents:** The dose is the same as for adults (5-10mg maximum).

**Dosage Adjustments:** The manufacturer's labeling doesn't provide specific dosage adjustments for hepatic (liver) or renal (kidney) impairment.  Use is contraindicated in cases of acute hepatic failure, and caution is advised in individuals with renal impairment. Always consult a healthcare professional before administering sodium picosulfate to patients with impaired liver or kidney function.


### Side Effects

Common side effects (occurring in more than 10% of users) include diarrhea.

Side effects occurring in 1-10% of users may include:

* Abdominal cramps
* Abdominal distress
* Abdominal pain


Less common side effects (frequency not precisely defined) include:

* Headache
* Vertigo
* Flatulence
* Allergic skin rash
* Angioedema
* Dizziness
* Hypersensitivity reaction
* Nausea
* Pruritus (itching)
* Skin rash
* Syncope (fainting)
* Vomiting


If you experience any adverse effects, particularly serious ones,  contact your doctor or other healthcare provider immediately.


### How it Works

Sodium picosulfate is a stimulant laxative that works locally in the colon.  After ingestion, bacteria in the colon break down sodium picosulfate into its active metabolite. This metabolite stimulates the muscles in the large intestine and rectum, increasing their contractions and promoting bowel movements.  It also helps draw water and electrolytes into the bowel, softening the stool and making it easier to pass.

### Precautions

**Contraindications:** Sodium picosulfate should not be used by individuals with:

* Hypersensitivity (allergy) to sodium picosulfate or any of its components.
* Ileus (intestinal blockage) or intestinal obstruction.
* Acute abdominal conditions characterized by pain, fever, nausea, and vomiting.
* Acute inflammatory bowel diseases.
* Severe dehydration.


**Warnings:**

* Prolonged or excessive use may lead to fluid and electrolyte imbalances, including hypokalemia (low potassium levels).
* Use with caution in patients with a history of inflammatory bowel disease.


**Drug Interactions:**

* Antibiotics may decrease the effectiveness of sodium picosulfate.
* Sodium picosulfate can increase the effects of polyethylene glycol-electrolyte solutions.


**Pregnancy and Lactation:** Sodium picosulfate is categorized as pregnancy category B. This means that studies in animals have not shown evidence of fetal harm, but there are no adequate and well-controlled studies in pregnant women.  Sodium picosulfate and its active metabolites are not found in breast milk. However, always consult a doctor before using any medication during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.


### FAQs

**Q: How long does it take for sodium picosulfate to work?**
A: The onset of action typically ranges from 6 to 12 hours after taking the medication.


**Q: Can I take sodium picosulfate every day?**
A: No. Sodium picosulfate is not intended for daily use.  Long-term use can lead to dependence and electrolyte imbalances. It should only be used for occasional relief of constipation.


**Q: How should I store sodium picosulfate?**
A: Store sodium picosulfate as directed on the product label, typically at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.


**Q: Is sodium picosulfate safe for everyone?**
A:  No.  See the precautions section above for contraindications and warnings.  Always consult your healthcare provider before using sodium picosulfate, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.


**Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?**
A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.
