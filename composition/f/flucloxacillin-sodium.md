# Flucloxacillin Sodium

**Generic Name:** Flucloxacillin Sodium

**Usage:** Flucloxacillin sodium is a penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections. It's effective against a range of Gram-positive bacteria, making it a common choice for infections such as:

* **Skin infections:**  Boils, abscesses, cellulitis, impetigo.
* **Respiratory tract infections:** Pneumonia (certain types), lung abscesses.
* **Bone and joint infections:** Osteomyelitis.
* **Sepsis (in some cases):**  When caused by susceptible bacteria.


**Important Note:** Flucloxacillin is NOT effective against viral infections (like the common cold or flu) or many Gram-negative bacteria.  A doctor should always determine the appropriate antibiotic based on the specific infection and bacterial identification.  Inappropriate use of antibiotics contributes to antibiotic resistance.


**Side Effects:** Like all medications, flucloxacillin can cause side effects.  These can range from mild to severe.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain.
* **Allergic reactions:** Rash, itching, hives.  More serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) are rare but potentially life-threatening.  Signs of anaphylaxis include difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue.
* **Liver problems:**  Rare but possible, usually indicated by changes in liver function tests.
* **Blood disorders:**  Rare, but can include a decrease in white blood cells (neutropenia).
* **Interstitial nephritis:** Inflammation of the kidneys (rare).


**How it Works:** Flucloxacillin is a beta-lactam antibiotic. It works by interfering with the synthesis of peptidoglycan, a crucial component of bacterial cell walls.  This disruption leads to cell wall instability and ultimately bacterial death.  Flucloxacillin is acid-stable, meaning it can survive the acidic environment of the stomach, allowing for oral administration.  It's considered a penicillinase-resistant penicillin, meaning it's less susceptible to breakdown by certain bacterial enzymes (penicillinases) that can render other penicillins ineffective.
