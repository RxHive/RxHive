# Ferrous asparto glycinate + Methylcobalamin + L-Methyl Folate

**Generic Name:** Ferrous Asparto Glycinate + Methylcobalamin + L-Methylfolate

**Usage:** This combination supplement is primarily used to address deficiencies in iron, vitamin B12 (cobalamin), and folate (vitamin B9).  These nutrients are crucial for various bodily functions, including:

* **Iron:** Red blood cell production, oxygen transport, energy production, and various enzyme functions.  Iron deficiency can lead to anemia.
* **Methylcobalamin:** Nerve function, DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, and metabolism. Deficiency can cause pernicious anemia and neurological problems.  Methylcobalamin is a more bioavailable form of vitamin B12 than cyanocobalamin.
* **L-Methylfolate:** DNA synthesis, cell division, and the metabolism of amino acids and neurotransmitters.  Deficiency can lead to megaloblastic anemia and neurological issues. L-Methylfolate is a more bioavailable form of folate than folic acid.

Therefore, this supplement is often used to treat or prevent anemia, improve energy levels, support neurological health, and address deficiencies resulting from poor diet, malabsorption, or certain medical conditions.  It's important to note that this is a *supplement*, and should not replace a healthy diet and medical advice.  A doctor should diagnose any deficiencies before starting supplementation.


**Side Effects:** Side effects are generally mild but can include:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, constipation, diarrhea, stomach upset. These are more common with iron supplementation.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Rare but possible, including rash, itching, or swelling.
* **Headache:**  Less common but possible.
* **Changes in bowel movements:** Constipation or diarrhea.
* **Metallic taste:** Particularly associated with iron supplements.

More serious side effects are rare but should be reported to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Each component works independently but synergistically:

* **Ferrous Asparto Glycinate:** This form of iron is considered gentler on the stomach than other iron salts and is more readily absorbed by the body. It helps increase hemoglobin levels, improving oxygen transport throughout the body.
* **Methylcobalamin:** This active form of vitamin B12 directly participates in various metabolic processes, including the formation of myelin (protective sheath around nerves) and red blood cell production.
* **L-Methylfolate:** This bioavailable form of folate is easily utilized by the body.  It plays a crucial role in DNA synthesis and cell division, alongside B12 in red blood cell production.  It also participates in the metabolism of homocysteine, a substance elevated in cardiovascular disease.


The combination of these three nutrients addresses common deficiencies and supports various physiological functions, particularly blood production and neurological health.
