# Ampicillin + Sulbactam

**Generic Name:** Ampicillin/Sulbactam

**Usage:** Ampicillin/Sulbactam is a combination antibiotic used to treat a variety of bacterial infections.  It's often prescribed for infections of the:

* **Respiratory tract:**  Pneumonia, bronchitis, sinusitis
* **Skin and skin structures:** Cellulitis, abscesses, wound infections
* **Genitourinary tract:** Urinary tract infections (UTIs), pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
* **Intra-abdominal infections:** Infections of the abdomen
* **Sepsis:** A life-threatening condition caused by the body's overwhelming response to infection.

The specific infections treated will depend on the severity and the bacteria causing the infection.  It's important to note that this antibiotic is not effective against all types of bacteria and should only be used when prescribed by a doctor.


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, ampicillin/sulbactam can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them. Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
* **Allergic reactions:** Rash, itching, hives (less commonly, more serious allergic reactions such as anaphylaxis can occur)
* **Headache**
* **Dizziness**
* **Vaginal yeast infection (candidiasis)**
* **Elevated liver enzymes** (seen in blood tests)


Serious but less common side effects include:

* **Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD):** A potentially life-threatening intestinal infection
* **Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis):** Difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat
* **Seizures:** More likely in patients with pre-existing conditions like kidney disease
* **Jaundice** (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
* **Stevens-Johnson syndrome** and **toxic epidermal necrolysis**: Rare but serious skin reactions


**How it Works:** Ampicillin/sulbactam is a combination of two antibiotics:

* **Ampicillin:** A penicillin-type antibiotic that works by interfering with the formation of the bacterial cell wall, leading to bacterial death.  It's effective against a range of Gram-positive and some Gram-negative bacteria.

* **Sulbactam:** A beta-lactamase inhibitor.  Many bacteria produce beta-lactamases, enzymes that break down penicillin-type antibiotics like ampicillin, rendering them ineffective. Sulbactam inhibits these enzymes, allowing ampicillin to work effectively even against bacteria that produce beta-lactamases.
