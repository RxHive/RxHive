# Ambroxol + Salbutamol

**Generic Name:** Ambroxol + Salbutamol

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to treat symptoms of respiratory illnesses, particularly those involving both congestion and bronchospasm.  Ambroxol is an expectorant, meaning it helps to thin and loosen mucus in the airways, making it easier to cough up. Salbutamol is a bronchodilator, which means it relaxes the muscles around the airways, opening them up to improve breathing.  It's often used for conditions like bronchitis, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and asthma, although the specific use will be determined by a doctor.  **It's crucial to note that this combination is not a first-line treatment for asthma and should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.**

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual and dosage, but common ones include:

* **From Ambroxol:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach upset, dizziness, headache.  Rarely, allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).
* **From Salbutamol:** Tremors, nervousness, headache, palpitations (rapid or irregular heartbeat), muscle cramps, dizziness.  More severe, though less common, side effects include increased blood pressure and heart rate irregularities.  Paradoxical bronchospasm (worsening of wheezing) can occur rarely.

**How it Works:**

* **Ambroxol:** Increases the secretion of fluid in the respiratory tract, thus reducing the viscosity (thickness) of mucus. This facilitates the removal of phlegm through coughing.  It also has some anti-inflammatory effects.
* **Salbutamol:**  Stimulates beta-2 adrenergic receptors in the lungs. This leads to relaxation of the smooth muscles surrounding the bronchioles, resulting in bronchodilation (widening of the airways).  This improves airflow and makes breathing easier.
