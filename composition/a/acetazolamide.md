# Acetazolamide

**Generic Name:** Acetazolamide

**Usage:**

Acetazolamide is a medication primarily used to:

* **Treat glaucoma:** It reduces intraocular pressure (IOP) by decreasing the production of aqueous humor in the eye.  This is often used in conjunction with other glaucoma treatments.
* **Treat altitude sickness (acute mountain sickness):** It helps to prevent or reduce symptoms like headache, nausea, and shortness of breath associated with rapid ascent to high altitudes.
* **Treat edema (swelling) associated with congestive heart failure:** While less common now, it can help reduce fluid buildup.
* **Treat idiopathic intracranial hypertension (pseudotumor cerebri):**  It reduces cerebrospinal fluid pressure.
* **Treat metabolic alkalosis:**  It helps correct an imbalance in the body's acid-base balance.
* **Treat certain types of epilepsy (as an adjunctive therapy):** It may be used in combination with other anti-seizure medications.  This is less common now due to the availability of more effective anti-epileptic drugs.


**Side Effects:**

Less common but more serious side effects include:

**How it Works:**

Acetazolamide is a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor. Carbonic anhydrase is an enzyme that plays a role in fluid balance in the body, particularly in the eyes, kidneys, and brain. By inhibiting this enzyme, acetazolamide:

* Reduces the production of aqueous humor in the eyes, lowering intraocular pressure.
* Increases the excretion of bicarbonate and sodium in the urine, leading to increased fluid loss and reduced edema.
* Affects the pH balance in the body.  This is relevant for its use in treating metabolic alkalosis.
