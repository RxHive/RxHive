# Alpha-Beta Arteether

**Generic Name:** Artemether/Lumefantrine

**(Note:  There is no single drug called "Alpha-Beta Arteether".  "Arteether" is the name of a component within a combination drug, typically combined with lumefantrine.  The correct generic name is Artemether/Lumefantrine.)**

**Usage:**

Artemether/lumefantrine is used to treat uncomplicated malaria caused by *Plasmodium falciparum*, the most dangerous species of malaria parasite.  It's crucial to note that it is **not effective against other types of malaria**.  It's usually prescribed for a specific duration as a course of treatment, and should be completed even if symptoms improve before the end of the course.  Dosage is determined by weight and must be followed strictly as prescribed by a healthcare professional.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Abdominal pain
* Loss of appetite
* Skin rash

Less common but more serious side effects include:

* Prolonged QT interval (can lead to potentially fatal heart rhythm problems)
* Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine)
* Neurological problems (seizures, confusion)
* Allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)


**How it Works:**

Artemether and lumefantrine work in different ways to kill the malaria parasite:

* **Artemether:** This is an artemisinin derivative.  It acts quickly to kill the parasites in the blood.  It disrupts the parasite's ability to use heme, a byproduct of hemoglobin breakdown, leading to the parasite's death.

* **Lumefantrine:** This is a synthetic antimalarial drug. It inhibits the parasite's growth and reproduction, preventing further spread of the infection.  It works by interfering with the parasite's ability to convert heme into hemozoin. The combination of both drugs ensures a more complete eradication of the parasite and helps to delay the development of resistance.
