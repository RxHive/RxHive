# Artesunate

**Generic Name:** Artesunate

**Usage:** Artesunate is an antimalarial drug used to treat severe malaria caused by *Plasmodium falciparum*, particularly cerebral malaria.  It's often used in combination with other antimalarial drugs (e.g., mefloquine, amodiaquine, sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine) for better efficacy and to prevent the development of resistance.  It can be administered intravenously (IV), intramuscularly (IM), or orally (though oral formulations are less common for severe malaria).  It is *not* a first-line treatment for uncomplicated malaria.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the route of administration and dosage.  Common side effects include:

* **Nausea and vomiting:** These are frequently reported, especially after intravenous administration.
* **Fever:**  Paradoxically, fever can worsen initially before improving.
* **Headache:**  This is a relatively common side effect.
* **Hypotension (low blood pressure):**  More common with IV administration.
* **Injection site reactions:** Pain, swelling, or redness at the injection site (with IM administration).
* **Rare but serious side effects:**  These include allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), seizures, and hypoglycemia (low blood sugar).


**How it Works:** Artesunate is a derivative of artemisinin, a natural compound derived from the *Artemisia annua* plant.  It acts by inhibiting the parasite's heme detoxification pathway.  Malaria parasites produce heme as a byproduct of hemoglobin digestion.  Artesunate interferes with the parasite's ability to detoxify this toxic heme, leading to the production of free radicals that damage the parasite and ultimately kill it.

* **Can artesunate be used to prevent malaria?** No, artesunate is a treatment for malaria, not a prophylactic (preventative) drug.  Other medications are used for malaria prevention.

* **What are the contraindications?**  Artesunate should be avoided in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to artemisinin derivatives. Caution is needed in patients with liver or kidney impairment.
