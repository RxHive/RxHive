# Azilsartan medoxomil + Chlorthalidone
### Generic Name
Azilsartan Medoxomil + Chlorthalidone

### Usage

Azilsartan medoxomil/chlorthalidone is a combination medication used primarily to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It works by addressing two aspects of blood pressure regulation. Azilsartan medoxomil is an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), meaning it blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels.  This helps to relax and widen blood vessels, lowering blood pressure. Chlorthalidone is a thiazide diuretic, which helps the body remove excess sodium and water through urine. This also contributes to lower blood pressure.  The combination of these two drugs provides a synergistic effect, often resulting in more effective blood pressure control than either drug alone.  While primarily used for hypertension, this combination may be considered in patients with other conditions that benefit from blood pressure control, always under the strict supervision of a physician.

### Dosage

Dosage should always be determined by a physician and tailored to the individual's needs and response to treatment.  There is no universally recommended dosage and formulations vary. Typical adult dosages may range from one tablet daily, but this depends on factors such as current blood pressure, other medications, and overall health.  

**Oral:** The typical dosage involves taking one tablet once daily, typically in the morning.

**IV:** This medication is not administered intravenously (IV).  It's solely administered orally.


**Children:** This combination medication is not typically recommended for children.  Pediatric hypertension treatment usually employs different approaches.


**Elderly:** Dosage adjustments may be necessary for elderly patients due to potential age-related changes in kidney function.  Lower starting doses may be considered and close monitoring is vital.


**Specific Instructions:**  Always follow your doctor's instructions meticulously regarding dosage and timing.  Take the medication with or without food as directed. Do not crush, chew, or break the tablets unless specifically instructed by your healthcare provider.

### Side Effects

**Common Side Effects:**

* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Back pain
* Muscle cramps

**Less Common but Serious Side Effects:**

* Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat) - **Seek immediate medical attention if this occurs.**
* Hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood) - Symptoms may include muscle weakness, fatigue, and irregular heartbeat.
* Hypotension (low blood pressure) - Symptoms may include dizziness, lightheadedness, and fainting.
* Renal impairment (worsening kidney function)


**Note:** This is not an exhaustive list.  If you experience any unusual or concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.


### How it Works

Azilsartan medoxomil blocks the action of angiotensin II, a hormone that causes blood vessels to constrict. By blocking angiotensin II, azilsartan medoxomil allows blood vessels to relax and widen, thus reducing blood pressure.  Chlorthalidone increases the excretion of sodium and water from the body through the kidneys. This reduction in fluid volume contributes to lower blood pressure.  The combined effect of these two mechanisms leads to a more significant reduction in blood pressure than either drug alone could achieve.

### FAQs

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No, never stop taking this medication abruptly without consulting your doctor.  Sudden cessation can lead to a rebound increase in blood pressure.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store this medication at room temperature, away from light and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medicine?** A:  Excessive alcohol consumption can interact with this medication and lower blood pressure further, potentially leading to dizziness or lightheadedness.  Moderate alcohol consumption should be discussed with your doctor.
* **Q: What are the long-term effects of this medication?** A: Long-term use is generally safe for most individuals when monitored by a healthcare provider. Regular check-ups to monitor blood pressure and kidney function are crucial.
