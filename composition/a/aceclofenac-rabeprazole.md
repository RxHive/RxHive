# Aceclofenac + Rabeprazole

**Generic Name:** Aceclofenac + Rabeprazole

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to relieve pain and inflammation associated with osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis.  The aceclofenac component is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that reduces pain and inflammation. The rabeprazole component is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that reduces stomach acid production.  The rabeprazole is included to help protect the stomach lining from the potential ulcer-causing effects of aceclofenac.  It is crucial to use this medication as directed by a doctor and not for self-medication.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual, but common ones include:

* **Gastrointestinal:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, heartburn, indigestion, gas.  More serious gastrointestinal complications, though less common, include ulcers, bleeding, and perforation.
* **Central Nervous System:** Headache, dizziness, drowsiness.

**How it Works:**

* **Aceclofenac:**  This NSAID works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, chemicals in the body that cause pain, inflammation, and fever.  By reducing prostaglandin production, aceclofenac helps to alleviate pain and inflammation.
* **Rabeprazole:** This PPI works by reducing the production of stomach acid.  It does this by blocking the action of the proton pump, an enzyme in the stomach cells responsible for producing acid. By decreasing stomach acid, it protects the stomach lining from damage caused by NSAIDs like aceclofenac.
