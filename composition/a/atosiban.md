# Atosiban

**Generic Name:** Atosiban

**Usage:**

Atosiban is a synthetic peptide analog of oxytocin used primarily to delay preterm labor in pregnant women at risk of delivery between 24 and 33 weeks of gestation.  It's administered intravenously.  It's not used to stop labor that is already well-established, nor is it used for other conditions.  The decision to use atosiban is made in consultation with a physician considering the risks and benefits for both mother and baby.

**How it Works:**

Atosiban acts as a competitive antagonist at oxytocin receptors.  Oxytocin plays a crucial role in uterine contractions during labor. By blocking oxytocin's action at these receptors, atosiban helps reduce the frequency and intensity of uterine contractions, thereby delaying the onset of delivery and giving the fetus more time to mature in the womb.
