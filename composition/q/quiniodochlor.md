# Quiniodochlor

**Generic Name:** Quiniodochlor

**Usage:** Quiniodochlor is an obsolete antimalarial drug.  It is no longer used in modern medical practice due to the availability of safer and more effective alternatives. Historically, it was used to treat malaria, specifically *Plasmodium falciparum* infections, but its use was limited due to its toxicity.

**Side Effects:**  Quiniodochlor had significant toxicity.  Side effects were common and could be severe, including:

* **Gastrointestinal disturbances:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
* **Hematologic effects:** Anemia (reduction in red blood cells), agranulocytosis (reduction in white blood cells)
* **Neurological effects:**  Headache, dizziness, tinnitus (ringing in the ears),  convulsions in severe cases
* **Hepatotoxicity:** Liver damage
* **Hypersensitivity reactions:** Allergic reactions ranging from mild rashes to severe anaphylaxis


**How it Works:** Quiniodochlor's mechanism of action is similar to other 8-aminoquinoline antimalarials.  It interferes with the parasite's ability to utilize heme, a byproduct of hemoglobin digestion by the malaria parasite.  The accumulation of toxic heme leads to parasite death. However, its exact mechanism and precise targets within the parasite were not as well understood as with newer antimalarials.
