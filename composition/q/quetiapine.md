# Quetiapine
### Generic Name
Quetiapine

### Usage

Quetiapine is an atypical antipsychotic medication primarily used to treat schizophrenia and bipolar disorder.  More specifically, it's effective in managing the acute manic or mixed episodes associated with bipolar I disorder, and the acute depressive episodes associated with bipolar disorder.  It's also used to treat schizophrenia and as an add-on treatment (adjunctive therapy) to antidepressants for major depressive disorder (MDD) when the antidepressants alone aren't providing sufficient relief.  While not its primary use, it has also shown efficacy in managing agitation and delirium in intensive care unit (ICU) patients, though this is considered off-label usage.


### Dosage

Quetiapine dosage varies greatly depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age, and the specific formulation (immediate-release or extended-release).  Dosage adjustments are frequently made based on individual patient response and tolerability.  **It is crucial to follow a healthcare provider's instructions precisely.**  The following information provides a general overview and should not replace professional medical advice:

**Adults:**

* **Bipolar Disorder (Mania):**  Dosage typically starts low and is gradually increased. Immediate-release formulations may begin at 100 mg/day and increase to 400-800 mg/day, while extended-release may start at 300 mg/day and increase to 400-800 mg/day.  The maximum recommended dose is generally 800 mg/day.

* **Bipolar Disorder (Depression):** Initial doses are generally lower and titrated upward.  Extended-release formulations commonly start at 50 mg/day and may be increased to 300 mg/day.

* **Schizophrenia:** Similar to bipolar mania, dosage is often started low and increased gradually. Both immediate-release and extended-release formulations may reach doses of 400-800 mg/day, with a maximum of 800 mg/day.

* **Major Depressive Disorder (adjunctive therapy):** Extended-release formulations are usually prescribed, starting at 50 mg/day and possibly increasing to 150-300 mg/day.


**Children and Adolescents:**

Quetiapine's use in children and adolescents is limited and generally only for bipolar mania or schizophrenia in specific age ranges (typically 10-17 years for bipolar mania and 13-17 years for schizophrenia). Dosages are significantly lower than adult doses and are carefully titrated under close medical supervision.

**Dosage Adjustments:**  Doses may need adjusting in individuals with hepatic (liver) impairment or those taking other medications that interact with Quetiapine.  Renal (kidney) impairment usually doesn't require dosage changes.  Elderly patients often start on lower doses.  Specific adjustments depend heavily on the individual and their circumstances.  A physician will guide these decisions.

**Forms:** Quetiapine is available in immediate-release and extended-release tablets. The extended-release tablets should be swallowed whole and not crushed or chewed.

### Side Effects

**Common Side Effects (>10%):**

* Drowsiness
* Dizziness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Agitation
* Increased appetite
* Dry mouth (xerostomia)
* Orthostatic hypotension (low blood pressure upon standing)
* Increased blood pressure (systolic and diastolic)

**Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**

* Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) – a rare but life-threatening condition
* Extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) – involuntary muscle movements
* Tardive dyskinesia – a potentially irreversible movement disorder
* Suicidal thoughts or behavior
* Worsening of depression
* Changes in blood sugar and cholesterol levels
* Weight gain
* Changes in menstrual cycles
* Liver problems

**Other possible side effects:**  Numerous other side effects are possible, ranging from mild to severe, and encompassing various body systems.  Consult the full prescribing information for a comprehensive list.

If you experience any concerning side effects, contact your doctor immediately.

### How it Works

Quetiapine is an atypical antipsychotic that works by affecting multiple neurotransmitter systems in the brain. Primarily, it blocks dopamine D2 and serotonin 5-HT2 receptors.  Dopamine is involved in regulating mood, thought processes, and movement. Serotonin plays a role in mood, sleep, and appetite.  By blocking these receptors, Quetiapine helps to stabilize mood and reduce psychotic symptoms like hallucinations and delusions. Its effects on other receptors, such as histamine H1 and alpha-adrenergic receptors, contribute to some of its side effects (drowsiness, low blood pressure).

### Precautions

* **Contraindicated:** Quetiapine is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity to quetiapine or any component of the formulation.
* **Drug Interactions:** Quetiapine can interact with numerous other medications, including CNS depressants, antihypertensives, and drugs affecting the CYP3A4 enzyme system (liver enzyme involved in medication metabolism).  Always inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Quetiapine is categorized as Pregnancy Category C.  The potential risks and benefits should be carefully considered before use during pregnancy.  Quetiapine is excreted in breast milk, so a decision regarding breastfeeding should be made with a doctor.
* **Elderly Patients:** Elderly patients with dementia-related psychosis are at increased risk of death when taking antipsychotics. Quetiapine is not approved for this population.
* **Children and Adolescents:** Use in children and adolescents is limited and requires careful monitoring.
* **Suicidal Ideation:**  Increased risk of suicidal thoughts and behaviors, particularly in young adults. Close monitoring is essential.
* **Other Precautions:**  Patients with conditions like liver or kidney disease, heart problems, low white blood cell count, diabetes, or seizure disorders should discuss these with their doctor before taking Quetiapine.

### FAQs

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking Quetiapine?**  A: No. Alcohol can intensify the sedative effects of Quetiapine and increase the risk of side effects.

* **Q: Can I drive while on Quetiapine?** A:  Drowsiness is a common side effect, so avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how Quetiapine affects you.

* **Q: How should I store Quetiapine?** A: Store at room temperature, away from moisture and heat.  Keep it out of reach of children.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A:  Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not double up on doses.

* **Q: Can I stop taking Quetiapine suddenly?** A: No.  Sudden cessation can lead to withdrawal symptoms. Always taper off the medication under the guidance of your healthcare provider.

* **Q:  What should I do if I experience side effects?** A: Contact your doctor immediately if you experience any concerning side effects.  Some side effects are manageable, while others may require dose adjustments or alternative treatments.
