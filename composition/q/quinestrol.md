# Quinestrol

Quinestrol

### Usage
Quinestrol is a synthetic estrogen with a prolonged effect in the body.  Historically, it was used to treat symptoms associated with estrogen deficiency, such as those experienced during menopause.  It was also a component in some combined oral contraceptive pills.  In some cases, it was explored for the treatment of breast and prostate cancers, though this use was not widespread.  It's important to note that quinestrol is no longer commercially available.

### How it Works
Quinestrol is a synthetic estrogen, meaning it mimics the effects of the natural hormone estradiol. It works by binding to estrogen receptors in various cells throughout the body.  This binding triggers changes in gene expression, leading to a range of effects, including those related to the female reproductive system and other tissues. Its prolonged action is due to its slower metabolism compared to other estrogens.  The suppression of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) is a key mechanism in its contraceptive and menopausal symptom relief effects. When combined with a progestin, as in oral contraceptives, it further suppresses the hypothalamic-pituitary system to prevent ovulation.
