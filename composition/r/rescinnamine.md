# Rescinnamine
### Generic Name
Rescinnamine

### Usage
Rescinnamine is a medication that was previously used to treat hypertension (high blood pressure).  It's important to note that this drug is no longer available in the U.S. market and was withdrawn in 1992.  Its primary use was to lower blood pressure by affecting the body's renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS).  While it did have a sedative effect due to its influence on serotonin, this was not its primary therapeutic application.

### How it Works
Rescinnamine works primarily by inhibiting angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE). ACE is an enzyme that converts angiotensin I to angiotensin II. Angiotensin II is a potent vasoconstrictor (a substance that narrows blood vessels), leading to increased blood pressure. By blocking this conversion, Rescinnamine reduces the amount of angiotensin II in the body, thereby causing vasodilation (widening of blood vessels) and lowering blood pressure. Additionally, it impacted serotonin levels in the brain, contributing to its sedative effects.
