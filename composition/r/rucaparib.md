# Rucaparib

**Generic Name:** Rucaparib

**Usage:**

Rucaparib is an oral medication used to treat certain types of ovarian, fallopian tube, and peritoneal cancers.  Specifically, it's used in patients with:

* **Relapsed ovarian, fallopian tube, or peritoneal cancer:**  This means the cancer has returned after initial treatment.  It's often used in patients whose cancer has a specific genetic mutation, particularly BRCA mutations (though it can be used in some without BRCA mutations as well, depending on the specific clinical context).
* **Maintenance treatment of ovarian cancer:** After a patient has responded to platinum-based chemotherapy, rucaparib may be used to help prevent the cancer from returning.

The specific usage and dosage will be determined by a doctor based on the patient's individual condition and response to treatment.


**Side Effects:**

Rucaparib can cause a range of side effects, some common and others rare.  The most common side effects include:

* **Nausea:** This is a frequent side effect and can often be managed with anti-nausea medication.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling tired or weak is also common.
* **Vomiting:** Similar to nausea, this can be managed with medication.
* **Diarrhea:** Changes in bowel habits are possible.
* **Decreased appetite:** Loss of appetite can lead to weight loss.
* **Anemia:** A low red blood cell count, which can cause fatigue and weakness.
* **Low white blood cell count (leukopenia) and low platelet count (thrombocytopenia):** This increases the risk of infection and bleeding.
* **Hair loss (alopecia):**  This is less common but possible.

More serious, though less frequent, side effects can include:

* **Myelosuppression:**  Suppression of bone marrow function leading to low blood cell counts.
* **Increased risk of infection:** Due to low white blood cell counts.
* **Bleeding:** Due to low platelet counts.
* **Liver damage:**  Elevated liver enzymes.
* **Lung damage (interstitial lung disease):**  A rare but serious complication.

This is not an exhaustive list, and the severity and frequency of side effects can vary from person to person.  It's crucial to report any new or worsening symptoms to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Rucaparib is a PARP inhibitor.  PARP (poly ADP-ribose polymerase) is an enzyme involved in DNA repair.  Cancer cells, especially those with BRCA mutations, often rely heavily on PARP for DNA repair.  By inhibiting PARP, rucaparib prevents these cancer cells from repairing their damaged DNA, leading to cell death.  This targeted approach makes it more effective against specific cancer types with certain genetic defects, minimizing damage to healthy cells.
