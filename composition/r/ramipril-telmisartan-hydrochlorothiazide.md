# Ramipril + Telmisartan + Hydrochlorothiazide

**Generic Name:** Ramipril/Telmisartan/Hydrochlorothiazide (various brand names exist)

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's often prescribed when a single medication hasn't been effective enough in lowering blood pressure.  The three active ingredients work together synergistically to achieve better blood pressure control than any one drug alone.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual, but common ones include:

* **Common:** Dizziness, lightheadedness, headache, fatigue, cough (especially with ramipril), diarrhea, nausea, muscle cramps, and changes in potassium levels.

**How it Works:** This triple-combination drug acts in three ways to lower blood pressure:

* **Ramipril:** An ACE inhibitor. It blocks the production of a hormone called angiotensin II, which narrows blood vessels. By blocking angiotensin II, ramipril relaxes and widens blood vessels, reducing blood pressure.
* **Telmisartan:** An angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).  It works similarly to ramipril, but instead of blocking the production of angiotensin II, it blocks its action on blood vessels. This also leads to vasodilation and reduced blood pressure.
* **Hydrochlorothiazide:** A thiazide diuretic. It increases the excretion of sodium and water from the body through the urine. By reducing fluid volume, it helps lower blood pressure.

The combination of these three mechanisms provides a more robust and effective blood pressure lowering effect than any single drug would alone.
