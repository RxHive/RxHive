# Rifampicin + Isoniazid + Ethambutol + Pyrazinamide + Vitamin B

**Generic Name:** Rifampicin + Isoniazid + Ethambutol + Pyrazinamide + Vitamin B6 (often presented as individual components in combination therapy)

**Usage:** This combination of drugs is a first-line treatment for pulmonary tuberculosis (TB).  The specific dosage and duration will be determined by a physician based on individual factors.  Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) is included because isoniazid can interfere with its metabolism, leading to potential side effects.  This combination aims to quickly kill actively replicating TB bacteria and prevent the development of drug resistance.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual and the specific drugs involved.  They can include:

* **Rifampicin:**  Orange discoloration of urine, sweat, and tears; nausea; vomiting; abdominal pain; dizziness; headache; flu-like symptoms; liver damage (rare but serious).
* **Isoniazid:** Peripheral neuropathy (numbness and tingling in the extremities); hepatitis (liver inflammation); seizures (rare but serious).
* **Ethambutol:**  Visual disturbances (blurred vision, difficulty with color discrimination); optic neuritis (inflammation of the optic nerve); joint pain; rash.
* **Pyrazinamide:**  Hyperuricemia (high uric acid levels); rash; nausea; vomiting; joint pain; liver damage (rare but serious).
* **Vitamin B6:** While generally well-tolerated, high doses can cause nausea.


**How it Works:**  This combination works synergistically to target *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, the bacteria that causes TB. Each drug has a different mechanism of action:

* **Rifampicin:** Inhibits bacterial RNA synthesis.
* **Isoniazid:** Interferes with bacterial cell wall synthesis.
* **Ethambutol:** Inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis through a different mechanism than isoniazid.
* **Pyrazinamide:**  Its precise mechanism isn't fully understood, but it is believed to interfere with bacterial metabolism and is particularly effective against bacteria residing in an acidic environment.
* **Vitamin B6:** Counteracts the effects of isoniazid on vitamin B6 metabolism, preventing or reducing the risk of peripheral neuropathy.
