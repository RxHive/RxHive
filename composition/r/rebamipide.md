# Rebamipide
### Generic Name
Rebamipide

### Usage

Rebamipide is primarily used for the treatment of peptic ulcer disease and gastritis.  It acts as a mucosal protective agent, helping to safeguard the lining of the stomach and intestines from damage.  While its main application is in treating these conditions, it's important to note that the specific use and effectiveness might vary depending on individual factors and the severity of the condition.  Always consult a healthcare professional for diagnosis and treatment.

### Dosage

**Adults:** For the treatment of gastric ulcers or lesions, the typical dosage is 100 mg three times daily (TID), administered in the morning, evening, and before bedtime.

**Children:** The safety and efficacy of rebamipide in children have not been established, and it is not generally recommended for pediatric use.

**Dosage Adjustments:**  The manufacturer's labeling does not provide specific dose adjustments for patients with hepatic (liver) or renal (kidney) impairment. However, caution is advised when using rebamipide in these populations.  Always consult a physician before administering the medication to individuals with compromised liver or kidney function.

### Side Effects

Common side effects can include:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal distention
* Dizziness
* Drowsiness
* Edema (swelling)

Less common, but more serious side effects may include:

* Allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
* Liver dysfunction (jaundice, increased liver enzymes)
* Blood disorders (leukopenia, thrombocytopenia)
* Gynecomastia (breast enlargement in males)

If any adverse effects occur, particularly severe or persistent ones, seek immediate medical attention.

### How it Works

Rebamipide works by increasing the production of prostaglandin E2 in the gastric mucosa (the lining of the stomach).  This prostaglandin plays a crucial role in protecting the stomach lining from damage caused by acid and other irritants.  Additionally, rebamipide increases gastric mucus production, improves blood flow to the gastric mucosa, enhances secretion of gastric alkaline substances, and stimulates the growth of gastric mucosal cells.  Through these various mechanisms, it promotes healing and protects against further damage.

### Precautions

* **Hypersensitivity:** Rebamipide is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to the drug or any of its components.
* **Pregnancy and Lactation:** The safety of rebamipide during pregnancy and lactation has not been fully established.  Its use is not recommended during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.
* **Geriatric Patients:**  Caution should be exercised when administering rebamipide to older adults due to the potential for increased sensitivity to side effects.
* **Drug Interactions:** While specific interactions haven't been extensively documented, potential interactions with other medications cannot be ruled out.  Always inform your doctor about all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements.
* **Liver and Kidney Impairment:** Use with caution in patients with impaired liver or kidney function.  Close monitoring may be necessary.


### FAQs

**Q: Can I take rebamipide with food?**

A: Rebamipide can be taken with or without food.

**Q: How should I store rebamipide?**

A: Store rebamipide as directed on the product label, typically in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.

**Q:  What should I do if I miss a dose?**

A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose to catch up.

**Q:  Is rebamipide addictive?**

A:  No, rebamipide is not considered an addictive substance.

**Q: Can I stop taking rebamipide suddenly?**

A: It's important to follow your doctor's instructions regarding discontinuation of the medication.  Do not abruptly stop taking rebamipide without consulting your physician first.
