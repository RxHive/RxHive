# Rifaximin

**Generic Name:** Rifaximin

**Usage:**

Rifaximin is an antibiotic used to treat:

* **Traveler's diarrhea:**  Caused by bacteria (specifically *Escherichia coli*, *Salmonella*, and *Shigella*).  It's primarily used for mild to moderate cases.
* **Hepatic encephalopathy (HE):** A complication of liver disease where toxins build up in the blood, affecting brain function. Rifaximin helps reduce the ammonia levels in the gut that contribute to HE.
* **Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) with diarrhea (IBS-D):**  While not a first-line treatment, it may be used in some cases to reduce symptoms.  Its effectiveness in IBS-D is less established than in traveler's diarrhea and HE.


**Side Effects:**

Rifaximin is generally well-tolerated, but side effects can occur.  These include:

* **Common (affecting more than 10% of users):** Headache, nausea.
* **Less Common:** Abdominal pain, flatulence, dizziness.
* **Rare but serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), *Clostridium difficile* infection (C. difficile colitis – a serious infection of the colon).


**How it Works:**

Rifaximin is a non-absorbable antibiotic. This means it stays primarily in the gut and doesn't get absorbed significantly into the bloodstream.  It works by interfering with the bacterial DNA synthesis of certain types of bacteria, preventing their growth and killing them.  This reduces the amount of toxins produced in the gut, which is particularly beneficial for managing conditions like traveler's diarrhea and hepatic encephalopathy.
