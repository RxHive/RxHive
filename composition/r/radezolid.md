### How it Works
Radezolid works by interfering with bacterial protein synthesis.  Specifically, it binds to the 50S ribosomal subunit within bacterial cells.  The 50S subunit is a critical component of the ribosome, the cellular machinery responsible for building proteins. By binding to this subunit, Radezolid prevents the formation of the initiation complex, a necessary step for protein synthesis.  Without the ability to produce essential proteins, the bacteria cannot survive and multiply, leading to the eradication of the infection. This mechanism of action is similar to other oxazolidinone antibiotics, but Radezolid's unique biaryloxazolidinone structure may offer advantages in terms of efficacy against drug-resistant bacteria.
