# Ribociclib

**Generic Name:** Ribociclib

**Usage:**

Ribociclib is a type of cancer medication called a cyclin-dependent kinase 4 and 6 (CDK4/6) inhibitor. It's used in combination with an aromatase inhibitor (like letrozole or anastrozole) to treat:

* **Hormone receptor-positive, human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2)-negative advanced or metastatic breast cancer:** This means the cancer cells have receptors for hormones like estrogen and aren't HER2-positive.  It's typically used in patients who haven't received prior systemic therapy for advanced disease.
* **Hormone receptor-positive, HER2-negative early-stage breast cancer:**  It's used in combination with endocrine therapy after surgery to reduce the risk of the cancer returning.

**How it Works:**

Ribociclib works by blocking the activity of cyclin-dependent kinases 4 and 6 (CDK4/6).  These enzymes play a crucial role in the cell cycle, specifically in the progression from the G1 to the S phase (the phase where DNA is replicated). By inhibiting CDK4/6, ribociclib slows down or stops the growth and division of cancer cells, particularly those that are hormone receptor-positive.  This effect is enhanced when used in combination with an aromatase inhibitor, which blocks estrogen production.
