# Ritonavir

**Generic Name:** Ritonavir

**Usage:** Ritonavir is an antiretroviral medication primarily used in combination with other antiretroviral drugs to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection.  It's not typically used as monotherapy (on its own) because resistance develops quickly. Its main role is as a "booster" for other protease inhibitors, increasing their blood levels and effectiveness.  This boosted effect extends the duration of other medications, making them more potent against the virus.  It may also be used in combination therapy for Hepatitis C.

**Side Effects:** Ritonavir can cause a wide range of side effects, varying in severity.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas - rare but serious)
* **Metabolic:** Increased blood sugar levels (hyperglycemia), increased cholesterol and triglycerides (lipids), fat redistribution (lipodystrophy - changes in body fat distribution), insulin resistance
* **Neurological:** Dizziness, headache, paresthesia (numbness or tingling), insomnia
* **Skin:** Rash, photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight)

**How it Works:** Ritonavir is a protease inhibitor.  HIV relies on a protease enzyme to replicate.  Ritonavir inhibits this enzyme, preventing the virus from producing infectious viral particles.  Importantly, Ritonavir's primary mechanism of action as a treatment is boosting the levels of other protease inhibitors, making them more effective against HIV.  It achieves this by inhibiting the cytochrome P450 enzyme system (specifically CYP3A4), which is responsible for metabolizing many drugs.  By inhibiting CYP3A4, Ritonavir increases the concentration of other drugs in the bloodstream.
