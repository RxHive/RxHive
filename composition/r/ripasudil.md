# Ripasudil

**Generic Name:** Ripasudil

**Usage:**

Ripasudil is primarily used to treat:

* **Normal-tension glaucoma (NTG):** A type of glaucoma where eye pressure is not elevated but optic nerve damage still occurs.
* **Ocular hypertension:**  Elevated eye pressure that increases the risk of glaucoma.

It's administered as eye drops.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of ripasudil eye drops may include:

* Eye irritation (burning, stinging, redness)
* Headache
* Conjunctival hyperemia (redness of the conjunctiva, the membrane lining the inside of the eyelids and covering the white part of the eye)
* Eyelid irritation
* Blurred vision


Less common but more serious side effects are possible and require immediate medical attention.  These can include:

* Allergic reactions (such as rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Changes in vision
* Severe eye pain


**How it Works:**

Ripasudil is a Rho-kinase inhibitor.  Rho-kinase is an enzyme that plays a role in regulating the tone of blood vessels in the eye. By inhibiting Rho-kinase, ripasudil helps to:

* **Improve blood flow:** This leads to better drainage of fluid from the eye, reducing intraocular pressure (IOP).
* **Reduce inflammation:**  It can also potentially reduce inflammation in the eye, which may contribute to optic nerve damage in glaucoma.
