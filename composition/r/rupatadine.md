# Rupatadine

**Generic Name:** Rupatadine

**Usage:**

Rupatadine is an antihistamine used to relieve the symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and urticaria (hives).  This includes symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, itchy nose and eyes, itching, and rash.  It's often prescribed for both seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis.  It is generally a second-line treatment, meaning it's often used if other treatments like intranasal corticosteroids haven't been effective enough.

**Side Effects:**

Rupatadine is generally well-tolerated, but side effects can occur. These are usually mild and transient and include:

* **Common (affecting more than 1 in 100 people):** Headache, somnolence (sleepiness), fatigue, nausea, dizziness, dyspepsia (indigestion).
* **Uncommon (affecting 1 to 10 in 1000 people):**  Dry mouth, insomnia, palpitations, increased appetite, abnormal liver function tests.
* **Rare (affecting less than 1 in 1000 people):**  Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis - seek immediate medical attention if this occurs),  vision changes, abnormal dreams.

This list is not exhaustive, and other side effects may occur.  Always consult a doctor or pharmacist if you experience any concerning side effects.

**How it Works:**

Rupatadine is a dual antagonist, meaning it blocks two types of receptors in the body:

* **Histamine H1 receptors:**  Histamine is a chemical released by the body during an allergic reaction, causing many of the symptoms. By blocking these receptors, rupatadine prevents histamine from causing these symptoms.
* **Platelet-activating factor (PAF) receptors:** PAF is another inflammatory mediator involved in allergic reactions.  Blocking PAF receptors further reduces inflammation and allergic symptoms.

This dual action makes rupatadine effective in relieving a broader range of allergy symptoms compared to some other antihistamines that only block histamine.
