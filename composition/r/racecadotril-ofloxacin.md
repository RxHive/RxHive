**Racecadotril**

### Generic Name

### Usage
Racecadotril is primarily used to treat acute diarrhea, particularly in adults. It works by reducing the secretion of fluids into the intestines, thus helping to control the symptoms of diarrhea.  It is not typically used for all types of diarrhea, and its effectiveness varies.  A doctor should determine its suitability based on the cause and severity of the diarrhea.


### Dosage
Dosage varies depending on the patient's age and the severity of the diarrhea.  A healthcare professional will determine the appropriate dose.  It is usually administered orally.  There is no standard IV formulation.  Specific dosing instructions must be obtained from a physician or pharmacist.


### Side Effects
* Common: Nausea, abdominal discomfort
* Less Common: Headache, constipation, vomiting
* Serious (though rare): Allergic reactions (requiring immediate medical attention).


### How it Works
Racecadotril is an enkephalinase inhibitor.  Enkephalinases are enzymes that break down certain peptides in the gut involved in fluid secretion. By inhibiting these enzymes, racecadotril reduces the amount of fluid released into the intestines, thereby helping to alleviate diarrhea.


### Precautions
* Not suitable for all types of diarrhea (e.g., those caused by bacterial infection requiring antibiotics).
* Should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity to racecadotril.
* Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a doctor.
* Should not be given to children, as its safety and efficacy in this population have not been established.

**Ofloxacin**

### Generic Name
Ofloxacin

### Usage
Ofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, including those of the respiratory tract, urinary tract, skin, and intestines.  It's prescribed only when other antibiotics are ineffective or unsuitable.


### Dosage
Dosage varies depending on the infection being treated, the patient's age, weight, and kidney function.  It's available in oral and intravenous forms.  A physician determines the correct dose and administration route.


### Side Effects
* Common: Nausea, diarrhea, headache, dizziness
* Less Common:  Insomnia, abdominal pain
* Serious (though rare): Tendonitis, tendon rupture, peripheral neuropathy, liver damage, seizures.  These require immediate medical attention.


### How it Works
Ofloxacin works by inhibiting bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication and repair.  This inhibits bacterial growth and ultimately leads to bacterial cell death.


### Precautions
* Not suitable for all types of bacterial infections.
* Should not be used in patients with known hypersensitivity to fluoroquinolones.
* Can interact with other medications, including antacids and some vitamins.
* Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with a doctor.  Fluoroquinolones are generally avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding unless absolutely necessary.
* Can cause tendon problems, especially in older adults.


### FAQs
* **Q: Can I take ofloxacin with dairy products?** A:  It's generally recommended to avoid dairy products near the time of taking ofloxacin, as they can affect absorption.
* **Q: How should I store ofloxacin?** A: Store as directed on the product label, typically at room temperature, away from moisture and light.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.
* **Q: How long should I take ofloxacin?** A: The duration of treatment depends on the infection and is determined by your doctor. Do not stop treatment prematurely.
