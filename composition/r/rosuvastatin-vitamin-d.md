# Rosuvastatin + Vitamin D

**Generic Name:**  This doesn't exist as a single generic product.  The individual generic names are Rosuvastatin Calcium and Cholecalciferol (Vitamin D3).

**Usage:**

* **Rosuvastatin Calcium:**  Used to lower levels of LDL ("bad") cholesterol and triglycerides, and increase levels of HDL ("good") cholesterol in the blood. This is primarily prescribed to prevent cardiovascular disease in people with high cholesterol or those at high risk.
* **Cholecalciferol (Vitamin D3):** Used to treat and prevent vitamin D deficiency. Vitamin D plays a crucial role in calcium absorption, bone health, and immune function.  It may also have other health benefits, though more research is needed.

A compounded combination might be prescribed if a doctor believes a patient needs both rosuvastatin and vitamin D supplementation simultaneously.  This is often a case-by-case decision.

**Side Effects:**

* **Rosuvastatin Calcium:** Common side effects include headache, muscle aches (myalgia), nausea, diarrhea, and constipation.  Rare but serious side effects include rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown), liver damage, and allergic reactions.
* **Cholecalciferol (Vitamin D3):**  High doses of vitamin D can lead to hypercalcemia (high blood calcium), which can cause symptoms like nausea, vomiting, constipation, weakness, and kidney stones.  Less common side effects may include headache and fatigue.

The side effects of a compounded combination could include any of the side effects listed above for each individual component.

**How it Works:**

* **Rosuvastatin Calcium:**  It works by inhibiting an enzyme (HMG-CoA reductase) involved in cholesterol production in the liver. This leads to a reduction in LDL cholesterol production and an increase in HDL cholesterol.
* **Cholecalciferol (Vitamin D3):**  It's absorbed in the gut and converted into its active form in the liver and kidneys.  The active form of vitamin D binds to receptors in various cells, regulating calcium absorption, bone metabolism, and immune function.

In a compounded preparation, both components would exert their respective actions independently.
