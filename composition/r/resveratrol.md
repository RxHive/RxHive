# Resveratrol

**Generic Name:** Resveratrol

Resveratrol is primarily used as a dietary supplement.  It's marketed for a wide range of purported health benefits, although many lack strong scientific evidence.  These claimed benefits include:

* **Anti-aging:**  Protecting against cell damage from free radicals (oxidative stress) and potentially slowing the aging process.  This is a highly debated area.
* **Neurological health:** Potentially protecting against neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, though research is still ongoing.
* **Cancer prevention:**  Showing promise in some laboratory and animal studies, but human clinical trials have yielded mixed results and further research is needed.
* **Anti-inflammatory effects:**  Resveratrol exhibits anti-inflammatory properties, though the clinical significance remains unclear.


**Important Note:**  Many of the purported benefits of resveratrol are based on preclinical studies (cell cultures and animal models) and may not translate to similar effects in humans. * **Skin rash:**  Allergic reactions can occur.
* **Liver damage:** In rare cases and usually associated with high doses or long-term use.
