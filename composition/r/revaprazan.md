# Revaprazan

**Generic Name:** Revaprazan

**Usage:**  Revaprazan is used to treat gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), specifically erosive esophagitis (damage to the esophagus from stomach acid). It's a potassium-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB) and is taken orally once daily.  It may also be used for other conditions as determined by a physician.


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, revapazran can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them. Common side effects can include:

* Headache
* Diarrhea
* Nausea
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Constipation
* Abdominal pain
* Dizziness
* Flatulence

Serious side effects are less common but can occur.  These should be reported to a doctor immediately.  They may include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Kidney problems
* Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (C. difficile infection)


**How it Works:** Revaprazan is a potassium-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB).  Unlike proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) which irreversibly block the proton pump,  P-CABs reversibly bind to the potassium ion binding site on the gastric H+/K+-ATPase enzyme. This prevents acid secretion into the stomach, thereby reducing the amount of stomach acid that refluxes into the esophagus.  This mechanism of action allows for more predictable and consistent acid reduction compared to PPIs.
