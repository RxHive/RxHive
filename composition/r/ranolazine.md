# Ranolazine

**Generic Name:** Ranolazine

**Usage:**

Ranolazine is used to treat chronic angina (chest pain) in adults who have not been adequately helped by other medications.  It's specifically used to reduce the frequency and severity of angina episodes.  It's typically used *in addition to* other angina medications, not as a standalone treatment.  It is *not* used for the treatment of acute angina attacks.

**Side Effects:**

Ranolazine can cause a number of side effects, some common and some serious.  The most common side effects include:

* **Nausea:** This is a frequently reported side effect.
* **Constipation:** Difficulty with bowel movements can occur.
* **Dizziness:** Feeling lightheaded or unsteady.
* **Headache:** A common side effect.
* **Vomiting:**  In some individuals.


More serious, although less common, side effects include:

* **QT interval prolongation:** This is a potentially life-threatening effect on the heart's rhythm.  Regular ECG monitoring may be necessary.
* **Torsades de pointes:** A type of abnormal heart rhythm related to QT prolongation.
* **Hepatotoxicity (liver damage):**  Liver function tests may be monitored.
* **Bradycardia (slow heart rate):**  A decrease in heart rate.
* **Hypotension (low blood pressure):**  A drop in blood pressure.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Such as rash, itching, or swelling.

This is not an exhaustive list, and you should consult your doctor or pharmacist for a complete list of potential side effects.

**How it Works:**

Ranolazine works by reducing the flow of calcium ions into heart muscle cells.  This reduces the workload on the heart, decreasing the oxygen demand during periods of exertion.  By lessening the heart's oxygen demand, it helps to prevent or reduce the frequency and severity of angina attacks. It works differently from other angina medications like nitrates or beta-blockers.
