# Ramosetron

**Generic Name:** Ramosetron

**Usage:** Ramosetron is used to prevent nausea and vomiting caused by chemotherapy (cancer treatment).  It's specifically a 5-HT3 receptor antagonist.  It is typically administered intravenously (IV) or orally.  The specific dosage and administration route will be determined by a healthcare professional based on the individual's condition and chemotherapy regimen.

* **Dizziness:** Feeling lightheaded or unsteady.

This is not an exhaustive list, and other side effects may occur.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist for a complete list and advice on managing any side effects you experience.

**How it Works:** Ramosetron works by blocking the action of serotonin (5-HT3) in the gastrointestinal tract and the chemoreceptor trigger zone (CTZ) in the brain.  Serotonin is a neurotransmitter that plays a role in the vomiting reflex triggered by chemotherapy. By blocking serotonin receptors, ramosetron prevents the signals that cause nausea and vomiting.
