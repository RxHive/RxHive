# Rioprostil
### Generic Name
Rioprostil

### Usage
Rioprostil is a synthetic prostaglandin E1 analog that was investigated for the treatment and prevention of peptic ulcer disease.  Its primary intended use was to reduce gastric acid secretion and protect the stomach lining from damage.  While it showed promise in pre-clinical and early clinical trials, its development was ultimately discontinued in 2000.  Therefore, it is not currently available for prescription or use. ### How it Works
Rioprostil's mechanism of action involved directly inhibiting the secretion of gastrin, a hormone that stimulates the production of stomach acid. By reducing gastrin levels, Rioprostil aimed to decrease the amount of acid in the stomach, thus protecting the stomach lining from damage caused by acid. It also possessed cytoprotective properties, meaning it could help to enhance the stomach's natural defense mechanisms against ulcers.
