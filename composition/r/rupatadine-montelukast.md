# Rupatadine + Montelukast

**Generic Name:** Rupatadine fumarate + Montelukast sodium

**Usage:**

Rupatadine + Montelukast is used to relieve symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and chronic idiopathic urticaria (hives).  It's a combination medication treating both the early and late phases of allergic reactions.  It's typically prescribed for adults and children (age and dosage vary depending on specific product formulation and physician recommendation).  The specific usage should always be determined by a physician based on individual needs and medical history.

**Side Effects:**

Side effects are generally mild and infrequent, but may include:

* **Common:** Headache, nausea, dizziness, somnolence (sleepiness), fatigue, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.
* **Less Common:**  Dry mouth, vomiting, insomnia, nervousness, and upper respiratory tract infections.
* **Rare:**  Serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis),  liver problems,  suicidal thoughts or behavior (although this is more commonly associated with montelukast alone and requires close monitoring).


It's crucial to note that this is not an exhaustive list, and the severity and frequency of side effects can vary greatly between individuals.  Any new or worsening symptoms should be reported to a doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

This combination medication works through two different mechanisms:

* **Rupatadine:**  This is a peripheral H1-antihistamine, meaning it blocks histamine receptors in the periphery (outside the central nervous system). Histamine is a chemical released during an allergic reaction, causing symptoms like itching, sneezing, and runny nose. By blocking these receptors, rupatadine helps to reduce these symptoms.

* **Montelukast:** This is a leukotriene receptor antagonist.  Leukotrienes are inflammatory chemicals also involved in allergic reactions.  Montelukast blocks the action of leukotrienes, reducing inflammation and further alleviating symptoms like nasal congestion, coughing, and wheezing.  It's particularly effective in controlling the late phase of allergic reactions.

The combination of these two active substances provides a broader and more effective approach to managing allergic symptoms than either drug alone.
