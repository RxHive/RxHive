# Ramipril + Atorvastatin

**Generic Name:** Ramipril and Atorvastatin

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to treat patients with both high blood pressure (hypertension) and high cholesterol (hyperlipidemia).  It's often prescribed when a patient needs both an ACE inhibitor (ramipril) and a statin (atorvastatin) to manage their cardiovascular risk.  The specific dosage of each component will be determined by a physician based on individual needs and health status.

**How it Works:**

* **Ramipril:** This is an ACE (angiotensin-converting enzyme) inhibitor.  ACE inhibitors work by blocking the production of angiotensin II, a hormone that narrows blood vessels. By blocking this hormone, ramipril helps to relax and widen blood vessels, lowering blood pressure and reducing the strain on the heart.

* **Atorvastatin:** This is a statin, a type of medication that lowers cholesterol levels in the blood.  It works by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase, an enzyme that plays a crucial role in cholesterol production in the liver.  By reducing cholesterol production, atorvastatin helps lower LDL ("bad") cholesterol and triglycerides, while increasing HDL ("good") cholesterol.  Lowering cholesterol helps reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke.
