# Repaglinide + Voglibose

**Generic Name:** Repaglinide + Voglibose (This is not a standard, FDA-approved combination. Repaglinide and Voglibose are used individually.)

**Usage:**  There is no FDA-approved combination product of Repaglinide and Voglibose.  Repaglinide and Voglibose are used separately to treat type 2 diabetes.

* **Repaglinide:** Used to improve blood sugar control in adults with type 2 diabetes. It's usually used along with diet and exercise, and sometimes in combination with other diabetes medications (but not Voglibose in an approved combination).  It's a meglitinide, a type of short-acting insulin secretagogue.

* **Voglibose:** Used to improve blood sugar control in adults with type 2 diabetes. It's an alpha-glucosidase inhibitor, which works by slowing down the digestion and absorption of carbohydrates.  It's often used in combination with other diabetes medications, but not with Repaglinide in an approved formulation.

**Side Effects:**

* **Repaglinide:**  Common side effects include hypoglycemia (low blood sugar), which can be serious,  headache, diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting. Less common but more serious side effects include weight gain and pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas).

* **Voglibose:** Common side effects include diarrhea, abdominal pain, gas, and flatulence.  Less frequent but more serious side effects are rarely reported.

**How it Works:**

* **Repaglinide:**  Stimulates the pancreas to release more insulin when blood glucose levels are elevated.  It's a short-acting medication, meaning its effects are relatively brief.

* **Voglibose:** Slows down the breakdown of carbohydrates in the digestive tract, reducing the rate at which glucose enters the bloodstream after meals. This helps to prevent spikes in blood sugar levels.
