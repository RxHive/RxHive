# Rifampicin

**Generic Name:** Rifampin

**Usage:**

Rifampin is an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, including:

* **Tuberculosis (TB):**  Often used in combination with other medications for both active and latent TB infection.
* **Leprosy:**  Used in combination with other drugs to treat this chronic bacterial infection.
* **Meningitis (caused by *Haemophilus influenzae*, *Neisseria meningitidis*):** Sometimes used in combination therapy.
* **Other bacterial infections:**  May be used for certain types of bacterial pneumonia, and some other infections depending on sensitivity testing.


**Side Effects:**

Rifampin can cause a range of side effects, some common and others rare but serious.  These include:

* **Common:**  Orange-red discoloration of urine, sweat, and tears; nausea; vomiting; diarrhea; loss of appetite; stomach pain; dizziness; fatigue.
* **Less Common:**  Rash; itching; fever; flu-like symptoms.
* **Serious (requires immediate medical attention):**  Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes); dark urine; light-colored stools; abdominal pain; liver problems; kidney problems;  blood disorders (decreased blood cell counts); allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis); muscle pain or weakness.  


**How it Works:**

Rifampin is a bacteriostatic (inhibits bacterial growth) or bactericidal (kills bacteria) antibiotic depending on the concentration and the bacteria being treated. It works by inhibiting bacterial DNA-dependent RNA polymerase.  This enzyme is essential for bacterial RNA synthesis (transcription), which is crucial for bacterial protein production and survival. By blocking this process, rifampin prevents bacteria from multiplying and ultimately leads to their death or inhibits their growth.
