# Recombinant follicle stimulating hormone

**Generic Name:** Recombinant Follicle-Stimulating Hormone (rFSH)  *(Note: There isn't one single generic name as different manufacturers produce rFSH under various brand names.  This is the general designation.)*

**Usage:**

rFSH is primarily used to stimulate the growth and maturation of follicles in the ovaries in women and sperm production in men.  Specific uses include:

* **Infertility in women:**  In women undergoing assisted reproductive technologies (ART) such as in-vitro fertilization (IVF), rFSH is used to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple mature eggs. It's also used in cases of anovulatory infertility (where the ovaries don't release eggs).
* **Infertility in men:** In men with hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (where the pituitary gland doesn't produce enough hormones to stimulate the testes), rFSH is used to stimulate sperm production.  It's often used in conjunction with another hormone, luteinizing hormone (LH).
* **Premature ovarian failure:** In some cases, it may be used to stimulate ovarian function.
* **Off-label uses:** While not formally approved for all uses, it may be used in other situations under a doctor's supervision.

**How it Works:**

rFSH is a bioengineered version of the naturally occurring follicle-stimulating hormone.  It works by binding to receptors on the cells in the ovaries (granulosa cells) and testes (Sertoli cells). This binding stimulates the growth and maturation of follicles in the ovaries, leading to the production of eggs.  In men, it stimulates Sertoli cells to promote spermatogenesis (sperm production).
