# Betamethasone + Gentamicin + Tolnaftate + Clioquinol Iodochlorhydroxyquin
### Generic Name
Betamethasone + Gentamicin + Tolnaftate + Clioquinol (Iodochlorhydroxyquin)


**Note:**  This combination is not a standard, commercially available fixed-dose formulation.  The components are often used together in compounding pharmacies to create a customized topical preparation for specific dermatological conditions.  The information below pertains to the individual components and their typical uses.  **Always consult a doctor or pharmacist before using any compounded medication.**  The dosages and instructions provided are general guidelines and may not accurately reflect the specific compounded preparation you receive.


### Usage

This combination of medications targets various skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Each component plays a specific role:

* **Betamethasone:** A corticosteroid that reduces inflammation and itching.
* **Gentamicin:** An aminoglycoside antibiotic effective against various bacteria.
* **Tolnaftate:** An antifungal agent targeting dermatophytes (fungi causing skin infections like athlete's foot and ringworm).
* **Clioquinol (Iodochlorhydroxyquin):** An antimicrobial agent with activity against bacteria and fungi.  It has also shown some anti-inflammatory properties.


The combination is typically used for treating inflammatory skin conditions complicated by bacterial and/or fungal infections.  Examples include eczema with secondary infection, psoriasis with superimposed infection, or other dermatological conditions where both inflammation and infection are present.


### Dosage

Because this is a compounded medication, the dosage will vary significantly depending on the prescriber's instructions and the specific needs of the patient.  **Dosage should never be determined by the patient.**

* **Topical Application:**  The cream or ointment is usually applied thinly to the affected area once or twice daily, as directed by the physician. The duration of treatment will also depend on the specific condition and response to therapy.

* **Pediatric Use:** Dosage for children will be determined by the physician, considering the child's age, weight, and the severity of the condition.  Generally, lower doses may be used for children.

* **Adult Use:** Dosage for adults will be determined by the physician, based on the size of the affected area and severity of the condition.


**It is crucial to follow the prescribed dosage and frequency precisely, as provided by the compounding pharmacist and physician.**


### Side Effects

The side effects listed below represent potential adverse effects of the *individual* components.  The likelihood and severity of side effects from a compounded preparation will vary.

* **Common Side Effects:**
    * Burning or stinging sensation at the application site
    * Skin irritation (redness, itching, dryness)
    * Thinning of the skin (with prolonged use of betamethasone)
    * Skin discoloration (with prolonged use of clioquinol)


* **Less Common, but Serious Side Effects:**
    * Allergic reactions (rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing)
    * Systemic absorption of corticosteroids (leading to elevated blood sugar, weight gain, etc.)  More likely with prolonged use or large surface area application.
    * Nephrotoxicity (kidney damage) and ototoxicity (hearing loss) are potential, though rare, side effects associated with gentamicin, particularly with systemic absorption.


**If any unusual or severe side effects occur, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional immediately.**


### How it Works

This combination medication works through the synergistic effects of its components:

* **Betamethasone:** Decreases inflammation and itching by suppressing the body's inflammatory response.
* **Gentamicin:** Kills bacteria by interfering with their protein synthesis, ultimately leading to bacterial cell death.
* **Tolnaftate:** Disrupts the fungal cell membrane, leading to fungal cell death.
* **Clioquinol:** Disrupts bacterial and fungal cell membranes and has some anti-inflammatory effects.


The combined action of these components provides broad-spectrum antimicrobial coverage and reduces inflammation associated with skin infections.


### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  This combination should not be used on patients with known allergies to any of its components.  Use caution with patients with impaired kidney function (due to gentamicin).
* **Drug Interactions:**  This compounded medication may interact with other drugs.  Inform your physician of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use during pregnancy and breastfeeding should only be under the strict supervision of a physician. The benefits must outweigh the potential risks.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Use should be under the close supervision of a physician, particularly in children and older adults.


**Always inform your physician about any existing health conditions, allergies, and medications before starting this treatment.**


### FAQs

* **Q: How long should I use this medication?**  A: The duration of treatment varies depending on the condition and response, and will be determined by your physician.
* **Q: Can I use this medication on my face?** A:  This should only be determined by your physician.  Use on the face may be advised against due to potential for skin thinning and absorption of the corticosteroids.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Apply the medication as soon as you remember, but do not double the dose.
* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed by the compounding pharmacy, typically at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
* **Q: Is it safe to use this medication for a long time?** A: Prolonged use should be avoided due to potential side effects from the corticosteroids and other components. Your physician will determine the appropriate treatment duration.
