# Buclizine
### Generic Name
Buclizine

### Usage

Buclizine is primarily used to prevent and treat nausea, vomiting, and dizziness associated with motion sickness.  It's also used to alleviate symptoms of various conditions including:

* **Allergies:** Buclizine's antihistamine properties can help reduce allergic symptoms.
* **Appetite Loss:**  It can stimulate appetite in individuals experiencing a loss of appetite, although the precise mechanism isn't fully understood.
* **Pruritic Skin Disorders (Itchy Skin):** Buclizine may help manage the itching associated with certain skin conditions.
* **Migraines:** Some studies suggest a potential role in migraine treatment, though further research is needed.


### Dosage

Dosage varies depending on the condition being treated, the patient's age, and the specific formulation of buclizine (typically as hydrochloride).  Always follow your doctor's or pharmacist's instructions.  The information below is for general guidance only and should not replace professional medical advice.

**Adults:**

* **Motion Sickness (Prophylaxis):** 25-50 mg orally 30 minutes before travel, repeated every 4-6 hours as needed.
* **Nausea and Vomiting:** 25-50 mg orally three times a day (TID).
* **Allergies:** 25-50 mg as a single dose, with additional doses as needed, not exceeding 100 mg/day.
* **Appetite Loss:** 25 mg orally twice a day (BID).
* **Migraine:** 12.5 mg orally as soon as symptoms begin.

**Children:** Dosages for children are significantly lower and vary greatly depending on age and weight.  Always consult a pediatrician before administering buclizine to children.  Examples include:

* **Migraine (10-14 years):** 6.25 mg at symptom onset.
* **Allergies, Nausea & Vomiting, Motion Sickness (5-10 years):** 12.5 mg once or twice daily.
* **Allergies, Nausea & Vomiting, Motion Sickness (>10 years):** 25-50 mg as a single dose, with additional doses as needed, not exceeding 100 mg/day.
* **Appetite Loss (6-12 years):** 12.5 mg BID.
* **Appetite Loss (>12 years):** 25 mg BID.

**Dosage Adjustments:**  The manufacturer's labeling does not provide specific dosage adjustments for hepatic (liver) or renal (kidney) impairment.  Consult your doctor if you have liver or kidney problems.


### Side Effects

Common side effects are generally mild but can include:

* Drowsiness
* Dizziness
* Dry mouth

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Hypotension (low blood pressure)
* Palpitations
* Difficulty breathing (dyspnea)
* Skin rash
* Headache
* Sedation
* Insomnia
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Urinary retention
* Tremor
* Blurred vision
* Weight gain

If you experience any concerning side effects, stop taking buclizine and consult your doctor immediately.


### How it Works

Buclizine is a piperazine antihistamine with antiemetic (anti-vomiting) and orexigenic (appetite-stimulating) effects. Its exact mechanism for appetite stimulation isn't completely understood, but it may involve influencing the hypothalamus, a brain region that regulates appetite.  Buclizine works by:

* Reducing vestibular stimulation (inner ear signals that contribute to motion sickness).
* Depressing labyrinthine function (reducing signals from the inner ear).
* Acting on the medullary chemoreceptor trigger zone to suppress vomiting.
* Blocking histamine receptors in the vomiting center.
* Possessing anticholinergic properties (blocking muscarinic receptors).


### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  Buclizine is contraindicated (should not be used) in individuals with hypersensitivity to buclizine or any of its components, neonates (newborns), pregnant women, and breastfeeding women.
* **Interactions:**  Buclizine can interact with other medications, particularly central nervous system (CNS) depressants (like sedatives or alcohol) and other anticholinergic drugs.  Concurrent use may increase drowsiness or other side effects.  Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.
* **Warnings:**  Use with caution in patients with open-angle glaucoma, urinary tract obstruction, pyloroduodenal obstruction, prostatic hyperplasia, epilepsy, renal or hepatic impairment, hyperthyroidism, and peptic ulcers.  Avoid driving or operating machinery if drowsiness occurs.
* **Pregnancy and Lactation:**  Buclizine is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding.


### FAQs

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking buclizine?** A: No, avoid alcohol as it can increase drowsiness and dizziness.
* **Q: How should I store buclizine?** A: Store it as directed on the label, typically in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.
* **Q: Is buclizine habit-forming?** A: Buclizine is not considered habit-forming.
* **Q:  Can I take buclizine long-term?** A:  Long-term use should only be under the guidance of a doctor.  They will assess the need for continued treatment and monitor for side effects.
