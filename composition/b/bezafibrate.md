# Bezafibrate

**Generic Name:** Bezafibrate

**Usage:**

Bezafibrate is a fibric acid derivative used to lower high levels of triglycerides and cholesterol in the blood.  It's primarily prescribed for patients with hypertriglyceridemia (high triglyceride levels) and/or mixed dyslipidemia (a combination of high triglycerides and low HDL cholesterol, often with high LDL cholesterol).  It's often used in conjunction with lifestyle modifications such as diet and exercise.  Bezafibrate is not usually a first-line treatment for high cholesterol; statins are typically preferred unless there are contraindications.

**How it Works:**

Bezafibrate works primarily by activating peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor alpha (PPAR-α).  This receptor plays a role in regulating lipid metabolism.  By activating PPAR-α, bezafibrate increases the breakdown of triglycerides and increases the excretion of cholesterol from the body.  It also slightly increases levels of HDL ("good") cholesterol.
