# Lactulose

**Generic Name:** Lactulose

**Usage:**

Lactulose is a laxative used to treat constipation and to reduce the amount of ammonia in the blood of people with liver disease (hepatic encephalopathy).  For constipation, it's typically used short-term to relieve symptoms. For hepatic encephalopathy, it's used long-term to manage the condition.  The dosage and frequency will vary depending on the individual and the specific condition being treated.  Always follow your doctor's instructions.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of lactulose include:

* Gas
* Bloating
* Abdominal cramping
* Diarrhea (especially with higher doses)
* Nausea

Less common but more serious side effects can occur.  These include dehydration (due to diarrhea), electrolyte imbalances, and in rare cases, rectal discomfort or irritation.  If you experience severe or persistent side effects, discontinue use and contact your doctor immediately.

**How it Works:**

Lactulose is a synthetic disaccharide (a type of sugar). It works in two ways:

1. **Osmotic effect:**  Lactulose is poorly absorbed in the small intestine. It draws water into the colon, softening the stool and making it easier to pass. This is how it treats constipation.

2. **Ammonia reduction:** In the colon, bacteria break down lactulose, producing acids that lower the pH.  This acidic environment traps ammonia, preventing its absorption into the bloodstream. Ammonia is a toxic byproduct of protein metabolism that can build up in people with liver disease, leading to hepatic encephalopathy. By lowering ammonia levels, lactulose helps improve brain function and reduce symptoms of this condition.

* **Q: Can I take lactulose long-term?** A: For constipation, lactulose is generally for short-term use only.  Long-term use should only be considered under a doctor's supervision.  For hepatic encephalopathy, long-term use is often necessary to manage the condition.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at once.
