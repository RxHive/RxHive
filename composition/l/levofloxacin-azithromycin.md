# Levofloxacin + Azithromycin

**Generic Name:** Levofloxacin + Azithromycin (combination not commercially available as a fixed-dose combination)

**Usage:**  Levofloxacin and azithromycin are *not* typically prescribed together as a fixed-dose combination.  They are frequently used separately, or sequentially, to treat similar infections, particularly respiratory tract infections.  A doctor might prescribe them individually or consecutively depending on the specific infection, severity, and patient factors.  Using them together would only be done under very specific circumstances and would require careful monitoring by a physician.  Potential uses in combination might include treating severe or atypical infections where the suspected pathogens are susceptible to both antibiotics, or in cases where initial treatment with one antibiotic fails.

**Side Effects:**  The side effects listed below are those associated with *either* Levofloxacin *or* Azithromycin, individually.  Combining them could potentially increase the risk of experiencing some side effects.  It's crucial to note that not everyone experiences these side effects, and their severity can vary.

* **Levofloxacin:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, insomnia, anxiety, tendonitis or tendon rupture (especially in older adults or those on corticosteroids), photosensitivity, seizures (rare), prolonged QT interval (can lead to potentially fatal heart rhythm problems), and allergic reactions (including severe ones).

* **Azithromycin:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, rash, allergic reactions (including severe ones), abnormal liver function tests, changes in taste, and potentially rare but serious heart rhythm problems.


**How it Works:**

* **Levofloxacin:**  A fluoroquinolone antibiotic that works by inhibiting the enzymes (topoisomerase II and IV) necessary for bacterial DNA replication and repair.  This leads to bacterial cell death.  It's effective against a broad range of Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria.

* **Azithromycin:** A macrolide antibiotic that works by binding to the 50S ribosomal subunit of bacteria, preventing protein synthesis. This stops the bacteria from growing and reproducing.  It's effective against a range of Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria, as well as some atypical bacteria.
