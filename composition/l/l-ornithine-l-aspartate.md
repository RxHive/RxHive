# L-Ornithine L-Aspartate

**Generic Name:** L-Ornithine L-Aspartate (LOLA)

**Usage:**

L-Ornithine L-Aspartate is primarily used to support liver function and reduce the build-up of ammonia in the blood (hyperammonemia).  This is particularly relevant in individuals with liver disease, including cirrhosis and hepatic encephalopathy. It's also sometimes used to:

* **Improve athletic performance:** Some studies suggest it may aid in reducing fatigue and improving muscle recovery after intense exercise, though more research is needed.
* **Promote wound healing:**  There's limited evidence suggesting a potential role in improving wound healing, but more robust clinical trials are necessary.
* **Reduce fatigue:**  While some individuals report decreased fatigue, this is not consistently supported by research.

It's crucial to understand that the evidence supporting uses beyond liver support is often preliminary or limited.  Always consult a healthcare professional before using LOLA for any purpose.

**Side Effects:**

Generally, L-Ornithine L-Aspartate is considered well-tolerated. However, some individuals may experience:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, diarrhea, and abdominal discomfort are the most commonly reported side effects.
* **Headache:**  Less common, but can occur in some individuals.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Although rare, allergic reactions are possible.  Stop use and seek medical attention if you experience symptoms such as rash, itching, or difficulty breathing.

The severity and frequency of side effects can vary depending on the dosage and individual sensitivity.

**How it Works:**

L-Ornithine L-Aspartate works primarily by influencing the urea cycle, a crucial metabolic pathway in the liver responsible for eliminating ammonia, a toxic byproduct of protein metabolism.  Ornithine and aspartate are amino acids that act as intermediates in this cycle. By supplementing with LOLA, the body may be better equipped to convert ammonia into urea, a less toxic substance that can be excreted by the kidneys. This helps reduce the accumulation of ammonia in the blood, which is particularly important for individuals with impaired liver function.
