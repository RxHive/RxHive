# Levofloxacin + Ambroxol

**Generic Name:** Levofloxacin + Ambroxol

**Usage:**  This combination medication is used to treat respiratory tract infections, particularly those involving bronchitis or pneumonia, where both bacterial infection and mucus buildup are present. Levofloxacin, a fluoroquinolone antibiotic, targets and kills bacteria causing the infection. Ambroxol, a mucolytic agent, helps to thin and loosen the mucus, making it easier to cough up.  The precise indication will vary depending on local regulations and the prescribing physician's judgment. It's crucial to understand that this combination should *only* be used under the guidance of a doctor and not for self-medication.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary from person to person but may include:

* **From Levofloxacin:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, insomnia,  rash, tendonitis or tendon rupture (rare but serious), photosensitivity,  QT prolongation (a heart rhythm abnormality, rare but serious), seizures (rare),  peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage),  pseudomembranous colitis (a severe intestinal infection),  liver problems.
* **From Ambroxol:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea,  dry mouth,  indigestion,  allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).

**Important Note:** This is not an exhaustive list, and more serious side effects are possible.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any severe or unusual side effects.

**How it Works:**

* **Levofloxacin:**  This antibiotic works by inhibiting bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication and repair.  By inhibiting these enzymes, Levofloxacin prevents bacterial growth and eventually kills the bacteria.
* **Ambroxol:** This mucolytic agent increases the secretion of watery mucus from the bronchial glands, reducing the viscosity of mucus and making it easier to expectorate. It also stimulates the activity of cilia, tiny hair-like structures in the lungs that help clear mucus.  This dual action aids in clearing the airways of infected mucus.
