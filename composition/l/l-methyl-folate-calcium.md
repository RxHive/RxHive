# L-Methyl Folate Calcium

**Generic Name:** L-Methylfolate Calcium

**Usage:**

L-Methylfolate Calcium is a form of folate (vitamin B9) used to treat or prevent folate deficiency.  It's often preferred over folic acid because it's the biologically active form of folate, meaning the body can use it directly without needing to convert it.  This is especially important for individuals with genetic variations (like MTHFR gene mutations) that impair the conversion of folic acid to its active form.  Prescriptions are usually for:

* **Folate deficiency:**  Symptoms can include fatigue, anemia, and neurological problems.
* **Prevention of neural tube defects:** In women of childbearing age, it's crucial during pregnancy planning and early pregnancy.
* **Depression:** Some research suggests it may be helpful in treating certain types of depression, particularly those related to folate deficiency.
* **Other neurological conditions:**  It's being explored for potential benefits in other neurological conditions like Alzheimer's disease, but more research is needed.


**Side Effects:**

* **Gastrointestinal issues:**  These include nausea, diarrhea, bloating, and stomach upset. These are usually mild and tend to subside as the body adjusts.
* **Allergic reactions:**  While rare, allergic reactions are possible.  Symptoms can range from rash to more serious reactions requiring immediate medical attention.
* **Sleep disturbances:** Some individuals report difficulty sleeping.
* **Headache:** Although uncommon.
