# Levo-carnitine

**Generic Name:** Levo-carnitine (L-carnitine)

**Usage:**

Levo-carnitine is primarily used to treat carnitine deficiency, a condition where the body doesn't have enough carnitine to transport fatty acids into the mitochondria for energy production.  This deficiency can be inherited or acquired (e.g., due to kidney dialysis).  It's also used in some cases to:

* **Improve energy levels and exercise performance:** Although evidence is mixed and more research is needed, some studies suggest benefits for athletes and individuals with certain medical conditions impacting energy production.

* **Help with heart health:**  Some studies suggest potential benefits for heart health in specific populations, such as those with heart failure, but more research is required.
* **Cognitive function:** Preliminary research suggests potential benefits for cognitive function in specific populations, but further research is needed to confirm these benefits.

**Side Effects:**

* **Body odor:** A fishy body odor is a common side effect.
* **Headache:**  Mild headaches can occur.
* **Dizziness:** In some cases, dizziness may be experienced.
* **Allergic reactions:** Although rare, allergic reactions, such as skin rash, itching, and swelling, can occur.


**How it Works:**

Levo-carnitine is a naturally occurring amino acid-like compound that plays a crucial role in energy metabolism. It acts as a transporter, carrying long-chain fatty acids into the mitochondria, the powerhouses of cells.  Once inside the mitochondria, these fatty acids are broken down to produce energy.  In carnitine deficiency, the lack of levo-carnitine impairs this process, leading to fatigue, muscle weakness, and other symptoms.  Supplementing with levo-carnitine can help restore this process and improve energy production.
