# L-Methyl Folate + Methylcobalamin + Pyridoxal-

**Generic Name:** L-Methylfolate (L-5-Methyltetrahydrofolate), Methylcobalamin, Pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (P5P)

**Usage:** This combination supplement is primarily used to address deficiencies in folate, vitamin B12 (as methylcobalamin), and vitamin B6 (as pyridoxal-5'-phosphate).  These deficiencies can contribute to various health issues, including:

* **Neural tube defects:**  During pregnancy, adequate folate is crucial for preventing neural tube defects.
* **Anemia:** Deficiencies in folate and B12 can cause megaloblastic anemia.
* **Cognitive function:**  All three vitamins are important for brain health and cognitive function.  Deficiencies can impact mood, memory, and concentration.
* **Cardiovascular health:**  Folate and B vitamins play a role in homocysteine metabolism.  Elevated homocysteine levels are linked to an increased risk of cardiovascular disease.
* **Depression and anxiety:**  Some studies suggest that deficiencies in these B vitamins may contribute to depression and anxiety.
* **Peripheral neuropathy:**  B12 deficiency is a common cause of peripheral neuropathy.


**Side Effects:** Generally well-tolerated, but potential side effects can include:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, diarrhea, bloating, or upset stomach. These are more common with higher doses.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Rare, but possible, including skin rash, itching, or swelling.
* **Sleep disturbances:**  In some individuals, higher doses might lead to sleep problems.
* **Headache:**  A less common side effect.


**How it Works:**

* **L-Methylfolate:** The active form of folate, it directly participates in various metabolic processes, including DNA synthesis, cell division, and the metabolism of homocysteine. It bypasses the need for enzymatic conversion, unlike folic acid.
* **Methylcobalamin:** The active form of vitamin B12, it's crucial for myelin production (protective sheath around nerves), DNA synthesis, and red blood cell formation.  It also plays a vital role in the metabolism of homocysteine.
* **Pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (P5P):** The active form of vitamin B6, it's a coenzyme involved in over 100 enzymatic reactions, including amino acid metabolism, neurotransmitter synthesis, and carbohydrate metabolism.  It also contributes to homocysteine metabolism.
