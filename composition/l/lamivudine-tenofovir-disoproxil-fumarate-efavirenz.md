# Lamivudine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Efavirenz
### Generic Name
Lamivudine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Efavirenz

**Note:** This combination is often referred to by its brand name, Atripla, or a similar name depending on the manufacturer.  This information applies to the generic combination of these three active ingredients.  Always refer to your prescription and consult with your doctor or pharmacist for specific details regarding your medication.

### Usage
This combination medication is a crucial treatment for Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection.  It's a type of antiretroviral therapy (ART) and works by suppressing the HIV virus, preventing it from replicating and damaging the immune system.  The primary use is to reduce the viral load (the amount of HIV in the blood) and improve the CD4 cell count (a type of white blood cell vital for a healthy immune system).  This, in turn, helps to slow the progression of HIV infection and prevent the development of Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS).  It's typically used as a first-line treatment for HIV in adults and is sometimes part of a regimen for adolescents.

### Dosage
Dosage is determined by a healthcare professional and is highly individualized. There's no universal dosage recommendation.  A typical adult dose involves taking one tablet orally once daily.

### Side Effects
Common side effects can include:

*   Nausea
*   Vomiting
*   Diarrhea
*   Headache
*   Fatigue
*   Rash
*   Dizziness
*   Sleep disturbances

Less common, but potentially serious, side effects include:

*   Liver problems (hepatotoxicity): This can manifest as jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), dark urine, or abdominal pain.
*   Mental health changes: Depression, anxiety, or suicidal thoughts.
*   Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas): This can cause severe abdominal pain.
*   Severe allergic reactions: Difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat.

**Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any serious side effects.**

### How it Works
This combination medication employs three different antiretroviral drugs, each targeting a specific stage of the HIV life cycle:

*   **Lamivudine:** This nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI) prevents HIV from making copies of its genetic material (RNA) into DNA.
*   **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate:**  Another NRTI, it works similarly to lamivudine by interfering with the HIV replication process.
*   **Efavirenz:** A non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI), it blocks reverse transcriptase, a viral enzyme crucial for HIV replication, through a different mechanism than NRTIs.

By combining these three drugs, the likelihood of HIV developing resistance to treatment is significantly reduced.

### Precautions
*   **Liver disease:**  This combination should be used cautiously in individuals with pre-existing liver problems. Regular liver function tests are necessary.
*   **Kidney disease:** Renal impairment can affect the elimination of tenofovir.  Dosage adjustments may be needed based on kidney function.
*   **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:** Consult your healthcare provider immediately; this medication may pose risks during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
*   **Drug interactions:** This combination can interact with other medications.  Inform your healthcare provider of all medications, herbal supplements, or vitamins you are taking.
*   **Mental health:** Individuals with a history of depression or other mental health issues should be monitored closely.
*   **Hypersensitivity:** Allergic reactions can occur; stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical help if an allergic reaction is suspected.


### FAQs

*   **Q: Can I stop taking this medication if I feel better?**  A: No, you should continue taking this medication as prescribed by your doctor, even if you feel better.  Stopping the medication prematurely can lead to the development of drug resistance and a worsening of your HIV infection.

*   **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store this medication at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.  Keep it out of reach of children.

*   **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed dose.

*   **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Moderate alcohol consumption is generally acceptable, but excessive alcohol use can affect liver function, which is important to consider while taking this medication. Consult your healthcare provider for advice specific to your situation.

*   **Q: Are there any long-term effects of taking this medication?** A: Long-term use of this medication can have potential side effects, such as bone loss, kidney problems, and lactic acidosis. Regular monitoring by your doctor is crucial to identify and manage any potential long-term effects.
