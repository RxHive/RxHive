# Etoricoxib

**Generic Name:** Etoricoxib

**Usage:** Etoricoxib is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve the signs and symptoms of osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and acute pain in adults.  It's also sometimes used to treat ankylosing spondylitis and other forms of inflammatory arthritis.  The specific dose and duration of treatment will depend on the condition being treated and the individual's response.  It's important to note that etoricoxib is *not* a pain reliever for headaches or migraines.

**Side Effects:** Like all medications, etoricoxib can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:**  Heartburn, nausea, abdominal pain, constipation, diarrhea, gas.  More serious gastrointestinal problems such as ulcers, bleeding, or perforation are less common but possible.
* **Cardiovascular:** Increased risk of heart attack, stroke, and blood clots (especially with higher doses or longer use).  Edema (swelling) may also occur.
* **Fluid Retention:**  Etoricoxib can cause fluid retention, which may lead to high blood pressure or swelling in the ankles and feet.
* **Allergic Reactions:** Skin rash, itching, hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema).  Severe allergic reactions are rare but can be life-threatening.
* **Other:** Headache, dizziness, insomnia, upper respiratory tract infections.

These are not all the possible side effects.  If you experience any unusual or concerning symptoms, consult your doctor or pharmacist immediately.


**How it Works:** Etoricoxib selectively inhibits the enzyme cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2).  COX-2 is involved in the production of prostaglandins, substances that cause pain, inflammation, and fever.  By inhibiting COX-2, etoricoxib reduces inflammation and pain, while having less impact on COX-1, an enzyme involved in protecting the stomach lining.  However, it's important to note that even selective COX-2 inhibitors can still cause gastrointestinal problems.
