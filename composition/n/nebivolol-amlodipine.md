# Nebivolol + Amlodipine

**Generic Name:** Nebivolol and Amlodipine

**Usage:**  This combination medication is used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension).  It's typically prescribed when a single medication hasn't been effective enough in controlling blood pressure. Nebivolol and amlodipine work through different mechanisms to lower blood pressure, providing a synergistic effect.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary from person to person and may include:

* **Common:** Headache, dizziness, fatigue, nausea, swelling in the ankles or feet (peripheral edema), palpitations.
* **Less Common:**  Slow heart rate (bradycardia), low blood pressure (hypotension), fainting (syncope), constipation, drowsiness, shortness of breath.
* **Rare but Serious:**  Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing), liver problems, heart block.

This is not an exhaustive list, and you should consult your doctor or pharmacist for complete information on potential side effects.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any serious side effects.


**How it Works:**

* **Nebivolol:** This is a beta-blocker. It works by blocking the effects of adrenaline (epinephrine) and noradrenaline (norepinephrine) on the heart and blood vessels. This slows the heart rate and reduces the force of the heart's contractions, leading to lower blood pressure. It also has vasodilating properties, helping to relax blood vessels.

* **Amlodipine:** This is a calcium channel blocker. It works by relaxing blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow through them. This reduces the workload on the heart and lowers blood pressure.

By combining these two medications, the overall effect on blood pressure is amplified and more effectively controlled than with either medication alone.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  It's generally recommended to limit or avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication, as alcohol can lower blood pressure further and increase the risk of side effects like dizziness and fainting.  Always consult your doctor.

* **Q: Can I stop taking this medication suddenly?** A: No.  Abruptly stopping this medication can lead to a rebound effect, causing a sudden spike in blood pressure.  Always follow your doctor's instructions regarding tapering off the medication.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one.

It's crucial to inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs.
