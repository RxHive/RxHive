# Dexketoprofen + Paracetamol
### Generic Name
Dexketoprofen + Paracetamol

### Usage

Dexketoprofen + Paracetamol is a combination medication used to relieve moderate to severe pain and reduce fever.  It's often prescribed for conditions causing acute pain such as:

* **Musculoskeletal pain:** This includes pain from sprains, strains, back pain, arthritis flare-ups, and other musculoskeletal injuries.
* **Post-surgical pain:** It can help manage pain after certain surgical procedures.
* **Dental pain:**  Effective in relieving toothaches and other oral pain.
* **Menstrual pain (dysmenorrhea):** Can provide relief from the cramping and pain associated with menstruation.
* **Headache:** While not a primary treatment for all headaches, it may offer relief from certain types of headaches when pain is moderate to severe.

It's important to note that this combination is for short-term pain relief and fever reduction.  It's not intended for long-term use or for chronic pain management.  The individual components, dexketoprofen and paracetamol, work synergistically to provide more potent pain relief than either drug would alone.


### Dosage

Dosage varies depending on the specific formulation (tablet, capsule, etc.), the patient's age, weight, and the severity of their condition.  **Always follow the instructions provided by your doctor or pharmacist.**  The information below is for general guidance only and should not be considered a substitute for professional medical advice.

**Adults (oral):**  Typical dosages range from one to two tablets or capsules every 8 hours as needed for pain and fever relief.  Never exceed the maximum daily dose recommended on the packaging or by your doctor.

**Children (oral):** This combination medication is generally not recommended for children. Paracetamol is often used alone in children for fever and pain, and the dosage must be carefully calculated based on the child's weight and age.  **Never give this medication to a child without explicit instruction from a doctor.**

**IV Administration:** This particular combination is not typically available in an intravenous (IV) formulation. Dexketoprofen may be administered IV in some cases, but only under strict medical supervision. Paracetamol is also available in an IV formulation, but usually separately, not as part of this combination product.

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those listed as serious, stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical advice.


### How it Works

This combination drug contains two pain relievers with different mechanisms of action:

* **Dexketoprofen:** This is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that works by reducing the production of prostaglandins, substances in the body that cause inflammation, pain, and fever.
* **Paracetamol (Acetaminophen):**  The exact mechanism of paracetamol is not fully understood, but it's believed to act centrally in the brain to reduce pain and fever.


By combining these two drugs, a broader and more effective pain-relieving effect is achieved than with either drug alone.


### Precautions

* **Allergies:** Do not use this medication if you are allergic to dexketoprofen, paracetamol, or any other NSAIDs.
* **Pregnant or breastfeeding women:** Consult your doctor before using this medication during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.
* **Liver or kidney disease:** Use with caution if you have liver or kidney problems.
* **Elderly patients:** Older adults may be more susceptible to side effects.
* **Alcohol consumption:** Avoid alcohol consumption while taking this medication, as it can increase the risk of stomach bleeding and other side effects.
* **Other medications:** Inform your doctor or pharmacist about all other medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements, as interactions are possible.
* **Aspirin or other NSAIDs:** Avoid taking this medication in combination with aspirin or other NSAIDs.


### FAQs

* **Q: How long can I take this medication?**  A: This medication is intended for short-term use only. Do not take it for longer than recommended by your doctor.

* **Q: Can I take this medication with food?** A: Taking it with food may help reduce stomach upset.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose. Do not double the dose.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store it in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and out of the reach of children.

* **Q:  Can I take more than the recommended dose to get faster relief?** A: No, exceeding the recommended dose significantly increases the risk of serious side effects, particularly liver damage with paracetamol.  Always follow dosage instructions.
