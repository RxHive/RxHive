# Zinc acetate

**Generic Name:** Zinc Acetate

**Usage:**

Zinc acetate is primarily used for:

* **Treatment of zinc deficiency:** This is a common use, particularly in individuals with malabsorption syndromes or those on certain medications that deplete zinc.  It's often prescribed as a supplement.
* **Treatment of diarrhea (some formulations):** Certain formulations of zinc acetate are used to treat acute diarrhea, particularly in children.  The mechanism isn't fully understood, but it's believed to reduce the duration and severity of the illness.
* **Treatment of acrodermatitis enteropathica (rare):** This is a rare genetic disorder characterized by severe zinc deficiency. Zinc acetate is a crucial part of treatment.
* **Topical use for skin conditions (less common):**  Some topical formulations containing zinc acetate are used to treat mild skin irritations or acne, though this is less common than oral use.

**Side Effects:**

Side effects of zinc acetate are generally mild but can include:

* **Gastrointestinal upset:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal cramps are the most commonly reported side effects, especially with higher doses.
* **Headache:** Less common but can occur.
* **Metallic taste:** A common side effect, often described as a bitter or unpleasant taste in the mouth.
* **Stomach pain:**  Can range from mild discomfort to more significant pain.
