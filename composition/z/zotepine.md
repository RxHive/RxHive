# Zotepine

**Generic Name:** Zotepine

**Usage:** Zotepine is an atypical antipsychotic medication primarily used to treat schizophrenia and acute manic episodes associated with bipolar disorder.  It may also be used off-label for other conditions, but this should only be done under the strict supervision of a psychiatrist.

**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, zotepine can cause side effects.  These can vary in severity from person to person and include:

* **Common side effects:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, weight gain, constipation, dry mouth, blurred vision, increased appetite, sedation.
* **Less common but serious side effects:**  Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) – a rare but potentially life-threatening condition characterized by muscle rigidity, fever, altered mental status, and autonomic instability;  tardive dyskinesia (TD) – involuntary, repetitive movements, particularly of the face, mouth, and tongue;  extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) – muscle stiffness, tremors, restlessness;  prolonged QT interval (can lead to potentially fatal heart rhythm abnormalities); orthostatic hypotension (a sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing);  blood disorders; liver problems;  hyperglycemia and diabetes.

**How it Works:** Zotepine is believed to work primarily by blocking dopamine and serotonin receptors in the brain.  Dopamine is a neurotransmitter associated with psychosis, while serotonin plays a role in mood regulation. By blocking these receptors, zotepine helps to reduce the positive symptoms of schizophrenia (hallucinations, delusions) and the manic symptoms of bipolar disorder. The exact mechanism isn't fully understood, but it's thought that its impact on multiple neurotransmitter systems contributes to its efficacy.
