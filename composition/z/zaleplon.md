# Zaleplon

**Generic Name:** Zaleplon

**Usage:**

Zaleplon is a short-acting nonbenzodiazepine hypnotic used for the short-term treatment of insomnia characterized by difficulty falling asleep (sleep onset insomnia).  It's typically prescribed for a period of only a few days to a couple of weeks, not for long-term use.  It's generally taken right before bedtime.  The dosage is usually 5-10 mg, depending on the individual and physician's recommendation.  It is not recommended for use in patients with a history of sleep apnea.

* **Sleepwalking and other sleep-related behaviors:** These behaviors have been reported, especially at higher doses or if other medications are being taken concurrently.
* **Dependence/Withdrawal:** Although less likely than with benzodiazepines, long-term use can lead to dependence and withdrawal symptoms upon cessation.
