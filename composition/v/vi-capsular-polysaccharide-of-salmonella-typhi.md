# Vi Capsular Polysaccharide Of Salmonella Typhi

**Generic Name:** Vi Capsular Polysaccharide of *Salmonella Typhi* (Vi CPS)

**Usage:** Vi CPS is used as a vaccine to prevent typhoid fever, caused by *Salmonella Typhi*.  It's typically administered as an intramuscular injection.  Note that different formulations may exist with varying administration routes and age ranges.  Always consult the product label and healthcare provider for specific usage instructions.

**Side Effects:**  Like all vaccines, Vi CPS can cause side effects. These are usually mild and temporary and may include:

* Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site
* Headache
* Fever
* Muscle aches
* Fatigue
* Nausea

Serious side effects are rare but possible.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

* Severe allergic reaction (e.g., difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* High fever
* Fainting


**How it Works:** The Vi CPS vaccine works by stimulating the body's immune system to produce antibodies against the Vi capsular polysaccharide, a surface antigen of *Salmonella Typhi*. These antibodies help protect against infection by *Salmonella Typhi* bacteria.  The vaccine primarily confers humoral (antibody-mediated) immunity. It doesn't provide complete protection, and the duration of immunity varies.
