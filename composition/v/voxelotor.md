# Voxelotor
### Generic Name
Voxelotor

### Usage
Voxelotor is a medication used to treat sickle cell disease in adults and children aged 12 years and older.  Its primary use is to reduce the frequency and severity of sickle cell crises by modifying the properties of hemoglobin S (HbS), the abnormal hemoglobin responsible for the disease's characteristic complications.  While not a cure, Voxelotor helps improve the function of red blood cells, making them more flexible and less prone to sickling, thus alleviating symptoms and reducing the risk of painful episodes, organ damage, and other complications associated with sickle cell disease.

### Dosage

**Adults (18 years and older):** The usual dose is 1500 mg taken orally once daily.  This can be taken with or without food.

**Children (12 years and older):** The dosage is the same as for adults: 1500 mg orally once daily.  

**Dosage Adjustments:**

* **Hepatic Impairment:**  For patients with mild to moderate liver impairment, no dosage adjustment is necessary.  However, for those with severe liver impairment, the dose should be reduced to 1000 mg once daily.
* **Renal Impairment:**  No dosage adjustment is typically needed for patients with mild to severe kidney impairment, including those with end-stage renal disease requiring dialysis. However, close monitoring is advisable.

**Important Note:** Voxelotor is not approved for use in children under 12 years of age.


### Side Effects

Common side effects (occurring in more than 10% of patients) include:

* Headache
* Fatigue
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Nausea
* Skin rash
* Fever

Less common, but potentially serious side effects include:

* Pulmonary embolism (blood clot in the lung)
* Hypersensitivity reactions (ranging from mild rash to severe reactions requiring immediate medical attention)

If you experience any adverse effects, particularly those that are severe or persistent, contact your doctor immediately.

### How it Works

Voxelotor works by inhibiting the polymerization of hemoglobin S (HbS).  HbS is the abnormal form of hemoglobin found in people with sickle cell disease.  Under low-oxygen conditions, HbS molecules clump together, causing red blood cells to become rigid and sickle-shaped. This leads to blockages in blood vessels, resulting in the painful crises and organ damage characteristic of sickle cell disease.  Voxelotor binds to HbS, increasing its affinity for oxygen. This prevents HbS polymerization, keeping the red blood cells flexible and improving blood flow.

### Precautions

* **Hypersensitivity:** Voxelotor is contraindicated in individuals with a history of serious hypersensitivity reactions to the drug or its components.
* **Drug Interactions:** Voxelotor's metabolism can be affected by other medications.  Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole, ritonavir) may increase Voxelotor levels, potentially increasing the risk of side effects. Conversely, strong or moderate CYP3A4 inducers (e.g., rifampin, St. John's Wort) may decrease Voxelotor levels, reducing its effectiveness.  Discuss all medications you are taking with your doctor.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** The use of Voxelotor during pregnancy should be carefully considered, balancing the potential benefits against the potential risks to the developing fetus.  Breastfeeding is not recommended while taking Voxelotor and for at least two weeks after the last dose.


### FAQs

**Q: Can I take Voxelotor with other medications?**

A:  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking before starting Voxelotor.  Some medications may interact with Voxelotor, affecting its efficacy or safety.

**Q: How should I store Voxelotor?**

A:  Store Voxelotor at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.

**Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?**

A:  If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.  Continue with your regular schedule.

**Q: Is Voxelotor a cure for sickle cell disease?**

A: No, Voxelotor is not a cure but a treatment that helps manage the disease by reducing the frequency and severity of sickle cell crises.

**Q:  Are there any long-term effects associated with Voxelotor?**

A:  Long-term studies are ongoing to fully evaluate the long-term effects of Voxelotor.  Regular monitoring by your healthcare provider is essential.
