# Voglibose

**Generic Name:** Voglibose

**Usage:**

Voglibose is an alpha-glucosidase inhibitor used to treat type 2 diabetes mellitus.  It's typically used in conjunction with diet and exercise to improve blood sugar control.  It's most effective when taken with meals containing carbohydrates.  It does *not* work for type 1 diabetes.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of voglibose include:

* **Gastrointestinal problems:** These are the most frequent side effects and can include diarrhea, abdominal pain, flatulence (gas), and borborygmi (loud rumbling or gurgling noises in the abdomen).  These are often mild but can be significant enough to cause discontinuation of the medication in some individuals.
* **Increased risk of hypoglycemia:** While less common when used alone, the risk of low blood sugar increases if voglibose is combined with other diabetes medications, especially insulin or sulfonylureas.
* **Liver problems (rare):**  Although rare, liver enzyme abnormalities have been reported.
* **Allergic reactions:**  These are also rare but can include rash, itching, or swelling.

Less common or rare side effects can also occur. Consult a doctor or pharmacist for a complete list.

**How it Works:**

Voglibose works by slowing down the digestion and absorption of carbohydrates in the small intestine.  It inhibits alpha-glucosidase enzymes, which are responsible for breaking down complex carbohydrates into simpler sugars (glucose). By slowing this process, the rise in blood glucose after a meal is reduced, leading to better blood sugar control.
