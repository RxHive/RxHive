* **Nerve function:** Thiamine plays a vital role in nerve function and health.

**Side Effects:** Generally, both Vitamin C and Thiamine are well-tolerated at recommended doses. However, potential side effects can include:

* **Thiamine:**  Rare side effects are possible but generally mild, such as allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), nausea, and vomiting.


**How it Works:**

* **Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid):** Acts as a potent antioxidant, scavenging free radicals that can damage cells. It's also a cofactor in several enzymatic reactions, including collagen synthesis.
* **Thiamine (Vitamin B1):**  Plays a vital role as a coenzyme in carbohydrate metabolism, particularly in the conversion of pyruvate to acetyl-CoA, a crucial step in energy production. It's also important for nerve function and the synthesis of neurotransmitters.
