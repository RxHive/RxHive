# Vitamin C + Zinc + Selenium dioxide

**Generic Name:**  Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid) + Zinc (Zinc Acetate, Zinc Gluconate, Zinc Citrate, etc.) + Selenium Dioxide (or Selenomethionine)


**Usage:**  This combination is often used as a supplement to support immune function.  Each component plays a distinct role:

* **Vitamin C:**  An antioxidant that supports immune cell function and collagen production.  It's involved in numerous metabolic processes.
* **Zinc:**  Essential for immune cell development and function.  It plays a crucial role in cell growth and division.
* **Selenium:**  A trace mineral that acts as an antioxidant, protecting cells from damage. It's a component of several antioxidant enzymes.

The combination is frequently taken to address immune deficiencies, support recovery from illness, and potentially mitigate the severity of infections (although scientific evidence for this last point with respect to *preventing* or *significantly reducing* illness severity is still developing).  The exact usage may vary depending on the specific formulation and intended purpose.  It's crucial to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.


**Side Effects:**  Side effects are generally mild but can include:

* **Vitamin C:** Diarrhea, nausea, stomach cramps, heartburn (especially at high doses).  In some individuals, large doses can interfere with certain medications.
* **Zinc:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, loss of appetite. Long-term high doses can cause copper deficiency and other problems.
* **Selenium:** Hair loss, brittle nails, fatigue, skin rash, garlic breath (from the dioxide form).  Very high doses can be toxic.


**How it Works:**

The three components work synergistically (though not perfectly understood in every way) to bolster the immune system:

* **Vitamin C:** enhances the function of immune cells (lymphocytes, neutrophils, etc.), improves antibody production, and possesses direct antioxidant properties.
* **Zinc:** is crucial for the development and function of numerous immune cells, including T cells and natural killer cells. It also supports the activity of several immune-related enzymes.
* **Selenium:** functions as an antioxidant, protecting cells from free radical damage and supporting the activity of glutathione peroxidase, an essential enzyme for protecting against oxidative stress.  Selenium is also involved in immune cell regulation.
