**Usage:** This combination of vitamins is often used to support overall health and well-being.  Each vitamin plays a unique role:

* **Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid):**  A powerful antioxidant, supporting immune function, collagen synthesis (important for skin, bones, and connective tissue), and iron absorption.
* **Thiamine (Vitamin B1):** Essential for carbohydrate metabolism, nerve function, and muscle function. Deficiencies can lead to beriberi.
* **Folic Acid (Vitamin B9):** Crucial for cell growth and development, especially important during pregnancy to prevent neural tube defects.  Also involved in DNA synthesis and red blood cell formation.
* **Niacinamide (Vitamin B3):**  Supports DNA repair, cell signaling, and energy metabolism.  It plays a role in skin health and can help reduce redness and inflammation.


**Side Effects:**  Generally, these vitamins are considered safe when taken at recommended dosages. However, excessive intake can lead to side effects:

* **Vitamin C:**  High doses can cause nausea, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and kidney stones in susceptible individuals.
* **Thiamine:**  Generally well-tolerated, but high doses may cause nausea and vomiting.
* **Folic Acid:**  While rare, very high doses can mask the symptoms of vitamin B12 deficiency.
* **Niacinamide:**  High doses can cause flushing (redness of the skin), itching, and nausea.
