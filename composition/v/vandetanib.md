# Vandetanib

**Generic Name:** Vandetanib

**Usage:**

Vandetanib is an oral medication used to treat certain types of advanced or metastatic thyroid cancer, specifically medullary thyroid cancer (MTC) that has progressed after other treatments, and  recurrent or progressive differentiated thyroid cancer (DTC) that is refractory to radioactive iodine.  It is also sometimes used off-label in other types of cancer, but this should only be done under the strict guidance and supervision of an oncologist.

**Side Effects:**

Vandetanib can cause a range of side effects, some common and some serious.  Common side effects (affecting more than 10% of patients) may include:

* **Diarrhea:** Often severe and requiring medication to manage.
* **Nausea:** Can be accompanied by vomiting.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling unusually tired.
* **Rash:** Skin irritation or redness.
* **Hair thinning or loss (alopecia):**  Temporary hair loss.
* **High blood pressure (hypertension):** Requires monitoring and management.
* **Increased liver enzymes:**  Indicates potential liver damage and requires monitoring.


Serious side effects (though less common) can include:

* **Severe diarrhea:**  Leading to dehydration.
* **Bleeding:**  From the nose, gums, or other areas.
* **Heart problems:**  Such as irregular heartbeat or heart failure.
* **Blood clots:**  Increased risk of blood clots in the legs or lungs.
* **Severe skin reactions:**  Including Stevens-Johnson syndrome.
* **Liver damage:**  Including hepatitis.
* **Interstitial lung disease:**  Inflammation and scarring of the lungs.
* **Proteinuria:**  Presence of excessive protein in the urine.
* **QT prolongation:** An alteration of the heart's electrical activity.
