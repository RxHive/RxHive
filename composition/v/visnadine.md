### How it Works

Visnadine primarily works by inhibiting the influx of calcium ions (Ca²⁺) into cells through L-type calcium channels.  These channels play a crucial role in the contraction of smooth muscle cells in blood vessel walls. By blocking calcium entry, Visnadine causes relaxation of these smooth muscles, leading to vasodilation (widening of blood vessels).  This improved blood flow is the mechanism by which it addresses the symptoms of certain cardiovascular conditions.  Its mechanism of action in other applications, such as skin care or treatment of female sexual dysfunction, is less well understood and requires further research.
