# Virginiamycin
### Generic Name
Virginiamycin

### Usage
Virginiamycin is a combination antibiotic belonging to the streptogramin class.  It consists primarily of two components: Virginiamycin M1 and Virginiamycin S1.  Historically, it was used to treat infections caused by susceptible bacteria, mainly Gram-positive cocci (round bacteria).  However, it's important to note that Virginiamycin is largely discontinued for human use in most countries and is primarily utilized in veterinary medicine for treating animal infections.  Its use in human medicine is now very limited or non-existent.

### How it Works
Virginiamycin works by inhibiting bacterial protein synthesis.  Specifically, Virginiamycin M1 binds to the 50S ribosomal subunit of bacterial ribosomes. This prevents the addition of amino acids to the growing polypeptide chain, halting protein synthesis and ultimately killing the bacteria.  Virginiamycin S1 enhances the activity of Virginiamycin M1, making the combined effect more potent against bacterial growth.
