**Valdecoxib (Generic Name):**

* **Usage:**  Valdecoxib is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that selectively inhibits COX-2. It was primarily used to reduce pain and inflammation associated with osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and acute pain.  However, due to cardiovascular risks, it's largely been withdrawn from many markets.
* **Side Effects:**  Cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke), gastrointestinal issues (abdominal pain, ulcers, bleeding), increased risk of kidney problems, fluid retention, and allergic reactions.
* **How it Works:**  Valdecoxib selectively inhibits cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), an enzyme involved in the production of prostaglandins, which contribute to pain, inflammation, and fever.  By blocking COX-2, it reduces these symptoms without significantly affecting COX-1, which plays a role in protecting the stomach lining.  However, this selectivity is not absolute.

* **Side Effects:**  Drowsiness, dizziness, dry mouth, low blood pressure, weakness, nausea, and liver problems in rare cases.
* **How it Works:** Tizanidine works in the central nervous system to reduce muscle spasms by affecting the transmission of nerve impulses.  The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it involves interactions with alpha-2 adrenergic receptors in the spinal cord.
