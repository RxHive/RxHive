# Veralipride
### Generic Name
Veralipride

### Usage
Veralipride is a substituted benzamide derivative primarily used to manage cardiovascular and psychological symptoms associated with menopause.  These symptoms can include hot flashes, mood swings, and other menopausal discomforts.  However, it's crucial to note that Veralipride's marketing authorizations have been withdrawn in many countries due to concerns about its risk-benefit profile. Its continued use is largely restricted to specific regions, like Mexico, where guidelines still permit its use for controlling vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes) in menopause.  It's not currently widely used in most parts of the world.

### Dosage
**Adult Dosage (Cardiovascular and Psychological Symptoms of Menopause):** The typical oral dosage is 100 mg daily for 20 days, repeated at 7-10 day intervals.  This is based on previously available information and may not reflect current clinical practice due to the withdrawal of the drug in many countries.

**Pediatric Dosage:**  The safety and efficacy of Veralipride in children have not been established.  It should not be used in children.

### Side Effects
The following side effects have been reported, though the frequency is not clearly defined in available data.  Remember, this is not an exhaustive list:

* Galactorrhea (abnormal milk production)
* Weight gain
* Bulimia nervosa
* Epigastric pain (upper abdominal pain)
* Heartburn
* Somnolence (sleepiness)
* Tardive dyskinesia (a serious movement disorder)


It is crucial to contact a healthcare professional immediately if you experience any unusual or concerning side effects.

### How it Works
Veralipride is an antipsychotic that works by blocking dopamine D2 receptors in the brain.  While the exact mechanism of its action in managing menopausal symptoms isn't fully elucidated, its dopamine-blocking effects likely play a role in modulating neurotransmitter activity related to the regulation of body temperature and mood.  The reduction in dopamine activity may contribute to the relief of hot flashes and some psychological symptoms.

### Precautions
**Contraindications:** Veralipride is contraindicated (should not be used) in individuals with:

* Hypersensitivity (allergy) to veralipride or related compounds.
* Prolactinoma (a type of pituitary tumor).
* Porphyria (a group of genetic disorders).
* Conditions that put them at increased risk of hypertensive episodes (high blood pressure).

**Warnings:** Use with caution in patients with Parkinson's disease.

**Drug Interactions:** Concomitant use with selegiline should be avoided.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all other medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking to avoid potential interactions.

**Pregnancy and Lactation:** The safety of veralipride during pregnancy is unknown.  Excretion in breast milk is also unknown, therefore caution is advised.  It should only be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding after careful assessment of the potential benefits versus risks by a healthcare professional.
