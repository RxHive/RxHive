# Tenidap
### Generic Name
Tenidap

### Usage
Tenidap was a drug candidate primarily investigated for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis.  Its mechanism involved inhibiting the production of inflammatory mediators, offering potential relief from the symptoms of this autoimmune disease.  While it showed promise in pre-clinical and some clinical trials, it never reached widespread market availability due to concerns regarding liver and kidney toxicity.  It is important to note that Tenidap is not currently marketed.

### How it Works
Tenidap functioned as a dual inhibitor of cyclooxygenase (COX) and 5-lipoxygenase (5-LOX) enzymes.  These enzymes are involved in the production of inflammatory molecules like prostaglandins and leukotrienes, respectively.  By blocking these enzymes, Tenidap aimed to reduce inflammation and alleviate the symptoms associated with rheumatoid arthritis. It also showed potential effects on cytokine production, impacting inflammatory signaling pathways.  The specific mechanisms of its action are partially understood, and the interaction with cytokine release and synthesis pathways was only partially elucidated during its research and development phase.
