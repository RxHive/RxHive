**Side Effects:**

**Paracetamol:** Liver damage (especially with overdose), allergic reactions (rash, itching), nausea.

**Lornoxicam:**  Gastrointestinal upset (heartburn, nausea, abdominal pain, ulcers, bleeding), dizziness, headache, drowsiness, rash, fluid retention, increased risk of cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke) in some individuals.

**Combined Side Effects:** The combination increases the risk of experiencing side effects, especially those listed above. The combined use increases the risk of drowsiness, gastrointestinal problems, and liver toxicity.


**How it Works:**

* **Tramadol:** Acts on opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord to reduce the perception of pain.  It also affects the reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine, further modulating pain signals.
* **Paracetamol:**  The exact mechanism isn't fully understood, but it's thought to inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes in the central nervous system, reducing pain and fever.
* **Lornoxicam:**  Inhibits COX enzymes, reducing inflammation, pain, and fever. It is thought to preferentially inhibit COX-2, a COX enzyme involved in inflammation, potentially reducing the risk of some gastrointestinal side effects compared to older NSAIDs, though this risk still exists.
