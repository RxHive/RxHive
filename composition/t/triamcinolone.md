# Triamcinolone

**Generic Name:** Triamcinolone

**Usage:** Triamcinolone is a corticosteroid used to reduce inflammation and suppress the immune system.  Its uses vary depending on the formulation (cream, ointment, injection, tablet, etc.):

* **Topical (creams, ointments, lotions):** Treats inflammatory skin conditions like eczema, psoriasis, allergic reactions, and contact dermatitis.
* **Injections (intra-articular, intramuscular, intralesional):** Treats inflammatory conditions affecting joints (arthritis), bursae (bursitis), tendons (tendinitis), and other soft tissues.  It's also used in certain skin conditions and for allergic reactions.
* **Oral (tablets, syrup):**  Treats severe allergic reactions, inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), certain types of arthritis, and other inflammatory conditions.  It's less commonly used orally due to significant systemic side effects.
* **Inhalation (aerosol):** Treats asthma and other lung conditions by reducing inflammation in the airways.
* **Ophthalmic (eye drops):** Treats eye inflammation.

**Side Effects:** Side effects vary considerably depending on the route of administration and dosage.  Common side effects include:

* **Topical:** Skin thinning, burning, itching, dryness, skin irritation, acne, striae (stretch marks), discoloration.
* **Injections:** Pain, swelling, infection at injection site.
* **Oral:**  Weight gain, increased appetite, fluid retention, high blood pressure, increased blood sugar, thinning of the bones (osteoporosis), cataracts, glaucoma, mood changes, increased risk of infection, stomach upset.
* **Inhalation:**  Hoarseness, thrush (oral yeast infection), cough.
* **Ophthalmic:** Eye irritation, burning, stinging, increased eye pressure.

**More severe side effects (requiring immediate medical attention) are possible with all routes of administration and include:**

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, or tongue)
* Worsening of existing infections
* Signs of Cushing's syndrome (moon face, weight gain, muscle weakness)
* Osteoporosis (bone thinning)


**How it Works:** Triamcinolone is a synthetic glucocorticoid (a type of steroid hormone). It works by binding to receptors inside cells, affecting gene expression. This leads to decreased inflammation by:

* Reducing the production of inflammatory substances (like cytokines).
* Stabilizing cell membranes.
* Suppressing the immune system's activity.
