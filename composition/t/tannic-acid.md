# Tannic Acid
### Generic Name
Tannic Acid

### Usage
Tannic acid is primarily used as an astringent, meaning it constricts tissues and reduces secretions.  Historically, it's been applied topically to mucous membranes of the mouth and throat for conditions like mild mouth sores or throat irritation.  It has also been used in suppositories to treat hemorrhoids.  It's an ingredient in some dermatological preparations.  **However, it's crucial to note that many past uses of tannic acid, such as treating burns and inclusion in barium sulfate enemas, have been discontinued due to its association with liver toxicity.**  It is no longer recommended for these uses.

### Dosage
Dosage of tannic acid varies greatly depending on the specific formulation and intended use.  There is no standard, universally recommended dosage as its applications are largely historical and its use is now limited.  Any use of tannic acid should be under strict medical supervision, with the dosage determined by a healthcare professional based on the individual's condition and needs.  Different formulations (e.g., mouthwash, suppository, topical cream) will have their own specific instructions.  **Never use tannic acid without consulting a doctor.**

### Side Effects
* **Common side effects:**  Mild irritation at the application site (e.g., mouth, skin).
* **Serious (but less common) side effects:** Liver toxicity (potentially fatal).  This is a significant concern, and why the use of tannic acid has been largely discontinued for many past applications.
* **Other potential side effects:**  Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (if ingested).  Allergic reactions are possible.

**If you experience any adverse effects, stop using tannic acid immediately and consult a healthcare provider.**

### How it Works
Tannic acid's astringent action stems from its ability to precipitate proteins. This causes the constriction of tissues, leading to reduced secretions and a decrease in inflammation in affected areas.  The formation of a protective layer on the skin or mucous membranes is also thought to contribute to its action. While some antibacterial and antiviral effects have been suggested, further research is needed to confirm these properties.

### Precautions
* **Contraindications:** Tannic acid is contraindicated for individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to it or related compounds.  Its use is generally discouraged due to the risk of liver toxicity.
* **Drug Interactions:**  Interactions with other medications are possible, though not well-documented due to its limited current use.  It is essential to inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you are taking before using tannic acid (if prescribed).
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of tannic acid during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not established.  Avoid use unless specifically directed by a healthcare professional.
* **Children and Older Adults:**  Use in these populations is generally discouraged due to potential increased sensitivity and the risk of serious side effects.
