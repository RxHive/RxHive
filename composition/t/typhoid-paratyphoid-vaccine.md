# Typhoid-Paratyphoid Vaccine
### Generic Name
Typhoid-Paratyphoid Vaccine (Bivalent)


### Usage

This vaccine is designed to actively immunize against typhoid fever (caused by *Salmonella Typhi*) and paratyphoid fever (caused by *Salmonella Paratyphi* A and B).  It's important to note that while this bivalent vaccine was once used, it has largely been replaced by monovalent typhoid vaccines (targeting only *Salmonella Typhi*) because the paratyphoid component proved to be less effective. Currently, there isn't a widely available and effective vaccine specifically for paratyphoid.  Therefore, the use of this bivalent vaccine is limited and may not be readily accessible.  If protection against paratyphoid is required, alternative preventative measures like hygiene and sanitation should be prioritized.

### Side Effects

* **Common:**  Pain, swelling, or redness at the injection site.  Mild fever, headache, and fatigue are also possible.  These are generally mild and temporary.

* **Less Common but Serious:**  Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) are rare but possible.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience symptoms such as difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat, or a rapid heartbeat.


### How it Works

The typhoid-paratyphoid vaccine is a killed-bacteria vaccine.  This means it contains inactive *Salmonella Typhi* and *Salmonella Paratyphi* bacteria.  When injected, the vaccine stimulates the immune system to produce antibodies against these bacteria.  These antibodies provide active immunity, meaning the body is better prepared to fight off infection if exposed to live *Salmonella* bacteria in the future.  The mechanism involves the immune system recognizing the bacterial antigens and creating a specific immune response to neutralize them.

### Precautions

* **Allergies:**  Individuals with a known allergy to any component of the vaccine should not receive it.  Discuss any allergies with your doctor before vaccination.

* **Pregnant or Breastfeeding Women:**  The use of this vaccine during pregnancy or breastfeeding should be discussed with a healthcare provider to weigh the risks and benefits.  Currently available typhoid vaccines often have different guidelines for this population.

* **Immunocompromised Individuals:**  People with weakened immune systems may have a reduced response to the vaccine. Consult a healthcare provider for specific advice.

* **Interactions with other medications:**  While generally not expected, inform your doctor of all medications you are currently taking.
