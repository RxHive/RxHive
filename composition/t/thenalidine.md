# Thenalidine
### Generic Name
Thenalidine

### Usage
Thenalidine was primarily used as an antihistamine with anticholinergic properties. Its main application was in treating itching (pruritus) associated with various skin conditions.  While it showed some effectiveness in relieving itching, its use was limited due to significant safety concerns.  It is no longer available in many countries, including Canada, the US, and the UK.

### Dosage
Because Thenalidine is no longer commercially available, specific dosage information is not readily accessible through standard medical channels. Any historical dosage information should not be used for self-medication as Thenalidine's use carries significant risk.

### Side Effects
Thenalidine's use was associated with several side effects, some of which were serious.  These included:

* **Common:** Drowsiness, dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation.
* **Serious (but less common):**  Neutropenia (a decrease in white blood cells, increasing the risk of infection), agranulocytosis (a severe deficiency of granulocytes, a type of white blood cell), blood dyscrasias (abnormalities in the blood), liver damage.


**If you experience any adverse effects while taking Thenalidine (highly unlikely given its withdrawal from the market), seek immediate medical attention.**  This information is provided for historical context only and should not be construed as an endorsement for its use.

### How it Works
Thenalidine functions as an H1-receptor antagonist.  This means it blocks the action of histamine, a substance released by the body in response to allergic reactions or injury. By blocking histamine, it reduces the itching sensation associated with histamine release.  Its anticholinergic properties further contributed to its antipruritic effects, though the precise mechanism of this action within the context of its use is not fully elucidated in readily available medical literature.

### Precautions
Given Thenalidine's withdrawal from the market due to serious side effects, there are significant precautions to consider, all of which highlight the risk associated with its use and why it's no longer available.  These include:

* **Contraindications:**  Thenalidine should not be used by individuals with known hypersensitivity or allergic reactions to the drug.  Its use in the presence of any existing blood disorders or liver impairment was also contraindicated.
* **Drug Interactions:** Interactions with other medications were a significant concern, though specific details are not consistently available in modern medical resources due to its removal from the market.
* **Specific Warnings:** The risk of neutropenia and other serious blood disorders makes Thenalidine highly unsafe for use in pregnant women, children, and elderly individuals.  Its use in any population carries significant health risks.
