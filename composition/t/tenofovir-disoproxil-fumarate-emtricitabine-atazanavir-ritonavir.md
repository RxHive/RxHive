# Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Emtricitabine + Atazanavir + Ritonavir

**Generic Name:** Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate/emtricitabine/atazanavir/ritonavir (Note: This is a combination, not a single generic name)

**Usage:**  This combination of antiretroviral medications is used to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus type 1 (HIV-1) infection in adults.  It's specifically designed for individuals who haven't received prior HIV treatment or who have experienced virologic failure on a previous regimen and require a change.  The precise composition and dosage may be tailored to individual patient needs by their physician.  **It is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.** This information should not be used as a self-treatment guide.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary significantly among individuals, but some common ones include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, constipation.
* **Nervous System:** Headache, dizziness, insomnia, peripheral neuropathy (numbness or tingling in the extremities).
* **Skin:** Rash, itching.
* **Metabolic:** Increased cholesterol and triglycerides (lipids), changes in body fat distribution (lipodystrophy), hyperglycemia (high blood sugar), lactic acidosis (a rare but serious condition).
* **Hepatic (Liver):** Elevated liver enzymes (ALT, AST).
* **Renal (Kidney):**  Reduced kidney function (especially with tenofovir).
* **Other:**  Fatigue, weakness, muscle pain.

**This is not an exhaustive list.**  Serious side effects are possible, including pancreatitis, hypersensitivity reactions, and opportunistic infections.  It's vital to report any unusual or concerning symptoms to your healthcare provider immediately.

**How it Works:**

This drug combination works by targeting different stages of the HIV life cycle:

* **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF):** A nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). It interferes with the HIV reverse transcriptase enzyme, preventing the virus from converting its RNA into DNA, a necessary step for viral replication.

* **Emtricitabine (FTC):** Another NRTI, similar in mechanism to TDF. It also inhibits reverse transcriptase, further suppressing viral replication.

* **Atazanavir (ATV):** A protease inhibitor (PI).  It blocks the HIV protease enzyme, which is essential for the virus to assemble new infectious viral particles.

* **Ritonavir (RTV):** A boosting agent. It inhibits the metabolism of atazanavir, increasing its blood levels and effectiveness.

By combining these four drugs, the regimen creates a powerful multi-pronged attack against the virus, significantly reducing the viral load and improving immune function.
