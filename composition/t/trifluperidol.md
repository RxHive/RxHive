# Trifluperidol

### How it Works
Trifluperidol's mechanism of action involves primarily blocking dopamine D2 receptors in the brain.  Dopamine is a neurotransmitter involved in regulating mood, movement, and thought processes. By blocking dopamine receptors, trifluperidol reduces the excessive dopamine activity believed to contribute to the symptoms of psychoses like schizophrenia.  It also exhibits an affinity for sigma receptors, although the clinical significance of this remains to be fully understood.
