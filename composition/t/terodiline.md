# Terodiline
### Generic Name
Terodiline

### Usage
Terodiline was previously used to alleviate urinary incontinence.  However, it's crucial to understand that this medication is no longer available on the market due to serious safety concerns.  Its use was discontinued because of a demonstrated link to potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats).  Therefore, information provided here is for historical and educational purposes only, and it should not be interpreted as an endorsement or recommendation for its use.

### How it Works
Terodiline acted as a non-selective calcium-channel blocker, meaning it interfered with the flow of calcium into certain cells. It also had anticholinergic effects, reducing the activity of the parasympathetic nervous system.  The combination of these actions was believed to help relax the bladder muscles and reduce urinary urgency and frequency.  However, the mechanism by which it caused serious cardiac side effects remains a subject of study, emphasizing the risks associated with its use.
