# Torasemide

**Generic Name:** Torasemide

**Usage:**

Torasemide is a loop diuretic used to treat:

* **High blood pressure (hypertension):** It helps to lower blood pressure by increasing the excretion of sodium and water from the body.
* **Fluid retention (edema):** Associated with heart failure, liver disease (cirrhosis), or kidney disease (nephrotic syndrome).  It reduces swelling by removing excess fluid.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Dehydration
* Low blood pressure (hypotension)
* Increased thirst
* Frequent urination
* Muscle cramps
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Constipation
* Weakness
* Fatigue

Less common but more serious side effects include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Low potassium levels (hypokalemia) – can lead to muscle weakness, irregular heartbeat
* Low sodium levels (hyponatremia) – can lead to confusion, seizures
* Low magnesium levels (hypomagnesemia) – can lead to muscle cramps, irregular heartbeat
* Hearing loss (rare)
* Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
* Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)


**How it Works:**

Torasemide is a loop diuretic, meaning it works in the loop of Henle in the kidneys.  It inhibits the sodium-potassium-chloride co-transporter (NKCC2), preventing the reabsorption of sodium, potassium, chloride, and water back into the bloodstream.  By blocking this reabsorption, more sodium, potassium, chloride, and water are excreted in the urine, leading to a decrease in blood volume and blood pressure.  This reduced blood volume also helps alleviate fluid retention.
