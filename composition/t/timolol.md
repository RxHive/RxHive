# Timolol

**Generic Name:** Timolol

**Usage:**

Timolol is a beta-blocker used to treat several conditions, primarily:

* **Glaucoma:**  It lowers intraocular pressure (IOP), reducing the risk of vision loss from glaucoma.  This is its most common use.
* **Ocular Hypertension:**  It lowers elevated IOP even in the absence of glaucoma, preventing the development of glaucoma.
* **Migraine prophylaxis (oral timolol):** While less common than ophthalmic use, oral timolol can be prescribed to help prevent migraine headaches.  This is *not* the same as topical eye drops.

**Important Note:** The usage information here refers to the different formulations of Timolol.  Topical eye drops are used for glaucoma and ocular hypertension, while oral tablets are used for migraine prophylaxis.  Never use one formulation as if it were another; always follow your doctor's instructions.


**Side Effects:**

Side effects can vary depending on the route of administration (eye drops vs. oral tablets) and individual sensitivity.  Common side effects include:

* **Ophthalmic (eye drops):** Burning or stinging sensation in the eyes, eye irritation, dry eyes, blurred vision, headache.  Less common, but more serious, side effects can include allergic reactions, eye pain, and changes in vision.
* **Oral (tablets):**  Slow heart rate (bradycardia), low blood pressure (hypotension), dizziness, fatigue, shortness of breath, nausea, diarrhea, depression, sleep disturbances, and cold hands and feet.  Serious side effects can include heart problems and breathing difficulties.

This is not an exhaustive list. Consult a doctor or pharmacist for a complete list of potential side effects.

**How it Works:**

* **In glaucoma and ocular hypertension (ophthalmic):** Timolol works by decreasing the production of aqueous humor (the fluid inside the eye).  This reduces the pressure within the eye.
* **In migraine prophylaxis (oral):** The exact mechanism by which oral timolol prevents migraines isn't fully understood, but it's thought to be related to its effects on the nervous system, potentially influencing blood vessel constriction and neurotransmitter activity.
