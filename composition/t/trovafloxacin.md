# Trovafloxacin
### Generic Name
Trovafloxacin

### Usage
Trovafloxacin is a broad-spectrum fluoroquinolone antibiotic that was formerly used to treat a range of bacterial infections.  These included community-acquired pneumonia (infection of the lungs), complicated skin and skin-structure infections (such as cellulitis), gynecological infections (infections of the female reproductive organs), nosocomial pneumonia (pneumonia acquired in a hospital setting), pelvic infections, and complicated intra-abdominal infections (infections within the abdominal cavity).  It's crucial to understand that Trovafloxacin is no longer available on the market due to safety concerns.

### Side Effects
While Trovafloxacin is no longer available, it's important to note the reasons for its withdrawal.  The most significant risk associated with Trovafloxacin was the potential for severe liver damage, including fatal liver failure.  Other potential side effects, which varied in frequency and severity, may have included:

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal pain
* Headache
* Dizziness
* Photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight)
* Joint pain
* Tendinitis (inflammation of the tendons)


This list is not exhaustive, and the experience of side effects would have varied from person to person.  The potential for serious side effects was a major reason for the drug's removal from the market.

### How it Works
Trovafloxacin's bactericidal (bacteria-killing) action stems from its ability to inhibit bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV. These are crucial enzymes involved in bacterial DNA replication, transcription (the process of making RNA from DNA), and repair. By blocking these enzymes, Trovafloxacin prevents bacterial DNA from properly functioning, leading to bacterial cell death.  This mechanism of action is common to many fluoroquinolone antibiotics.
