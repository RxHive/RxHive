# Thioctic Acid
### Generic Name
Thioctic Acid

### Usage
Thioctic acid, also known as alpha-lipoic acid, is primarily used as a dietary supplement to manage diabetic neuropathy.  Diabetic neuropathy is nerve damage caused by high blood sugar levels, leading to symptoms like pain, numbness, tingling, and burning sensations in the extremities.  While research is ongoing, some studies suggest potential benefits in other conditions, but its efficacy in these areas is not definitively established.  Therefore, its primary and currently most supported use remains in the management of diabetic neuropathy symptoms.

### Dosage
**Adult Dosage:** For diabetic neuropathy, the typical oral dosage is 600 mg once daily.

**Pediatric Dosage:** The safety and efficacy of thioctic acid in children have not been established.  It should not be administered to children without the explicit guidance of a pediatrician or other qualified healthcare professional.


### Side Effects
Common side effects, though not specified in terms of frequency, may include:

*   Allergic reactions (skin rash)
*   Nausea
*   Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)

Less common, but potentially serious, side effects are possible.  It's crucial to seek immediate medical attention if you experience any unusual or concerning symptoms after taking thioctic acid.

### Precautions
*   **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of thioctic acid during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Use during these periods should only be considered under the strict supervision of a healthcare professional.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Although not specifically listed, potential interactions with other medications are possible.  Always inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking to avoid potential adverse interactions.
*   **Other Precautions:**  Patients should be advised to take thioctic acid on an empty stomach, approximately 30 minutes before meals.  The tablets should not be chewed or crushed.
