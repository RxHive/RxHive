# Theophylline + Terbutaline

**Generic Name:** Theophylline and Terbutaline (combination not typically available as a single generic product)

**Usage:**  A combination of theophylline and terbutaline is *not typically available as a single pre-mixed formulation*.  Theophylline and terbutaline are bronchodilators often used separately to treat asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). They might be prescribed *together* by a physician in specific cases, but this would involve separate prescriptions and administration.

* **Theophylline:**  Used to prevent and treat wheezing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness caused by asthma and COPD. It's also sometimes used to treat apnea in premature infants.

* **Terbutaline:** Used for short-term relief of wheezing and shortness of breath caused by asthma and COPD.  It can also be used to delay preterm labor.

**Side Effects:**  Since this is usually not a combined formulation, side effects are listed separately.  The severity of side effects varies from person to person and with dosage.

* **Theophylline:** Common side effects include headache, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, insomnia, restlessness, and heart palpitations.  Serious side effects (though less common) include seizures, arrhythmias, and liver damage.

* **Terbutaline:** Common side effects include headache, nervousness, tremors, muscle cramps, dizziness, and palpitations.  Serious side effects can include chest pain, irregular heartbeat, and low blood potassium.

* **Terbutaline:**  A beta-2 adrenergic agonist.  This means it stimulates beta-2 receptors in the lungs, leading to relaxation of the bronchial smooth muscles and widening of the airways.
