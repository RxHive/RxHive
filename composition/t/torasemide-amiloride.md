# Torasemide + Amiloride

**Generic Name:** Torasemide and Amiloride

**Usage:** This combination is not a standard, pre-made formulation.  Torasemide and amiloride are often prescribed *together*, but separately, not as a single pill.  Doctors may prescribe them concurrently to treat certain types of heart failure and edema (swelling) associated with conditions like congestive heart failure and cirrhosis.  The rationale is that torasemide increases urine output (diuresis) while amiloride helps to prevent potassium loss, a common side effect of torasemide.  **It's crucial to understand this combination is not a single drug but a prescribed combination of two separate medications.**  Always follow your doctor's instructions.

**How it Works:**

* **Torasemide:** This is a loop diuretic. It works by blocking the reabsorption of sodium, chloride, and water in the loop of Henle in the kidneys. This leads to increased excretion of these substances in the urine, resulting in increased urine production (diuresis) and reduction of fluid volume in the body.

* **Amiloride:** This is a potassium-sparing diuretic.  It works by blocking sodium channels in the kidneys, promoting the excretion of sodium and reducing the excretion of potassium.  This helps counteract the potassium loss caused by torasemide.

The combined effect is a more effective reduction in fluid retention while minimizing the risk of dangerously low potassium levels.
