# Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Lamivudine + Atazanavir + Ritonavir

**Generic Name:** Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate + Lamivudine + Atazanavir + Ritonavir  (This is not a single FDA-approved fixed-dose combination.  These are four separate drugs often prescribed together.)

**Usage:** This combination of drugs is used to treat HIV-1 infection.  It's a highly active antiretroviral therapy (HAART) regimen.  The specific combination and dosages would be determined by a physician based on individual patient factors.  It's crucial to understand that this is *not* a single pill combination readily available; rather, it's a prescription of four separate medications that must be taken together according to the physician's instructions.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects vary depending on the individual and the specific drugs involved but can include:

* **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate:** Kidney problems (including renal failure), bone loss, lactic acidosis, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.
* **Lamivudine:** Headache, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, fatigue.
* **Atazanavir:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, jaundice, rash, hyperbilirubinemia (increased bilirubin levels), elevated liver enzymes.  Atazanavir's interaction with ritonavir can worsen these side effects.
* **Ritonavir:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, headache, fatigue, altered taste, hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol), pancreatitis.  Ritonavir is a booster drug that increases the blood levels of atazanavir, but also has its own side effects.

**It is crucial to note that these side effects are not exhaustive.**  More severe side effects are possible and immediate medical attention should be sought if any concerning symptoms occur.


**How it Works:**

* **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate:** A nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). It interferes with the HIV virus's ability to replicate by incorporating itself into the viral DNA chain, terminating further replication.
* **Lamivudine:** Also an NRTI, it works similarly to tenofovir by blocking reverse transcriptase.  It's often used in combination with other NRTIs to increase effectiveness and to reduce the chance of viral resistance.
* **Atazanavir:** A protease inhibitor (PI). It blocks the activity of HIV protease, an enzyme essential for the maturation of new viral particles.  By preventing maturation, the virus cannot effectively infect new cells.
* **Ritonavir:** A PI used as a "booster" drug. It inhibits the metabolism of atazanavir, increasing its blood levels and thus its effectiveness.
