# Trypsin Chymotrypsin

**Generic Name:** Trypsin-Chymotrypsin

**Usage:**  Trypsin-Chymotrypsin is a combination of two proteolytic enzymes (enzymes that break down proteins). It's primarily used topically to reduce inflammation and edema (swelling) associated with:

* **Acute injuries:** Sprains, strains, contusions (bruises).
* **Surgical procedures:**  To reduce post-surgical swelling and inflammation.
* **Inflammatory conditions:**  Some inflammatory conditions involving localized swelling, though this is less common due to the availability of more effective treatments.


**Side Effects:**  Side effects are generally mild and localized, but can include:

* **Burning or stinging sensation:** At the application site.
* **Skin irritation:** Redness, itching, rash.
* **Allergic reactions:** Although rare, allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis in extremely rare cases) are possible, particularly in individuals with known allergies to enzymes or other protein substances.
* **Systemic effects (rare):**  If absorbed systemically in significant amounts, potentially causing nausea, vomiting, or other gastrointestinal distress. This is unlikely with topical application.


**How it Works:**  Trypsin and chymotrypsin are proteolytic enzymes that break down proteins.  When applied topically to an inflamed area:

* **They degrade fibrin:** Fibrin is a protein involved in blood clot formation and inflammation. Breaking it down helps to reduce swelling and improve blood circulation.
* **They help to remove necrotic tissue (dead tissue):** This promotes healing and reduces the inflammatory response.
* **They reduce the viscosity of inflammatory exudates:**  Making it easier for the body to drain the fluid associated with swelling.
