# Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

**Generic Name:** Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)

**Usage:**

Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is primarily used in the treatment of:

* **HIV infection:**  It's a crucial component of antiretroviral therapy (ART) regimens, helping to suppress the HIV virus and improve the immune system.  It's usually used in combination with other antiretroviral drugs.
* **Chronic hepatitis B (HBV) infection:** TDF can reduce HBV viral load and improve liver function in patients with chronic hepatitis B.  It's often used long-term to manage the infection.

**Important Note:** TDF is *not a cure* for HIV or HBV.  It requires lifelong adherence for optimal effectiveness.  Stopping treatment can lead to viral rebound and potential health complications.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of TDF include:

* **Gastrointestinal problems:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain
* **Kidney problems:**  TDF can affect kidney function, especially with long-term use.  Regular monitoring of kidney function is crucial.  This is a more significant risk in patients with pre-existing kidney disease.
* **Bone problems:**  Reduced bone mineral density (osteoporosis) and bone pain have been reported, particularly with long-term use.
* **Lactic acidosis:**  A rare but serious side effect, characterized by the buildup of lactic acid in the blood.  Symptoms can include fatigue, muscle weakness, and abdominal pain.
* **Other potential side effects:** Headaches, dizziness, fatigue, rash, and changes in blood counts.

The severity and frequency of side effects can vary depending on the individual and dosage.

**How it Works:**

Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate is a nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). It works by interfering with the HIV or HBV virus's ability to replicate itself. Specifically:

* It's converted into tenofovir diphosphate, a compound that inhibits reverse transcriptase, an enzyme crucial for the virus to make DNA copies of its RNA genome.  This blocks the viral replication cycle.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose.  Do not take two doses at once.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you have concerns about missed doses.

* **Q: Can Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate interact with other medications?** A: Yes, it can interact with other medications.  It's crucial to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you're taking.
