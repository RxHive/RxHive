# Tofisopam
### Generic Name
Tofisopam

### Usage
Tofisopam is a benzodiazepine primarily used to manage autonomic nervous system symptoms. These symptoms, which can include headache, malaise (general discomfort), palpitations, and sweating, may arise from various conditions such as dysautonomia (a dysfunction of the autonomic nervous system), head or neck injuries, menopause, and ovarian dysfunction.  It's also indicated for the short-term management of anxiety and for treating alcohol withdrawal symptoms.

### Dosage

**Adult Dosage:**

* **Autonomic nervous system symptoms:** The typical oral dose is 50 mg three times daily (TID).  Dosage adjustments should be made based on individual symptom response, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
* **Alcohol withdrawal syndrome and short-term anxiety management:**  The typical oral dose ranges from 50 to 100 mg, one to three times daily.  Again, dosage adjustments should be guided by a healthcare professional.


**Pediatric Dosage:**
The safety and efficacy of Tofisopam in children have not been established.  It should not be used in children.


**Dosage Adjustments:**  The manufacturer's labeling does not provide specific dosage adjustments for patients with hepatic (liver) or renal (kidney) impairment.  Always consult a doctor before taking Tofisopam if you have liver or kidney problems.

### Side Effects

Common side effects (frequency not specified in available data) can include:

* **Nervous System:** Agitation, anxiety, depression, dizziness, drowsiness, headache, insomnia, malaise, staggering gait, and tingling sensations.
* **Cardiovascular:** Facial edema (swelling), flushing, hypertension (high blood pressure), and palpitations.
* **Dermatologic:** Pruritus (itching) and skin rash.
* **Gastrointestinal:** Abdominal pain, anorexia (loss of appetite), constipation, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, and dry mouth (xerostomia).
* **Endocrine & Metabolic:** Menstrual irregularities.
* **Hepatic:** Increased levels of liver enzymes (ALT and AST).
* **Neuromuscular & Skeletal:** Tremor and weakness.
* **Genitourinary:** Lactation (milk production) and breast pain (mastalgia).
* **Other:** Fever


Serious side effects, although less common, are possible.  Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any concerning symptoms.

### How it Works

Tofisopam is a benzodiazepine that works by enhancing the effects of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain. GABA is a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve impulses, essentially calming the nervous system.  By increasing GABA activity, Tofisopam reduces the excessive or abnormal activity of nerve cells, thus alleviating symptoms of anxiety, autonomic dysfunction, and alcohol withdrawal.

### Precautions

* **Contraindications:**  No specific contraindications are listed in the manufacturer's labeling, but caution is advised in various circumstances (see below).
* **Increased Risk:**  Tofisopam carries an increased risk of paradoxical reactions (opposite effects, such as increased anxiety or aggression), central nervous system (CNS) depression, sleep-related activities (sleepwalking, sleep-driving, etc.), and anterograde amnesia (difficulty forming new memories).
* **Use with Caution:** Exercise caution when using Tofisopam in patients with acute angle-closure glaucoma, those at risk of falls, patients with severe myasthenia gravis, moderate or severe respiratory disease, organic brain damage, elderly patients, and when reducing the dose or discontinuing treatment. It should also be used cautiously in patients with a history of alcohol or drug abuse, insomnia, or psychological and psychiatric disorders.
* **Drug Interactions:** Tofisopam can interact negatively with other medications, particularly other CNS depressants such as opioid agonists, other benzodiazepines, and certain antihistamines.  Always inform your doctor of all medications you are taking, including over-the-counter drugs and herbal supplements.
* **Pregnancy and Lactation:** The safety of Tofisopam during pregnancy is unknown. Its use should be avoided, especially during the first trimester and near term. Tofisopam is excreted in breast milk; breastfeeding is not recommended while using this medication.


### FAQs

* **Q: Can I drive while taking Tofisopam?** A: No. Tofisopam can cause drowsiness and impair judgment, making it unsafe to drive or operate machinery.
* **Q: How should I store Tofisopam?** A: Store Tofisopam at room temperature, away from light and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children.
* **Q: Can I suddenly stop taking Tofisopam?** A: No. Abruptly stopping Tofisopam can lead to withdrawal symptoms. Always follow your doctor's instructions for gradually reducing your dosage.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
* **Q: Is Tofisopam habit-forming?** A:  Like other benzodiazepines, Tofisopam has the potential for dependence with prolonged use.  It should only be used for the duration recommended by your physician.
