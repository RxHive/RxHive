# Trimebutine
### Generic Name
Trimebutine

### Usage
Trimebutine is primarily used to treat symptoms associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), often referred to as spastic colon.  It helps manage the abdominal pain, cramping, bloating, and altered bowel habits characteristic of IBS.  A secondary, but significant, use is in the treatment of postoperative paralytic ileus. In this context, it helps to restore normal bowel function and movement after abdominal surgery, accelerating recovery.

### Dosage

**Adults:**

* **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) and Postoperative Paralytic Ileus (Oral):** The typical dose is 200 mg three times a day (TID).
* **Postoperative Paralytic Ileus (Intravenous/Intramuscular):**  The usual dose is 100 mg every 12 hours.

**Children:**

* **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) and Postoperative Paralytic Ileus (Oral):**  For children aged 12 years and older, the dosage is the same as for adults.  Trimebutine is generally not recommended for children under 12 years old unless specifically prescribed by a physician.


**Important Note:**  Dosage adjustments for hepatic (liver) or renal (kidney) impairment are not specified in standard prescribing information. Always consult with a healthcare professional for appropriate dosage in individuals with these conditions.  The medication should be taken as directed by your doctor or pharmacist. Never alter the prescribed dosage without medical advice.


### Side Effects

**Common Side Effects (Occurring in 1-10% of patients):**

* Nervous system effects: Dizziness, drowsiness, fatigue, mood changes, feeling hot or cold, altered taste.
* Gastrointestinal effects: Constipation, diarrhea, dyspepsia (indigestion), nausea, epigastric distress (upper abdominal discomfort), dry mouth (xerostomia).

**Less Common Side Effects (Frequency not precisely defined):**

* Nervous system effects: Anxiety, headache.
* Endocrine and metabolic effects: Gynecomastia (breast development in males), menstrual irregularities.
* Genitourinary effects: Breast hypertrophy (enlarged breasts), mastalgia (breast pain), urinary retention.
* Otic effects: Hearing impairment.


**Rare Side Effects (<1%, Post-marketing and/or Case Reports):**

* Skin rash

If you experience any of these side effects, particularly if they are severe or persistent, contact your doctor immediately.


### How it Works

Trimebutine is a spasmolytic agent, meaning it helps relax the muscles of the intestines. It achieves this through a multifaceted mechanism.  It possesses antiserotonergic activity, particularly affecting "M" receptors in the gut. It also exhibits a moderate affinity for opioid receptors.  By modulating these receptors, trimebutine reduces abnormal intestinal motility (muscle contractions) while generally preserving normal bowel movements.  It targets the abnormal contractions responsible for the symptoms of IBS and postoperative ileus without entirely suppressing gut function.


### Precautions

* **Contraindications:** Trimebutine is contraindicated in individuals with known hypersensitivity (allergy) to trimebutine or any component of the formulation.
* **Interactions:** Concurrent use with cisapride and pramlintide may diminish the effectiveness of trimebutine.  Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, or herbal remedies you are taking.
* **Pregnancy and Lactation:**  The safety of trimebutine during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  Its use during pregnancy is generally not recommended, and caution is advised if breastfeeding. Consult your doctor before using trimebutine if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
* **Central Nervous System (CNS) Depression:**  Trimebutine may increase the risk of CNS depression, so caution is needed when used with other CNS depressants.
* **Driving and Operating Machinery:**  Due to potential drowsiness, avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how trimebutine affects you.


### FAQs

* **Q: Can I take trimebutine with other medications?** A:  Inform your doctor or pharmacist of all medications, vitamins, and supplements you're taking before starting trimebutine. Some interactions may occur.
* **Q: How should I store trimebutine?** A: Store trimebutine as directed on the product label, typically at room temperature, away from moisture and direct sunlight.
* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Do not double the dose to catch up.
* **Q: Is trimebutine habit-forming?** A: Trimebutine is not considered habit-forming.
* **Q:  How long will it take to feel the effects of trimebutine?** A: The onset of effects varies from person to person, but relief from symptoms may be noticed within a few days to a few weeks of starting treatment.  Consistent use as prescribed is important.
