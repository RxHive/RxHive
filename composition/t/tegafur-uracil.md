# Tegafur + Uracil

**Generic Name:** Tegafur/Uracil (often marketed as UFT, or under various brand names depending on region)

**Usage:**

Tegafur/Uracil is a combination chemotherapy drug used primarily to treat:

* **Colorectal cancer:** This is its most common application, often used alone or in combination with other chemotherapeutic agents.
* **Gastric cancer:**  It may be used in certain instances of gastric cancer treatment.
* **Other solid tumors:**  While less common, it may be explored in the treatment of other solid tumors depending on the specific circumstances and the oncologist's judgment.

It's crucial to understand that Tegafur/Uracil is a prescription medication and should only be used under the direct supervision of a qualified oncologist or medical professional. **How it Works:**

Tegafur is a prodrug; it's converted in the body to 5-fluorouracil (5-FU), an antimetabolite.  5-FU inhibits the synthesis of DNA and RNA, thus interfering with the growth and replication of cancer cells. Uracil in the combination enhances the efficacy of Tegafur by inhibiting the enzyme dihydropyrimidine dehydrogenase (DPD), which is responsible for breaking down 5-FU. This leads to higher levels of active 5-FU in the body and improved anti-cancer activity.

* **Are there any drug interactions?** Yes, Tegafur/Uracil can interact with other medications. It's essential to inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.
