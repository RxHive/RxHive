# Tribendimidine
### Generic Name
Tribendimidine

### Usage
Tribendimidine is a broad-spectrum anthelmintic agent, meaning it's effective against a wide range of parasitic worms.  Laboratory and clinical studies in China have demonstrated its activity against at least 20 different species of nematodes, trematodes (flukes), and cestodes (tapeworms).  While its efficacy is promising, it's currently approved for use only in China, and further clinical trials are needed for wider approval and to fully define its range of applications.  The primary use is the treatment of parasitic worm infections, but the precise indications will depend on ongoing research and regulatory approvals.

### How it Works
Tribendimidine acts as an agonist at nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) found in the muscles of parasitic nematodes.  This means it binds to these receptors and activates them, leading to muscle paralysis and immobilization of the worm.  The paralyzed parasite is then more easily expelled from the host's body.  The exact mechanism of action against trematodes and cestodes may differ slightly, but the overall effect is the disruption of their normal function and subsequent elimination.
