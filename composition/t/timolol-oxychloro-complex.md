**Timolol:**

**Generic Name:** Timolol Maleate

**Usage:**  Primarily used to treat glaucoma and high blood pressure (hypertension) in its ophthalmic and systemic forms respectively.  Ophthalmic timolol lowers eye pressure. Systemic timolol lowers blood pressure and reduces the risk of heart attack in certain patients.

**Side Effects:**  Can vary depending on the form (eye drops or oral) and dosage. Common side effects include:
* **Ophthalmic:** Burning or stinging in the eyes, eye irritation, dry eyes, blurred vision. Less common but serious: bradycardia (slow heart rate), breathing problems.
* **Systemic:** Slow heart rate, low blood pressure, dizziness, fatigue, nausea, diarrhea, depression, sleep disturbances.  More serious side effects are possible.

**How it Works:**  In the eye, Timolol decreases the production of aqueous humor (fluid in the eye), reducing intraocular pressure. Systemically, it blocks beta-adrenergic receptors, affecting heart rate and blood pressure.


**Chloroquine/Hydroxychloroquine:**

**How it Works:** Chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine interfere with the parasite's ability to reproduce within the body's cells (in the case of malaria). Their mechanisms in autoimmune diseases are less well understood.
