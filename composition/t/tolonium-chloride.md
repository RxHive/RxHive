# Tolonium Chloride
### Generic Name
Tolonium Chloride

### Usage
Tolonium chloride is a phenothiazine dye that was historically used primarily as a diagnostic aid.  Its main application was in staining oral and gastric neoplasms (tumors) to help distinguish cancerous tissue from healthy tissue during examinations.  It was also used intravenously to help visualize parathyroid glands during surgery.  Other, less common uses explored included the treatment of menstrual disorders and methemoglobinemia (a condition where the blood's ability to carry oxygen is reduced).  It is important to note that Tolonium chloride is currently discontinued from the market and is not readily available for medical use.

### How it Works
Tolonium chloride is a basic metachromatic dye, meaning it changes color depending on the environment.  It stains tissues by binding to acidic components such as carboxylates and sulfates, which are found in higher concentrations in cancerous cells than in normal cells. This selective staining allows clinicians to visually differentiate between cancerous and healthy tissue. Its high affinity for DNA and RNA in the perinuclear cisternae (the space between the nucleus and the endoplasmic reticulum) further enhances its usefulness in identifying dysplasias and malignancies.
