# Tamsulosin

**Generic Name:** Tamsulosin

**Usage:**

Tamsulosin is used to treat the symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), also known as an enlarged prostate.  It relaxes the muscles in the prostate and bladder neck, making urination easier. It is also sometimes used to treat kidney stones.  **It is crucial to note that tamsulosin does not treat the underlying cause of BPH, only the symptoms.**  A doctor should diagnose the condition and determine the appropriate treatment plan.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Runny nose
* Fatigue
* Sleep disturbances
* Decreased libido
* Retrograde ejaculation (ejaculation backward into the bladder)
* Orthostatic hypotension (a sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing)


Less common but more serious side effects include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, hives, itching, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
* Priapism (a prolonged and painful erection)
* Intraoperative floppy iris syndrome (IFIS) (a complication during cataract surgery)


**How it Works:**

Tamsulosin is an alpha-1 adrenergic receptor blocker.  These receptors are found in the prostate and bladder neck.  By blocking these receptors, tamsulosin relaxes the muscles in these areas, reducing the resistance to urine flow and improving urination.
