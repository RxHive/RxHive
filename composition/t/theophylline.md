# Theophylline

**Generic Name:** Theophylline

**Usage:**

Theophylline is a bronchodilator used primarily to treat asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).  It relaxes the muscles around the airways in the lungs, making it easier to breathe. It's often used as a long-term maintenance medication, rather than for immediate relief of symptoms (like an inhaler).  The specific conditions for use and dosage will be determined by a physician based on individual needs and response. It may be used alone or in combination with other medications.


**Side Effects:**

Side effects can vary depending on the dosage and individual sensitivity. Common side effects include:

* **Headache:** A frequent side effect.
* **Nausea and vomiting:** Can be significant at higher doses.
* **Stomach upset:** Including diarrhea and heartburn.
* **Insomnia:** Difficulty sleeping.
* **Restlessness and nervousness:** Feeling anxious or jittery.
* **Rapid heartbeat (tachycardia):**  A serious side effect requiring immediate medical attention.
* **Tremors:** Shaking or trembling.
* **Seizures:** A rare but serious side effect, usually associated with very high blood levels.

More serious, though less common, side effects include:

* **Arrhythmias:** Irregular heartbeats.
* **Liver damage:**  Rare, but possible.  Regular blood tests are sometimes necessary.


**How it Works:**

Theophylline works by relaxing the smooth muscles in the airways of the lungs. It does this by inhibiting the enzyme phosphodiesterase, which leads to an increase in cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP). Increased cAMP levels cause relaxation of the airway muscles, resulting in bronchodilation and improved airflow.  The exact mechanisms are complex and involve interactions with other cellular processes.
