# Tinidazole

**Generic Name:** Tinidazole

**Usage:** Tinidazole is an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, most commonly:

* **Trichomoniasis:** A sexually transmitted infection caused by the parasite *Trichomonas vaginalis*.
* **Bacterial vaginosis (BV):** A vaginal infection caused by an imbalance of bacteria in the vagina.  Tinidazole is often used as an alternative to metronidazole for BV.
* **Giardiasis and amebiasis:** Intestinal infections caused by the parasites *Giardia lamblia* and *Entamoeba histolytica*, respectively.  It's less commonly used for these now.
* **Other infections:**  In some cases, it may be used to treat other infections caused by susceptible bacteria.  However, its use is often limited to situations where other antibiotics are ineffective or contraindicated.
