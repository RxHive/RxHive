# Usnic Acid
### Generic Name
Usnic Acid

### Usage
Usnic acid is a natural compound found in lichens.  It's primarily used in topical applications, such as cosmetics (creams, lotions), deodorants, and even some toothpastes, due to its antimicrobial properties. These applications leverage its ability to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi.  While it has been explored for its potential medicinal uses, its application in this area is limited due to significant safety concerns.  It is *not* intended for internal use.

### Dosage
Because of significant safety concerns and the risk of severe liver damage, there are no established safe and effective dosages for internal use of usnic acid.  Topical applications, such as in creams or lotions, typically involve low concentrations, the exact amount varying depending on the specific product formulation.  It's crucial to always follow the instructions provided on the product label.  **Never ingest usnic acid.**

### Side Effects
* **Common Side Effects (Topical):**  Generally, topical application of low concentrations of usnic acid is well-tolerated. However, some individuals may experience mild skin irritation such as redness or itching at the application site.
* **Serious Side Effects (Internal):** Ingesting usnic acid has been linked to severe liver damage (hepatotoxicity), which can be life-threatening.  Symptoms can include jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), dark urine, abdominal pain, and fatigue.  These are serious symptoms and require immediate medical attention.

### How it Works
Usnic acid's antimicrobial effect stems from its ability to disrupt the energy production processes in bacteria and fungi. Specifically, it interferes with oxidative phosphorylation, a crucial step in cellular respiration. By hindering this process, usnic acid prevents these microorganisms from generating the energy they need to survive and reproduce.  However, this same mechanism is responsible for its hepatotoxicity when ingested; it disrupts the energy production in liver cells leading to damage and potential cell death.

### Precautions
* **Contraindications:** Usnic acid is contraindicated for oral ingestion due to the significant risk of severe liver damage. It should also be avoided by individuals with known liver conditions.
* **Drug Interactions:** Interactions with other medications are not well-documented for topical use, however, there may be an interaction with medications processed in the liver if ingested.
* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  The safety of usnic acid during pregnancy and breastfeeding is unknown.  Avoid use unless explicitly advised by a doctor.
* **Children:** The safety of usnic acid in children is not established. Avoid use in children.
* **Elderly:**  The elderly may be more susceptible to adverse effects.  Exercise caution.
* **Allergic Reactions:**  Individuals with known allergies to lichens or related compounds should avoid usnic acid.  Stop use and seek immediate medical attention if an allergic reaction occurs.
