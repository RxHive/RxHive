# Ulipristal acetate

**Generic Name:** Ulipristal acetate

**Usage:**

Ulipristal acetate is primarily used for:

* **Emergency contraception (EC):**  Also known as the "morning-after pill," it prevents pregnancy after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure.  It's most effective when taken within 120 hours (5 days) of unprotected sex, but works best the sooner it's taken.

* **Treatment of uterine fibroids:**  It shrinks fibroids, reducing symptoms like heavy bleeding and pelvic pain. This is typically a longer-term treatment, involving regular dosing.

* **Treatment of endometriosis:**  While less common than its use for fibroids, it's sometimes used to manage endometriosis-related symptoms.

More serious, though less common, side effects can include:

**How it Works:**

* **Emergency contraception:** Ulipristal acetate works by delaying or inhibiting ovulation (the release of an egg).  It can also interfere with fertilization and implantation of a fertilized egg.

* **Uterine fibroids & Endometriosis:** Ulipristal acetate is a selective progesterone receptor modulator (SPRM). This means it blocks the action of progesterone in the uterus, leading to a reduction in the size of fibroids and a decrease in endometrial tissue growth (in endometriosis).  This reduction in tissue growth then leads to a reduction in symptoms.
