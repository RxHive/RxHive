# Iopromide

**Generic Name:** Iopromide

**Usage:** Iopromide is a non-ionic iodinated contrast medium used in various medical imaging procedures, primarily:

* **Computed Tomography (CT) scans:**  To enhance the visibility of blood vessels, organs, and tissues in CT images.  This allows for better diagnosis of various conditions affecting different parts of the body.
* **Angiography:** To visualize blood vessels during procedures such as coronary angiography (heart arteries) or peripheral angiography (limb arteries).
* **Myelography:** (Less common now) To image the spinal cord and surrounding structures.
* **Other procedures:** It may be used in other imaging procedures requiring contrast enhancement.

**How it Works:** Iopromide is a radiopaque contrast agent, meaning it absorbs X-rays to a greater extent than surrounding tissues.  When injected into the bloodstream, it increases the radiodensity of blood vessels and organs, making them appear brighter on X-ray or CT images. This improved contrast allows radiologists to better visualize and diagnose various medical conditions.  It works by its high iodine content which attenuates x-rays.
