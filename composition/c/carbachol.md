# Carbachol

**Generic Name:** Carbachol

**Usage:**

Carbachol is a cholinergic drug used primarily in ophthalmology and rarely in other medical situations.  Its uses include:

* **Glaucoma treatment:** Carbachol eye drops are used to lower intraocular pressure (IOP) in patients with glaucoma or ocular hypertension. It works by constricting the pupil (miosis) and improving aqueous humor outflow.
* **Postoperative treatment following cataract surgery:**  It can help prevent postoperative complications by reducing intraocular pressure.
* **Diagnosis of myasthenia gravis:** (Less common)  It can be used in diagnostic tests for myasthenia gravis, although edrophonium is more frequently used for this purpose.
* **Urinary bladder atony:** (Very rare) In rare instances, it may be used to treat urinary bladder atony (lack of muscle tone in the bladder).

**How it Works:**

Carbachol is a cholinergic agonist, meaning it mimics the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter in the parasympathetic nervous system.  It directly stimulates muscarinic receptors, particularly in the eye, causing:

* **Miosis:** Constriction of the pupil.
* **Increased outflow of aqueous humor:** This reduces intraocular pressure.
* **Accommodation:** Increased focusing power of the lens.

In the bladder, it directly stimulates the muscarinic receptors on the detrusor muscle, causing contraction and improved bladder emptying.
