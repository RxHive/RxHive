# Cefixime + Ofloxacin + Lactobacillus
### Generic Name
Cefixime + Ofloxacin + Lactobacillus

**Note:**  This combination is not a standard, pre-formulated generic medication. It represents a combination of three different drugs often prescribed *together* to treat specific infections, but not as a single, unified product with a single generic name.  Each component has its own established generic name and usage.  This information describes the individual components and their typical use in combination.  Always follow your doctor's specific prescription and instructions.


### Usage

This combination of medications is frequently prescribed for treating bacterial infections, particularly those affecting the urinary tract or gastrointestinal system.  The specific infections targeted depend on the bacterial species causing the infection.


* **Cefixime:** A third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic, effective against a broad range of gram-negative and some gram-positive bacteria.  It's primarily used to treat bacterial infections of the respiratory tract (e.g., bronchitis, pneumonia), urinary tract (e.g., cystitis, pyelonephritis), and skin.

* **Ofloxacin:** A fluoroquinolone antibiotic, effective against a wide spectrum of gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria.  It's often used to treat urinary tract infections, respiratory infections, and some gastrointestinal infections.

* **Lactobacillus:**  This is a probiotic, a live microorganism considered beneficial for gut health.  It's included in this combination to help restore the balance of gut flora that can be disrupted by antibiotic use.  Antibiotics can kill beneficial bacteria along with harmful bacteria, leading to digestive upset. Lactobacillus helps to mitigate this side effect.


### Dosage

Dosage of each component varies significantly depending on the patient's age, weight, the severity of the infection, and the prescribing physician's judgment. There's no single standard dosage for this combination because it's not a pre-mixed drug.  **A doctor will determine the correct dosage of each individual medication.**  

* **Cefixime:** Dosages typically range from 200mg to 400mg once or twice daily, orally, depending on the infection.  Pediatric dosing is significantly lower and weight-based.  IV administration is also possible but requires a healthcare professional's expertise.

* **Ofloxacin:**  Dosage ranges vary from 200mg to 400mg once or twice daily, orally, or intravenously. Dosage adjustments may be necessary for those with impaired kidney function. Pediatric dosing is weight-based and requires careful monitoring.

* **Lactobacillus:** Dosage of probiotics varies greatly depending on the specific strain and product. Follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully.


### Side Effects

**Cefixime:**

* Common: Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.
* Less Common but Serious: Allergic reactions (rash, hives, itching, difficulty breathing), pseudomembranous colitis (severe bowel inflammation).

**Ofloxacin:**

* Common: Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, headache, dizziness.
* Less Common but Serious: Tendonitis, tendon rupture, peripheral neuropathy, seizures.


**Lactobacillus:**

* Generally well-tolerated, but some individuals might experience mild gastrointestinal discomfort (gas, bloating).


**Important Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list.  Always report any side effect, especially if it's severe or concerning, to your doctor or pharmacist.


### How it Works

* **Cefixime and Ofloxacin:** Both are antibiotics that work by interfering with bacterial cell wall synthesis (Cefixime) or bacterial DNA replication (Ofloxacin), ultimately killing the bacteria or preventing their growth.  The combination is often used to broaden the spectrum of bacterial coverage.

* **Lactobacillus:** This probiotic helps maintain a healthy balance of gut bacteria.  It competes with harmful bacteria for space and nutrients, and it can also produce substances that inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria.


### Precautions

* **Allergies:**  Individuals with known allergies to cephalosporins (Cefixime) or fluoroquinolones (Ofloxacin) should not take these medications.

* **Drug Interactions:**  Cefixime and Ofloxacin can interact with other medications.  Inform your doctor of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

* **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:**  Use of Cefixime and Ofloxacin during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be carefully considered and only used under strict medical supervision.  Lactobacillus is generally considered safe.

* **Kidney and Liver Disease:**  Dosage adjustments may be necessary for individuals with impaired kidney or liver function.

* **Children:**  Pediatric use requires careful dosage adjustment based on weight and overall health.


### FAQs

* **Q: Can I take this combination without a prescription?** A: No.  These medications should only be taken under the guidance of a healthcare professional.  Self-medicating can be dangerous.

* **Q: How long should I take this medication?** A: The duration of treatment depends on the type and severity of the infection.  Follow your doctor's instructions closely.

* **Q: What should I do if I miss a dose?** A: Take the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for the next dose.  Do not double the dose.

* **Q: How should I store this medication?** A: Store as directed on the product label.  Generally, antibiotics should be stored at room temperature away from moisture and heat.

* **Q:  Can I drink alcohol while taking this medication?** A:  Generally, alcohol should be avoided while taking antibiotics. Check with your physician for specific guidance.
