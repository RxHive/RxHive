# Cefprozil

**Generic Name:** Cefprozil

**Usage:** Cefprozil is an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, including:

* **Middle ear infections (otitis media):**  Common in children.
* **Sinus infections (sinusitis):** Infections of the nasal sinuses.
* **Tonsillitis and pharyngitis (strep throat):** Infections of the tonsils and throat.
* **Pneumonia (in some cases):**  Infection of the lungs.
* **Skin infections:** Such as impetigo and cellulitis.


**Side Effects:** Like all medications, Cefprozil can cause side effects.  These can range from mild to severe. Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.
* **Allergic reactions:** Rash, itching, hives.  More serious allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) are rare but possible and require immediate medical attention.
* **Headache:** A common side effect.
* **Vaginal yeast infection (candidiasis):**  Antibiotics can disrupt the balance of gut and vaginal flora.
* **Changes in taste:** Temporary alteration of taste perception.

Less common, but more serious side effects include:

* **Seizures:** Particularly in individuals with pre-existing seizure disorders or those receiving high doses.
* **Pseudomembranous colitis:** A severe intestinal infection caused by *Clostridium difficile*.
* **Elevated liver enzymes:** Indicating potential liver damage.
* **Blood disorders:** Though rare, Cefprozil can affect blood cell counts.


**How it Works:** Cefprozil is a cephalosporin antibiotic.  It works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build and maintain their cell walls.  This weakens the bacteria, making them vulnerable and preventing them from multiplying and spreading the infection.
