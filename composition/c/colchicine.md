# Colchicine

**Generic Name:** Colchicine

**Usage:**

Colchicine is primarily used to treat:

* **Acute gout flares:** It reduces pain and inflammation associated with gout attacks.  It does *not* treat the underlying cause of gout (uric acid buildup).
* **Familial Mediterranean fever (FMF):** It helps to reduce the frequency and severity of FMF attacks.

**Side Effects:**

Colchicine can cause a range of side effects, varying in severity.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, cramping. These are often dose-related and can be severe.
* **Muscle weakness:** Myopathy (muscle disease) is a potential, though less common, serious side effect.
* **Bone marrow suppression:** This is rare but can lead to decreased blood cell counts.
* **Liver damage:**  Rare but serious.
* **Kidney damage:**  Rare but serious.
* **Hair loss:** Less common side effect.
* **Neurological effects:**  Rare but can include peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage in the extremities).


**How it Works:**

Colchicine works by inhibiting microtubule polymerization.  Microtubules are structures within cells involved in inflammation and cell division. By interfering with their function, colchicine reduces the inflammatory response that causes the pain and swelling associated with gout and other conditions.  It specifically inhibits the migration of neutrophils (a type of white blood cell) to the inflamed area.
