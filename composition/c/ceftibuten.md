# Ceftibuten

**Generic Name:** Ceftibuten

**Usage:** Ceftibuten is an antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections of the:

* **Ears:**  Acute otitis media (middle ear infection)
* **Lungs:** Acute bacterial exacerbations of chronic bronchitis
* **Throat:** Pharyngitis/Tonsillitis (strep throat)
* **Sinuses:** Acute bacterial sinusitis (sinus infection)
* **Skin:**  Uncomplicated skin and skin structure infections

**Important Note:** Ceftibuten is only effective against bacterial infections. It will not treat viral infections like the common cold or flu.  Always consult a doctor to determine the cause of your infection before starting antibiotics.  Improper use of antibiotics can lead to antibiotic resistance.

**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, ceftibuten can cause side effects.  These can range from mild to severe. Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
* **Allergic Reactions:** Rash, itching, hives (in rare cases, more serious allergic reactions like angioedema or anaphylaxis can occur)
* **Other:** Headache, dizziness, vaginitis (vaginal yeast infection)


Less common but potentially more serious side effects include:

* **Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD):** This is a serious intestinal infection that can occur after antibiotic use.  Seek medical attention immediately if you experience persistent diarrhea or bloody stools.
* **Pseudomembranous colitis:**  Inflammation of the colon.
* **Seizures:**  This is more likely to occur in patients with a history of seizures or kidney problems.


This is not an exhaustive list of side effects.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist for a complete list and to discuss any concerns.


**How it Works:** Ceftibuten is a cephalosporin antibiotic.  It works by interfering with the ability of bacteria to build their cell walls. This weakens the bacteria, preventing them from growing and multiplying, eventually leading to their death.
