# Carbetocin

**Generic Name:** Carbetocin

**Usage:** Carbetocin is used to prevent postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) in women after vaginal or Cesarean delivery.  It's administered intravenously (IV) after the delivery of the placenta.  Its use is specifically aimed at reducing the risk of significant blood loss following childbirth.

**Side Effects:**  Like any medication, carbetocin can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them.  Common side effects may include:

* **Nausea:** Feeling sick to your stomach.
* **Vomiting:** Throwing up.
* **Hypotension:** Low blood pressure.  This can cause dizziness or lightheadedness.
* **Hypertension:** High blood pressure, though less common.
* **Bradycardia:** Slow heart rate.
* **Headache:** Pain in the head.
* **Fever:** Elevated body temperature.
* **Uterine hypertonus:** Excessive tightening of the uterus, potentially leading to pain.
* **Allergic reactions:** In rare cases, severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) can occur.  Symptoms may include rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing.

It's crucial to note that this is not an exhaustive list, and the severity of side effects can vary.  Any concerning side effects should be reported to a medical professional immediately.

**How it Works:** Carbetocin is a synthetic analog of oxytocin.  Oxytocin is a hormone naturally produced by the body that plays a vital role in uterine contractions and milk production. Carbetocin works by stimulating the uterus to contract, which helps to constrict blood vessels in the uterus and minimize postpartum bleeding.  It has a longer duration of action compared to oxytocin.
