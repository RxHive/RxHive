# Omeprazole + Dicyclomine

**Generic Name:** Omeprazole/Dicyclomine combination (Note: This is not an officially approved fixed-dose combination in many countries.  Prescriptions often involve separate medications.)

**Usage:** This combination is sometimes used off-label to treat symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and peptic ulcers that are accompanied by abdominal cramping and spasms.  Omeprazole reduces stomach acid production, while dicyclomine reduces muscle spasms in the stomach and intestines.  It's important to note that this isn't a standard treatment and the use of the combination should be under strict medical supervision.  The doctor will assess if the benefits outweigh the risks in each individual case.

**Side Effects:**  The side effects are a combination of those associated with omeprazole and dicyclomine, and can vary in severity.  These can include:

* **Omeprazole:** Headache, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, constipation, abdominal pain, dizziness, rash, increased risk of bone fractures (with long-term use),  vitamin B12 deficiency (with long-term use),  Clostridium difficile infection.  Rare but serious side effects include allergic reactions and low magnesium levels.

* **Dicyclomine:** Dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention, drowsiness, dizziness, confusion (especially in older adults), increased heart rate.  Rare but serious side effects can include allergic reactions.

Combining the drugs can increase the likelihood of experiencing these side effects.

**How it Works:**

* **Omeprazole:** This is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI). It works by reducing the amount of acid produced by the cells in the lining of the stomach.  This lowers stomach acidity, relieving symptoms like heartburn and improving ulcer healing.

* **Dicyclomine:** This is an anticholinergic medication. It works by blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates muscle contractions in the digestive tract. This reduces stomach and intestinal spasms, relieving pain and cramping.

* **Is this combination available as a single pill?**  No, it's not commonly available as a fixed-dose combination in most countries.  Doctors usually prescribe omeprazole and dicyclomine as separate medications.

* **How long can I take this combination?**  The duration of treatment will be determined by your doctor and depends on your condition and response to therapy. Long-term use of PPIs like omeprazole has potential risks, so it should be minimized whenever possible.
