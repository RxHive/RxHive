### Ofloxacin

**Generic Name:** Ofloxacin

**Usage:** Ofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections.  Its primary uses include urinary tract infections (UTIs), respiratory tract infections (e.g., pneumonia, bronchitis), skin infections, and some sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

**Dosage:** Dosage varies widely depending on the infection, its severity, the patient's age and kidney function, and the form of the medication (oral tablets, intravenous infusion).  It's crucial to follow a doctor's prescription precisely.

**Side Effects:** Common side effects include nausea, diarrhea, headache, and dizziness.  Less common but serious side effects can include tendonitis, tendon rupture, and allergic reactions.

**How it Works:** Ofloxacin works by inhibiting an enzyme essential for bacterial DNA replication and repair, leading to bacterial cell death.

**Precautions:** Ofloxacin should be avoided in individuals with known hypersensitivity to fluoroquinolones. It should be used cautiously in pregnant or breastfeeding women and those with kidney or liver problems.


### Ornidazole

**Generic Name:** Ornidazole

**Usage:** Ornidazole is a nitroimidazole antibiotic used to treat infections caused by anaerobic bacteria and protozoa.  It is commonly prescribed for bacterial vaginosis, trichomoniasis, and amoebiasis.

**Dosage:**  Dosage depends on the infection being treated and patient factors. It's administered orally.

**Side Effects:** Common side effects can include nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.  More serious side effects are less common.

**How it Works:** Ornidazole interferes with the DNA of anaerobic microorganisms, leading to their destruction.

**Precautions:** Ornidazole should be used with caution in pregnant or breastfeeding women and individuals with liver disease.


### Terbinafine

**Generic Name:** Terbinafine

**Usage:** Terbinafine is an antifungal medication primarily used to treat fungal skin infections like athlete's foot, ringworm, and jock itch. It can also be used for onychomycosis (fungal nail infections).

**Dosage:** Dosage varies depending on the infection and the form of the medication (cream, tablet, oral solution).

**Side Effects:** Common side effects may include skin rash, nausea, and headache.  Less common but serious side effects include liver damage.

**How it Works:** Terbinafine interferes with the synthesis of ergosterol, a vital component of fungal cell membranes, leading to fungal cell death.

**Precautions:**  Terbinafine should be used cautiously in individuals with liver problems.


### Mometasone

**Generic Name:** Mometasone

**Usage:** Mometasone is a corticosteroid primarily used as a topical treatment for inflammatory skin conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and allergic dermatitis. It's available as a cream, ointment, or nasal spray.

**Dosage:** Dosage varies depending on the condition and the area being treated.

**Side Effects:** Common side effects with topical use include skin burning, itching, and thinning of the skin.

**How it Works:** Mometasone reduces inflammation by suppressing the activity of inflammatory cells.
