# Ondansetron + Ranitidine

**Generic Name:** Ondansetron + Ranitidine (This is not a standard combination marketed as a single product.  Ondansetron and Ranitidine are typically prescribed separately.)

**Usage:**  Because Ondansetron and Ranitidine are not typically combined in a single product, their usage is described separately.

* **Ondansetron:** Used to prevent nausea and vomiting caused by chemotherapy, radiation therapy, or surgery. It can also be used to treat postoperative nausea and vomiting (PONV) and nausea and vomiting associated with other conditions.
* **Ranitidine:** Used to treat and prevent ulcers in the stomach and duodenum, reduce stomach acid production, and relieve heartburn, acid indigestion, and sour stomach. It is also used to treat gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and Zollinger-Ellison syndrome.

**Side Effects:** Again, because these are usually given separately, side effects are listed separately:

* **Ondansetron:** Common side effects include headache, constipation, diarrhea, dizziness, drowsiness.  Less common but more serious side effects include prolonged QT interval (heart rhythm problems), serotonin syndrome (a rare but serious condition), and liver damage.
* **Ranitidine:** Common side effects include headache, constipation, diarrhea, dizziness. Less common side effects can include abdominal pain, confusion, and  rare cases of serious liver problems.  *Note: Ranitidine was withdrawn from the market in many countries due to concerns about NDMA, a cancer-causing impurity.  If you have a prescription for Ranitidine, consult your doctor.*

**How it Works:**

* **Ondansetron:** Blocks the action of serotonin in the brain, a neurotransmitter that can trigger nausea and vomiting.
* **Ranitidine:** Blocks histamine H2 receptors in the stomach, reducing the production of stomach acid.
