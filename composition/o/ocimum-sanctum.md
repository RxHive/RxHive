# Ocimum sanctum
### Generic Name
Ocimum sanctum (Holy Basil, Tulsi)

### Usage

Ocimum sanctum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a plant used in traditional medicine systems, particularly Ayurveda.  While it has a long history of use for various ailments,  scientific evidence supporting many of these claims is limited and often comes from pre-clinical studies (lab or animal studies) rather than large-scale human clinical trials.  

Traditionally, it's been used to address conditions such as:

* **Respiratory issues:**  Cough, cold, bronchitis, asthma (though clinical evidence is lacking for these uses).
* **Stress and anxiety:** Some studies suggest potential adaptogenic properties, meaning it might help the body adapt to stress. However, more research is needed to confirm these effects in humans.
* **Inflammatory conditions:**  It shows anti-inflammatory properties in lab settings, potentially beneficial for conditions involving inflammation, but human trials are limited.
* **Diabetes:**  Some studies indicate potential blood sugar-lowering effects, but robust clinical data in humans is lacking.
* **Gastrointestinal issues:** Traditionally used for digestive problems, but again, evidence from human trials is needed.

**Important Note:** While Ocimum sanctum has been used for centuries, it's crucial to remember that it's not a substitute for conventional medical treatment.  Always consult a healthcare professional before using it to treat any medical condition.


### Dosage

There's no universally established dosage for Ocimum sanctum due to the limited clinical trials.  Traditional use often involves consuming the leaves as a tea or using extracts in varying concentrations.  Dosage and preparation methods vary widely depending on the intended use and the specific product (tea, extract, capsules etc.).  **It's essential to follow the instructions provided on the product label, if available, and consult a healthcare professional or qualified herbalist for guidance on appropriate dosage.**  There is no established safe dosage for children.


### Side Effects

Common side effects are generally mild and infrequent, but may include:

* Mild gastrointestinal upset (nausea, diarrhea)
* Allergic reactions (skin rash, itching)

Serious side effects are rare but possible.  These might include interactions with other medications.

**If you experience any adverse effects, discontinue use and consult a healthcare provider immediately.**

### Precautions

* **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:**  The safety of Ocimum sanctum during pregnancy and breastfeeding is not fully established.  It's best to avoid use during these periods unless advised otherwise by a healthcare professional.
