# Oxybutynin

**Generic Name:** Oxybutynin

**Usage:**

Oxybutynin is an anticholinergic medication primarily used to treat overactive bladder (OAB).  OAB symptoms include urinary urgency, frequency, and urge incontinence. It's also sometimes used to treat other conditions involving bladder spasms, such as interstitial cystitis.  It's available in several forms including immediate-release tablets, extended-release tablets, and a topical patch. The specific dosage and form will depend on the individual and their condition, as determined by a doctor.

* **Urinary retention:** Difficulty emptying the bladder.
* **Allergic reactions:**  Rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing.


**How it Works:**

Oxybutynin works by blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates muscle contractions in the bladder. By blocking acetylcholine, oxybutynin relaxes the bladder muscles, reducing spasms and improving bladder control. This helps to decrease the frequency of urination and reduce urinary urgency and incontinence.
