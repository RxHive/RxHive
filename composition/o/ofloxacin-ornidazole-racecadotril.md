# Ofloxacin + Ornidazole + Racecadotril

**Generic Name:** Ofloxacin + Ornidazole + Racecadotril

**Usage:** This combination drug is typically used to treat infectious diarrhea, particularly diarrhea caused by bacterial and protozoal infections.  The specific bacteria and protozoa targeted vary, but often include those common causes of infectious gastroenteritis.  It's important to note that this combination is not a first-line treatment for all types of diarrhea and should only be used under the guidance of a physician.  Self-medication is strongly discouraged.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary depending on the individual and the specific dosage. Common side effects may include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (paradoxically, even though it treats diarrhea), abdominal pain, constipation, flatulence.
* **Nervous System:** Headache, dizziness, drowsiness, insomnia.
* **Skin:** Rash, itching.
* **Other:**  Taste disturbances, changes in liver function tests (rare but requires monitoring).  More severe allergic reactions are possible, though rare.


**How it Works:** This combination drug leverages the properties of its three components:

* **Ofloxacin:** A fluoroquinolone antibiotic. It works by inhibiting bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, essential enzymes for bacterial DNA replication and cell division, thus killing bacteria.
* **Ornidazole:** A nitroimidazole antiprotozoal and antibacterial agent.  It acts by damaging the DNA of susceptible microorganisms, leading to their death. It's effective against certain bacteria and protozoa that cause diarrhea.
* **Racecadotril:** An antidiarrheal agent. It acts by inhibiting the release of enkephalins, reducing intestinal motility and decreasing the frequency and urgency of bowel movements. This helps to manage the symptoms of diarrhea but doesn't treat the underlying infection.
