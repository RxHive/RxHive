# Oxycodone + Naltrexone
### Generic Name
Oxycodone + Naltrexone

### Usage
Oxycodone + Naltrexone is a combination medication used for the management of chronic pain that is severe enough to require long-term opioid treatment and for which other treatment options haven't been effective. ### How it Works
This combination medication leverages two different opioid mechanisms:

* **Oxycodone:** Acts as an opioid agonist, binding to mu-opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord. This interaction reduces the perception of pain signals.
* **Naltrexone:** Acts as an opioid antagonist, blocking the effects of other opioids, including oxycodone itself, at the mu-opioid receptor. However, the formulation would have been designed to minimize the antagonistic effect of Naltrexone while still acting as a deterrent to abuse.


The combined effect aimed to provide pain relief while making the medication less appealing for misuse.  By including naltrexone, manipulating or crushing the tablet to extract oxycodone for injection or other forms of abuse would have resulted in a reduced or blocked analgesic effect, negating the potential for abuse.
