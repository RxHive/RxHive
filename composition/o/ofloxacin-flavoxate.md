# Ofloxacin + Flavoxate

**Generic Name:** Ofloxacin + Flavoxate

**Usage:** This combination drug is typically prescribed for urinary tract infections (UTIs) that are accompanied by symptoms of overactive bladder, such as urgency, frequency, and incontinence.  The ofloxacin component targets the bacterial infection, while the flavoxate helps to relax the bladder muscles and reduce the associated bladder symptoms.  It's crucial to note that this combination isn't used for all UTIs; its use is specific to infections accompanied by bothersome bladder symptoms.  The prescribing physician will determine if this combination is appropriate based on the individual's condition.

**Side Effects:** Side effects can vary from person to person but may include:

* **From Ofloxacin:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, insomnia, photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), tendonitis (inflammation of tendons), rarely, seizures.
* **From Flavoxate:** Dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision, drowsiness, dizziness, urinary retention (difficulty urinating), increased heart rate.
* **Combined Side Effects:**  The combination may increase the likelihood or severity of some side effects listed above.


**How it Works:**

* **Ofloxacin:** This is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic. It works by interfering with the bacteria's DNA replication and repair mechanisms, ultimately killing the bacteria and resolving the infection.
* **Flavoxate:** This is an antispasmodic drug. It acts by relaxing the smooth muscles in the bladder, reducing spasms and relieving the symptoms of overactive bladder.  It doesn't directly treat the infection but helps manage the associated discomfort.
