# Ornipressin
### Generic Name
Ornipressin

### Usage
Ornipressin is a synthetic analog of vasopressin, primarily used as a hemostatic agent during surgical procedures.  Its main purpose is to induce ischemia and hemostasis (stop bleeding) in localized areas. This is achieved by causing vasoconstriction, reducing blood flow to the targeted surgical site.  While primarily used for hemostasis, it's important to note that Ornipressin's use is highly specialized and limited to surgical settings.  It is not used for general bleeding control or in non-surgical situations.

### Dosage
Ornipressin is administered via infiltration or topical application during surgery. The dosage is highly variable depending on the surgical site and the surgeon's judgment.  A typical maximum dose is 5 units.  The manufacturer's instructions usually recommend diluting the medication before use with normal saline (NS).  The dilution ratio varies depending on the type of surgery:

* **ENT surgery:** 5 units (one ampule) diluted in 20 mL NS.
* **Gynecologic surgery, laparotomy:** 5 units (one ampule) diluted in 50-60 mL NS.
* **Other surgeries:** 5 units (one ampule) diluted in 30 mL NS.

There are no specific dosage adjustments for hepatic or renal impairment mentioned in the manufacturer's labeling.  Pediatric and geriatric dosing information isn't available.  It is crucial that a qualified healthcare professional determines the appropriate dosage and dilution for each individual case.

### Side Effects
The frequency of the following side effects is not clearly defined in available information. Possible side effects include:

* **Cardiovascular:** Altered blood pressure, angina pectoris, cardiac arrhythmias, cardiovascular toxicity, decreased heart rate, and increased heart rate.
* **Dermatologic:** Allergic skin reactions, diaphoresis (excessive sweating), and pallor (pale skin).
* **Gastrointestinal:** Increased colonic motility.
* **Ophthalmic:** Exophthalmos (protruding eyeballs).
* **Respiratory:** Cyanosis (bluish discoloration of the skin).


Any unusual or concerning side effects should be reported to a doctor immediately.

### How it Works
Ornipressin works by binding to V1A receptors on vascular smooth muscle cells, causing vasoconstriction. This constriction reduces blood flow to the treated area, leading to hemostasis.  At very high concentrations, after V1A receptors are saturated, Ornipressin may also bind to V2 receptors, potentially leading to a paradoxical vasodilatory effect. Although Ornipressin possesses antidiuretic activity, it's considerably weaker than that of vasopressin.

### Precautions
Ornipressin is contraindicated (absolutely not to be used) in patients with:

* Hypersensitivity to Ornipressin or any of its components.
* Coronary heart disease.
* Severe hypertension (including preeclampsia).
* Pregnancy.
* Advanced arteriosclerosis.
* Epilepsy.
* Use as an adjunct to local anesthetics in terminal arteries (e.g., fingers) and spinal anesthesia.

Caution is advised in patients with ischemic heart disease.  The drug should be used cautiously in breastfeeding mothers as excretion in breast milk is unknown.
