**Usage:** This combination is typically used to treat bacterial vaginosis (BV), a common vaginal infection.  The specific application is based on the physician's assessment and may target other gynecological infections depending on the clinical scenario.  It's crucial to understand that this isn't a first-line treatment for BV in most cases;  Metronidazole or Clindamycin alone are usually preferred.  This combination might be used in cases of recurrent BV or when other treatments have failed. The lactic acid bacilli are included to help restore the normal vaginal flora.

**Side Effects:**

* **Ofloxacin:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, insomnia, photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling).  Rarely, tendonitis (inflammation of the tendons) or tendon rupture.
* **Metronidazole:** Nausea, vomiting, metallic taste in the mouth, diarrhea, headache, dizziness, dark urine, decreased white blood cell count (rare).  Alcohol consumption should be avoided during treatment and for several days afterward due to a disulfiram-like reaction (severe nausea and vomiting).
* **Lactic acid bacilli:** Generally well-tolerated.  Rarely, mild gastrointestinal upset.  Allergic reactions are possible, although uncommon.

**How it Works:**

* **Ofloxacin:** A fluoroquinolone antibiotic that works by inhibiting bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication and repair. This leads to bacterial cell death.
* **Metronidazole:** A nitroimidazole antibiotic that enters bacterial cells and disrupts their DNA, leading to cell death.  It is effective against anaerobic bacteria commonly implicated in BV.
* **Lactic acid bacilli:**  These are beneficial bacteria that help to restore the natural acidic pH of the vagina.  This acidic environment inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria, preventing recurrence of BV.  By restoring the normal vaginal flora, they contribute to a healthier vaginal environment.
