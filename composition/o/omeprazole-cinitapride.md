# Omeprazole + Cinitapride

**Generic Name:** Omeprazole + Cinitapride (This is not an officially recognized combination)

**Usage:**  This combination is *not* standard usage.  Omeprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) used to reduce stomach acid production, commonly treating conditions like GERD, ulcers, and gastritis. Cinitapride is a prokinetic agent used to improve gastric motility (the movement of food through the digestive system).  It's sometimes used in conditions involving delayed gastric emptying.  However, its use is limited in many regions due to safety concerns. * **Cinitapride:**  This drug carries a significant risk of cardiac arrhythmias (irregular heartbeat), particularly torsades de pointes, a potentially fatal condition.  Other side effects can include extrapyramidal symptoms (movement disorders), drowsiness, and headache.

The combined use increases the risk of interaction and side effects, especially the cardiac risks associated with Cinitapride.

**How it Works:**

* **Cinitapride:**  Increases the motility of the stomach and upper intestines by acting on dopamine receptors. This helps to move food through the digestive tract more quickly.

The theoretical rationale for combining them would be to reduce acid while simultaneously improving gastric emptying. However, this combination increases risk without clear clinical advantage.
