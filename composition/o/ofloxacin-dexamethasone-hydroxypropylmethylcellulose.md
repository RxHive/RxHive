# Ofloxacin + Dexamethasone + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose

**Generic Name:** Ofloxacin + Dexamethasone + Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose (Ophthalmic)

**Usage:** This combination medication is used to treat bacterial infections of the eye, specifically conjunctivitis (pinkeye) and other eye infections.  Ofloxacin is an antibiotic that kills bacteria, dexamethasone is a corticosteroid that reduces inflammation and swelling, and hydroxypropyl methylcellulose is a lubricating agent that helps to soothe and moisturize the eye.  It's typically prescribed for moderate to severe infections where both antibacterial and anti-inflammatory actions are needed.  It's crucial to only use this medication as directed by a doctor.  It is not appropriate for all types of eye infections (e.g., viral infections).


**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary in severity and frequency.  Common side effects may include:

* Burning or stinging sensation in the eye upon application.
* Itching
* Temporary blurred vision
* Eye redness
* Eye discharge
* Dry eyes (ironically, despite the presence of a lubricating agent, the medication itself can sometimes disrupt the natural tear film)
* Increased intraocular pressure (in rare cases)


Less common, but more serious side effects, warrant immediate medical attention and may include:

* Severe eye pain
* Vision changes (significant worsening or loss of vision)
* Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Development of secondary fungal or viral infection (due to suppression of natural immune responses by the corticosteroid)


**How it Works:**

* **Ofloxacin:** This fluoroquinolone antibiotic works by inhibiting bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication and cell division. This ultimately leads to bacterial cell death.
* **Dexamethasone:** This corticosteroid reduces inflammation by suppressing the body's inflammatory response. It decreases the production of inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins, thus reducing swelling, redness, and pain.
* **Hydroxypropylmethylcellulose:** This is a viscous polymer that acts as a lubricant and viscosity-increasing agent. It helps to coat and protect the eye surface, preventing further irritation and dryness.
