# Ornidazole + Ofloxacin + Lactobacillus

**Generic Name:** Ornidazole + Ofloxacin + Lactobacillus (This is a combination of three drugs, not a single generic name)

**Usage:** This combination is typically used to treat bacterial infections of the gastrointestinal tract, particularly those involving *Helicobacter pylori* (a bacterium that causes stomach ulcers) and other susceptible bacteria. The specific indications may vary depending on the formulation and prescribing physician.  Ornidazole is an antiprotozoal and antibacterial agent; Ofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic; and Lactobacillus is a probiotic. The combination aims to eliminate the infection while also replenishing beneficial gut bacteria that might be disrupted by the antibiotics.


**Side Effects:** The side effects can vary depending on the individual and the specific formulation.  They may include:

* **Ornidazole:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, dizziness, metallic taste, abdominal pain, decreased white blood cell count (leukopenia), and rarely, peripheral neuropathy (nerve damage).  
* **Ofloxacin:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, headache, dizziness, insomnia, photosensitivity (increased sensitivity to sunlight), allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), tendonitis or tendon rupture (rare but serious),  and rarely, seizures.
* **Lactobacillus:** Generally well-tolerated, but some individuals might experience mild gas or bloating.


**How it Works:**

* **Ornidazole:** Works by interfering with the DNA of bacteria and protozoa, ultimately killing them. It has a broader spectrum of activity than some other antiprotozoals.
* **Ofloxacin:** Works by inhibiting DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, enzymes essential for bacterial DNA replication and cell division. This leads to bacterial death.
* **Lactobacillus:** Lactobacillus is a probiotic bacterium that helps restore the normal balance of gut flora.  Antibiotics can disrupt this balance, leading to diarrhea or other digestive issues; lactobacillus helps to counteract this.  It competes with harmful bacteria for nutrients and space, preventing overgrowth of harmful bacteria.
