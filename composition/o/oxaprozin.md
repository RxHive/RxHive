# Oxaprozin

**Generic Name:** Oxaprozin

**Usage:**  Oxaprozin is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve the symptoms of osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.  This includes reducing pain, swelling, stiffness, and inflammation in the joints.  It's typically prescribed for long-term use in managing chronic conditions.

**Side Effects:**  Like other NSAIDs, oxaprozin can cause a range of side effects, varying in severity from mild to serious. Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:**  Heartburn, indigestion, nausea, vomiting, constipation, diarrhea, abdominal pain, ulcers (including stomach and intestinal bleeding).
* **Central Nervous System:** Dizziness, headache, drowsiness, insomnia.
* **Cardiovascular:** Fluid retention, high blood pressure, increased risk of heart attack or stroke (especially with long-term use or high doses).
* **Allergic Reactions:** Skin rash, itching, hives, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (angioedema).  In rare cases, severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) can occur.
* **Kidney:**  Kidney problems, including decreased kidney function.
* **Liver:** Liver damage (rare but serious).
* **Blood:** Anemia (decreased red blood cells).
* **Other:**  Increased risk of bleeding, tinnitus (ringing in the ears).


This is not an exhaustive list, and the severity and likelihood of side effects vary depending on individual factors and dosage.

**How it Works:** Oxaprozin works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which are chemicals in the body that cause pain, inflammation, and fever. By reducing prostaglandin production, oxaprozin helps to relieve the symptoms of arthritis.
