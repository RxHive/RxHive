# Albendazole 500mg

**Generic Name:** Albendazole

**Usage:** Albendazole is an anti-parasitic medication used to treat a variety of worm infestations (helminthiasis), including:

* **Nematode infections:**  Roundworm infections such as ascariasis (Ascaris lumbricoides), hookworm infections (Ancylostoma duodenale and Necator americanus), enterobiasis (pinworms, Enterobius vermicularis), trichuriasis (whipworm, Trichuris trichiura), and strongyloidiasis (Strongyloides stercoralis).
* **Cestode infections:** Tapeworm infections such as taeniasis (Taenia saginata, Taenia solium), and cysticercosis (caused by the larval stage of Taenia solium).
* **Trematode infections:**  Certain fluke infections (although often other drugs are preferred).
* **Hydatid disease:** Caused by the larval stage of Echinococcus.  Treatment often requires a longer course and may be combined with surgery.


**Side Effects:**  The most common side effects are generally mild and include:

* **Gastrointestinal:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain
* **Headache**
* **Dizziness**
* **Fatigue**
* **Increased liver enzymes (liver function tests)** - This requires monitoring, especially with higher doses or prolonged use.
* **Hair loss** (alopecia) - Usually reversible.
* **Bone marrow suppression** - Rare but serious; characterized by decreased white blood cells, red blood cells, or platelets.
* **Allergic reactions:** Rash, itching, swelling.


**How it Works:** Albendazole interferes with the ability of parasitic worms to absorb glucose, which is essential for their survival.  This leads to paralysis and eventual death of the parasites.  It disrupts the microtubules within the worm's cells, affecting their nutrient uptake and reproduction.


**FAQs:**

* **Q: How is Albendazole administered?** A: Albendazole is usually taken orally, with or without food, as directed by a healthcare professional. Dosage and duration vary depending on the type and severity of the infection.

Children's dosages are lower and should be strictly followed.

* **Q: What are the long-term effects?** A: Long-term use of albendazole can increase the risk of liver damage and bone marrow suppression. Regular monitoring of liver function tests is usually recommended during prolonged treatment.
* **Q: How effective is Albendazole?** A: The effectiveness of Albendazole varies depending on the type of parasite and the individual's response to the medication.  In many cases, it's highly effective, but treatment failure can occur.
