# Acyclovir 500mg

**Generic Name:** Acyclovir

**Usage:**

Acyclovir is an antiviral medication used to treat infections caused by herpes viruses, including:

* **Genital herpes:**  Reduces the duration and severity of outbreaks.  It doesn't cure herpes, but it can suppress outbreaks and lessen symptoms.
* **Oral herpes (cold sores):** Shortens the duration and severity of cold sores.
* **Shingles (herpes zoster):** Reduces the pain and duration of shingles outbreaks.
* **Chickenpox (varicella):** Treats chickenpox, especially in immunocompromised individuals.
* **Herpes encephalitis:** A severe brain infection caused by herpes simplex virus.
* **Herpes infections in newborns:**  Acyclovir is used in neonates with serious herpes infections.


The specific dosage and duration of treatment will vary depending on the type and severity of the infection, the patient's age, and overall health, as determined by a doctor.  **Acyclovir should only be taken as prescribed by a healthcare professional.**  Self-treating can be dangerous and may lead to ineffective treatment or the development of resistant viruses.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of acyclovir can include:

* Headache
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Dizziness
* Fatigue

Less common but more serious side effects can occur, including:

* Kidney problems (especially with high doses or impaired kidney function)
* Nervous system problems (confusion, hallucinations, seizures, tremors)
* Anemia
* Low blood cell counts
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)

**It is crucial to contact a doctor immediately if you experience any serious side effects.**


**How it Works:**

Acyclovir is a nucleoside analog.  This means it's a synthetic version of a building block of DNA.  It works by interfering with the ability of herpes viruses to replicate their DNA.  Specifically, acyclovir is converted into acyclovir monophosphate by viral thymidine kinase (an enzyme found in herpes viruses, but not in human cells). This monophosphate is then further converted into acyclovir triphosphate which inhibits viral DNA polymerase, preventing the virus from replicating. Because it primarily affects viral cells, it's less toxic to human cells.
