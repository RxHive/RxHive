# Adenosine 3mg

**Generic Name:** Adenosine

**Usage:**

Adenosine is used to diagnose certain types of rapid heartbeats (supraventricular tachycardias, SVTs).  It's administered intravenously (IV) by a healthcare professional, typically in a hospital setting, to temporarily slow the heart rate and allow for better diagnosis of the rhythm disturbance. It is *not* used to treat the rapid heartbeat long-term.  Its use is primarily diagnostic.

**Side Effects:**

Adenosine has a very short half-life, meaning its effects are brief.  However, side effects can occur, though they are usually temporary and mild.  These include:

* **Chest pressure or tightness:** This is the most common side effect and is often described as a feeling of impending doom, though it's typically harmless and resolves quickly.
* **Shortness of breath:**  Also common and usually transient.
* **Flushing (redness of the face):** A temporary vasodilation effect.
* **Nausea:**  Less common.
* **Headache:** Less common.
* **Dizziness:** Less common.
* **Asystole (temporary complete heart block):**  A rare but serious side effect, requiring immediate medical attention. This is why adenosine is always administered in a controlled medical setting.

**How it Works:**

Adenosine is a naturally occurring nucleoside that slows the conduction of electrical impulses through the heart.  It works by briefly binding to adenosine receptors in the heart's electrical conduction system, slowing the rate at which the heart's atria and ventricles contract. This temporary slowing allows cardiologists to better analyze the heart rhythm and determine the underlying cause of the rapid heartbeat.  The effect is short-lived because adenosine is rapidly metabolized by the body.
