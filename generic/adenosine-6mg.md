# Adenosine 6mg

**Generic Name:** Adenosine

**Usage:**

Adenosine is a medication primarily used to diagnose and treat certain types of rapid heartbeats (tachyarrhythmias), specifically those originating in the atrioventricular (AV) node or atria.  It's used intravenously (IV) in a hospital setting, typically during a cardiac electrophysiology study or when a rapid heartbeat needs immediate treatment.  Its use is generally restricted to emergencies or controlled medical settings due to its short half-life and potential side effects.  It's not used for chronic treatment of arrhythmias.

**Rare but serious side effects** include:

* **Bradycardia:**  A slow heart rate.
* **Asystole:**  Complete absence of electrical activity in the heart.
* **AV block:**  A disruption in the electrical conduction pathway between the atria and ventricles.
* **Seizures:**  Uncontrolled electrical activity in the brain.


**How it Works:**

Adenosine is a naturally occurring nucleoside that slows down the electrical conduction of the heart.  It does this by binding to adenosine receptors in the heart, specifically the A1 receptors.  This slows the rate at which electrical impulses travel through the AV node, which is responsible for conducting electrical signals between the atria and the ventricles of the heart.  This slowing can terminate certain types of tachyarrhythmias.  The effects are very short-lived, usually lasting only seconds to minutes.
