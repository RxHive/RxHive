# Aliskiren 300mg

**Generic Name:** Aliskiren

**Usage:** Aliskiren is used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension) in adults.  It may be used alone or in combination with other blood pressure medications.  It is specifically a renin inhibitor.  The physician will determine the appropriate dosage and combination therapy based on individual needs and response.

**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, aliskiren can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them.  Common side effects include:

* **Digestive issues:** Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
* **Dizziness or lightheadedness:** This is often more pronounced at the beginning of treatment.
* **Muscle cramps or pain**
* **Fatigue or weakness**
* **Cough (less common than with ACE inhibitors or ARBs)**
* **Hyperkalemia (high potassium levels in the blood):** This is a more serious side effect and can be life-threatening.  Regular blood tests are often necessary to monitor potassium levels.

Less common but more serious side effects include:

* **Angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat):** This is a medical emergency and requires immediate medical attention.
* **Hypotension (low blood pressure):** Can cause dizziness, fainting, or lightheadedness.


**How it Works:** Aliskiren is a direct renin inhibitor. Renin is an enzyme in the body that plays a crucial role in the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS).  This system regulates blood pressure.  By inhibiting renin, aliskiren blocks the production of angiotensin I, a substance that leads to vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels) and increased blood pressure.  By reducing angiotensin I production, aliskiren helps to lower blood pressure.
