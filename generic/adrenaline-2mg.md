# Adrenaline 2mg

**Generic Name:** Epinephrine (Adrenaline is the brand name)

**Usage:** Epinephrine 2mg is primarily used in emergency situations to treat life-threatening allergic reactions (anaphylaxis), including those caused by insect stings, medications, or food allergies. It can also be used to treat certain types of cardiac arrest and severe asthma attacks.  It's crucial to understand that self-administration of epinephrine should only be done with proper training and under the guidance of a physician. It's generally administered via auto-injector (like an EpiPen or Auvi-Q).  It is *not* for routine or preventative use.

**Side Effects:**  Epinephrine can cause several side effects, some mild and some serious.  These can include:

* **Common:** Increased heart rate, palpitations, high blood pressure, headache, dizziness, sweating, nausea, vomiting, trembling, anxiety, feeling restless or nervous.
* **Serious (requiring immediate medical attention):** Chest pain, irregular heartbeat, difficulty breathing, severe dizziness or lightheadedness, stroke.  Severe allergic reactions to the epinephrine itself are rare but possible.

**How it Works:** Epinephrine acts on the body's alpha and beta adrenergic receptors.  This action leads to several effects:

* **Increased heart rate and blood pressure:** Improves blood flow to vital organs.
* **Relaxation of airway muscles:** Opens the airways, relieving breathing difficulties.
* **Contraction of blood vessels:** Reduces swelling, particularly in the case of anaphylaxis.
