# Acenocoumarol 2mg

**Generic Name:** Acenocoumarol

**Usage:**

Acenocoumarol is an anticoagulant medication, meaning it prevents blood clots from forming.  It's primarily used for:

* **Long-term prevention of venous thromboembolism (VTE):** This includes deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE), often after surgery or due to a predisposition.
* **Prevention of recurrent thromboembolic events:** In patients who have already experienced a blood clot.
* **Atrial fibrillation (AFib) prophylaxis:**  Helps to prevent stroke in patients with atrial fibrillation.
* **Treatment of other thromboembolic disorders:**  As directed by a physician, in specific situations where blood clot formation is a risk.

**How it Works:**

Acenocoumarol is a vitamin K antagonist.  Vitamin K is essential for the synthesis of certain clotting factors in the blood. By inhibiting the action of vitamin K, acenocoumarol reduces the production of these clotting factors, thus decreasing the blood's ability to clot. This effect prevents the formation of potentially dangerous blood clots.
