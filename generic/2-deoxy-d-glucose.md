# 2-Deoxy-D-Glucose

**Generic Name:** 2-Deoxy-D-glucose (2-DG)

**Usage:**

2-Deoxy-D-glucose is primarily used as a research tool and is currently undergoing investigation for several therapeutic applications.  It's *not* widely available for general use as a medication. Its main areas of exploration include:

* **Cancer therapy:**  2-DG is being studied as a potential anti-cancer agent.  Cancer cells often exhibit high rates of glucose metabolism (Warburg effect).  2-DG interferes with this process, potentially slowing or stopping tumor growth.  It's often used in combination with other therapies.
* **Infectious disease research:**  Its ability to inhibit glucose metabolism is also being explored in the context of treating infections, particularly those caused by organisms reliant on high glucose consumption.
* **Neurological research:**  2-DG's effects on glucose metabolism are being investigated in relation to neurological conditions and disorders.

It's crucial to note that while promising, 2-DG's clinical efficacy in these areas is still under investigation, and it's not approved for widespread therapeutic use in most countries.
