# Abacavir 600mg  + Lamivudine 300mg

**Generic Name:** Abacavir/Lamivudine

**Usage:**

Abacavir/Lamivudine is a combination antiretroviral drug used to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection in adults and children. It's typically part of a combination therapy regimen, meaning it's used with other antiretroviral medications.  It is NOT a cure for HIV, but it helps to suppress the virus, reducing the viral load and improving the CD4 cell count (a type of white blood cell important for the immune system). This helps to slow the progression of HIV to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS) and improves the quality of life for people living with HIV.

**Side Effects:**

* **Nausea:** Can be accompanied by vomiting.
* **Diarrhea:** Can range from mild to severe.
* **Fatigue:** Feeling tired and weak.

* **Liver problems:**  Elevated liver enzymes may occur, though usually mild.
* **Lactic acidosis:**  A rare but serious side effect involving a buildup of lactic acid in the blood. This can be life-threatening.

This is not an exhaustive list of side effects.  Consult your doctor or pharmacist for a complete list and information specific to you.


**How it Works:**

Abacavir and lamivudine are nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NRTIs).  They work by interfering with the HIV virus's ability to replicate itself.  Specifically:

* **Abacavir:** Blocks the activity of reverse transcriptase, an enzyme HIV needs to convert its RNA into DNA.  Without this conversion, the virus cannot integrate its genetic material into the host cell's DNA and replicate.
* **Lamivudine:** Also blocks reverse transcriptase, preventing the virus from replicating.  It works in a slightly different way than abacavir.

By working together, abacavir and lamivudine effectively suppress HIV replication.
