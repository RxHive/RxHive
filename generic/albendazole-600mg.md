# Albendazole 600mg

**Generic Name:** Albendazole

**Usage:** Albendazole is an anthelmintic (anti-worm) medication used to treat a variety of parasitic worm infections, including:

* **Nematode infections:**  Roundworm infections like ascariasis (roundworm), hookworm infections (ancylostomiasis and necatoriasis), whipworm (trichuriasis), and strongyloidiasis (threadworm).
* **Cestode infections:** Tapeworm infections like taeniasis (tapeworm) and cysticercosis (larval tapeworm).
* **Protozoan infections:**  In combination with other drugs, it's sometimes used against certain protozoan infections, such as hydatid disease (caused by *Echinococcus* species).


**Note:** The specific usage and dosage will depend on the type of infection, the severity, the patient's age and weight, and other factors.  A doctor should always prescribe and supervise the use of albendazole.  Self-treating parasitic infections can be dangerous.


**Side Effects:** Albendazole is generally well-tolerated, but side effects can occur. These can include:

* **Common side effects:**  Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, headache, dizziness, hair loss (alopecia).
* **Less common side effects:**  Fever, rash, itching, elevated liver enzymes (indicated by abnormal liver function tests).
* **Rare but serious side effects:**  Bone marrow suppression (reduction in blood cell production), liver damage, Stevens-Johnson syndrome (a severe skin reaction), pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas).

It is crucial to report any unusual or concerning side effects to a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:** Albendazole interferes with the worm's ability to absorb glucose, a vital energy source.  This leads to the paralysis and eventual death of the parasite.  It also interferes with the worm's microtubules, affecting their growth and reproduction.
