# Alfuzosin 5mg

**Generic Name:** Alfuzosin

**Usage:**

Alfuzosin is used to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), also known as enlarged prostate.  It relaxes the muscles in the prostate and bladder neck, making urination easier.  It's specifically used to improve urinary symptoms associated with BPH, such as difficulty starting urination, weak urine stream, frequent urination, especially at night (nocturia), and the sensation of incomplete bladder emptying.  It does *not* treat the underlying cause of BPH.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects of alfuzosin can include:

* Dizziness
* Lightheadedness
* Headache
* Fatigue
* Nausea
* Sleep disturbances
* Runny nose
* Back pain
* Orthostatic hypotension (a sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing, leading to dizziness or fainting) - this is particularly important for older adults.

Less common but more serious side effects can include:

* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing)
* Priapism (a persistent, painful erection)
* Fast heartbeat
* Swelling of the ankles or feet
* Chest pain

If you experience any serious side effects, seek immediate medical attention.  This list is not exhaustive, and you should consult your doctor or pharmacist for a complete list of potential side effects.

**How it Works:**

Alfuzosin is an alpha-1 adrenergic receptor blocker.  The prostate and bladder neck contain alpha-1 adrenergic receptors.  By blocking these receptors, alfuzosin relaxes the muscles in these areas, reducing the resistance to urine flow and improving urinary symptoms.
