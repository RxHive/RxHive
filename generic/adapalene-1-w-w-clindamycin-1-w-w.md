# Adapalene 1% w/w + Clindamycin 1% w/w

**Generic Name:** Adapalene 1% w/w + Clindamycin 1% w/w  (This is a combination product, not a single generic name)

**Usage:** This topical medication is used to treat acne vulgaris, specifically inflammatory acne (pimples, pustules) and comedonal acne (blackheads, whiteheads).  It works by combining two active ingredients with different mechanisms of action to provide a broader approach to acne treatment.

**Side Effects:**  Side effects can vary in severity and frequency. Common side effects include:

* **Adapalene-related:** Dryness, peeling, redness, burning, itching, stinging at the application site.  Rarely, severe skin irritation may occur.  Increased sun sensitivity is also possible.
* **Clindamycin-related:** Mild skin irritation (redness, burning, itching),  diarrhea (though less common with topical application).  Rarely, allergic reactions (rash, hives, difficulty breathing) may occur.

**Important Note:**  This list isn't exhaustive. Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you experience any unusual or concerning side effects.  Stop use and seek medical attention immediately if you experience severe allergic reactions.


**How it Works:**

* **Adapalene:** This is a retinoid, a derivative of vitamin A. It works by increasing skin cell turnover, preventing clogged pores (comedones), and reducing inflammation. It helps to unclog pores, preventing the formation of acne lesions.

* **Clindamycin:** This is an antibiotic. It works by killing the bacteria *Cutibacterium acnes* (formerly *Propionibacterium acnes*), which is a key contributor to inflammatory acne.  It reduces the bacterial load, thus lessening inflammation.

The combination of adapalene and clindamycin provides a synergistic effect, addressing both the comedonal (blocked pores) and inflammatory aspects of acne.
