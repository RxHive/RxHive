# Abacavir 300mg

**Generic Name:** Abacavir

**Usage:** Abacavir is an antiretroviral medication used in combination with other medications to treat Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) infection.  It's specifically a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI).  It is *not* a cure for HIV, but it helps to reduce the amount of HIV in the body (viral load) and increase the CD4 cell count (a type of white blood cell important for the immune system).  It is typically prescribed as part of a combination antiretroviral therapy (cART) regimen, and should never be used alone.  The specific dosage and combination will be determined by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and health status.

**How it Works:** Abacavir is a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI).  HIV needs reverse transcriptase to convert its RNA into DNA, which is then incorporated into the host cell's DNA. Abacavir interferes with this process by acting as a substrate for reverse transcriptase.  It gets incorporated into the growing DNA chain, effectively terminating the process and preventing the virus from replicating.
