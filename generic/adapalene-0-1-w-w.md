# Adapalene 0.1% w/w

**Generic Name:** Adapalene

**Usage:**

Adapalene 0.1% w/w is a topical retinoid used to treat acne vulgaris.  It works by:

* **Unclogging pores:** It helps prevent the formation of comedones (blackheads and whiteheads) by promoting the normal shedding of skin cells.
* **Reducing inflammation:** It reduces inflammation in the skin, thereby lessening redness and swelling associated with acne.
* **Preventing future breakouts:** By unclogging pores and reducing inflammation, it helps prevent the formation of new acne lesions.

It's typically applied once daily, usually at night, to affected areas of the skin after washing and drying.  The specific application instructions should always be followed as directed by a doctor or pharmacist.  It's important to start slowly, perhaps applying every other night initially, and gradually increasing frequency as tolerated to minimize irritation.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and temporary, but can include:

* **Dryness:** Skin dryness, peeling, and flaking are very common.
* **Redness:**  Skin redness and irritation are also frequent.
* **Burning or stinging:** A burning or stinging sensation upon application can occur.
* **Increased sun sensitivity:** Adapalene can make your skin more sensitive to sunlight, increasing the risk of sunburn.  Using sunscreen is crucial.


Less common, but more serious side effects can include:

* **Severe skin irritation:** In rare cases, severe skin irritation or allergic reactions may occur.
* **Hyperpigmentation (darkening of the skin):**  More likely in people with darker skin tones.
* **Hypopigmentation (lightening of the skin):**  Less frequent than hyperpigmentation.


If you experience severe side effects, discontinue use and consult a doctor immediately.


**How it Works:**

Adapalene is a retinoid, a derivative of vitamin A. It works primarily by:

* **Increasing cell turnover:** It accelerates the rate at which skin cells shed, preventing pores from becoming clogged with dead skin cells and sebum (oil).
* **Reducing inflammation:** It modulates the inflammatory response in the skin, reducing redness and swelling associated with acne lesions.
* **Decreasing sebum production (to a lesser extent):**  While not its primary mechanism, it may also slightly decrease the production of sebum, the oily substance that contributes to acne.


**FAQs:**

* **How long does it take to work?**  You may not see significant improvement for several weeks, and it can take several months to achieve optimal results.  Consistency is key.

* **What should I do if I miss a dose?**  Simply apply it as soon as you remember, unless it's almost time for your next dose.  Don't double up on doses.

* **Can I use it during pregnancy or breastfeeding?**  Consult your doctor before using adapalene if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.  It's generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

* **Can I use it on my face, body, or both?**  It's commonly used on the face, but can be used on other acne-affected areas of the body as directed by a doctor.
