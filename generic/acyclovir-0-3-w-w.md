# Acyclovir 0.3% w/w

**Generic Name:** Acyclovir

**Usage:** Acyclovir 0.3% w/w is a topical antiviral medication used to treat herpes simplex virus (HSV) type 1 and type 2 infections.  This includes cold sores (HSV-1) and genital herpes (HSV-2).  It's applied directly to the affected skin.  It is *not* a cure, but it can help reduce the duration and severity of outbreaks, as well as the pain and itching associated with them.  It's important to note that the 0.3% w/w concentration is generally used for topical applications, not oral or intravenous administration.  Always follow the directions on the label or as prescribed by a doctor.

**Side Effects:** While generally well-tolerated,  topical acyclovir can cause mild side effects at the application site, including:

* Burning or stinging sensation
* Redness
* Itching
* Dryness
* Skin irritation

Rarely, more serious allergic reactions can occur.  Stop using the medication and seek immediate medical attention if you experience symptoms such as:

* Swelling of the face, lips, or tongue
* Difficulty breathing
* Severe skin rash


**How it Works:** Acyclovir is a nucleoside analog.  It works by inhibiting the replication of the herpes virus.  Specifically, it's incorporated into the viral DNA, preventing further viral DNA synthesis and thus halting the virus's ability to reproduce and spread.
