# Acenocoumarol 3mg

**Generic Name:** Acenocoumarol

**Usage:**

Acenocoumarol is an anticoagulant (blood thinner) used to prevent blood clots.  It's primarily prescribed for:

* **Prevention of venous thromboembolism (VTE):** This includes deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE), often after surgery or in patients with certain medical conditions.
* **Prevention of recurrent VTE:**  In patients who have already experienced a blood clot.
* **Atrial fibrillation (AFib):** To reduce the risk of stroke in patients with AFib.
* **Other thromboembolic conditions:** As determined by a physician.


**Important Note:** Acenocoumarol requires regular blood tests (INR monitoring) to ensure the dosage is appropriate and prevents excessive bleeding.  Dosage is highly individualized and depends on many factors.  **Never adjust the dosage yourself without consulting your doctor.**


**Side Effects:**

* **Bleeding:** This can range from minor bruising or nosebleeds to more serious internal bleeding, which can be life-threatening.
* **Gastrointestinal issues:**  Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.
* **Skin reactions:** Rash, itching.
* **Fever**
* **Purpura** (purple spots on the skin)


Less common but serious side effects include:

* **Hemorrhage (bleeding):**  This is the most serious potential side effect.
* **Anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction):** This is rare but can be life-threatening.


**How it Works:**

Acenocoumarol works by inhibiting the production of vitamin K-dependent clotting factors in the liver. Vitamin K is essential for blood clotting, and by reducing the availability of these factors, acenocoumarol decreases the blood's ability to clot, thus reducing the risk of thrombus (blood clot) formation.
