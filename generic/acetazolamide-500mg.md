# Acetazolamide 500mg

**Generic Name:** Acetazolamide

**Usage:**

Acetazolamide is a diuretic (water pill) and carbonic anhydrase inhibitor.  Its primary uses include:

* **Treatment of glaucoma:**  Reduces intraocular pressure (pressure inside the eye).  Often used in combination with other glaucoma medications.
* **Treatment of altitude sickness (acute mountain sickness):** Helps the body adapt to high altitudes by reducing fluid buildup in the brain and lungs.
* **Treatment of epilepsy:**  Used as an adjunctive therapy (along with other anticonvulsants) to control certain types of seizures, particularly those involving absences (petit mal seizures).
* **Treatment of idiopathic intracranial hypertension (pseudotumor cerebri):** Reduces pressure within the skull.
* **Treatment of metabolic alkalosis:** A condition where the body's pH is too alkaline.


**Side Effects:**

Acetazolamide can cause a range of side effects, varying in severity.  Common side effects include:

* **Tingling in the extremities (paresthesia):** A common and often mild side effect.
* **Nausea and vomiting:**  Can be mild to moderate.
* **Diarrhea:** Often resolves on its own.
* **Loss of appetite:**  May lead to weight loss.
* **Fatigue and drowsiness:** Can impair alertness and driving ability.
* **Metallic taste in the mouth:** A characteristic side effect.
* **Kidney stones:** A less common, but serious, side effect.
* **Increased urination:** Due to its diuretic effect.
* **Dehydration:** Due to increased urination; important to stay well-hydrated.
* **Electrolyte imbalances:** Can lead to serious health problems if severe.


Less common but more serious side effects include:

* **Severe allergic reactions:**  Seek immediate medical attention.
* **Liver problems:**  May require discontinuation of the medication.
* **Blood disorders:**  Monitor blood counts if taking long-term.
* **Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas):** A serious condition requiring immediate medical attention.


**How it Works:**

Acetazolamide works by inhibiting the enzyme carbonic anhydrase. This enzyme plays a crucial role in the formation of bicarbonate ions in the kidneys and eyes.  By inhibiting this enzyme, acetazolamide:

* **Reduces the production of aqueous humor in the eye:**  Lowering intraocular pressure.
* **Increases the excretion of bicarbonate and sodium in the urine:**  Acting as a diuretic and reducing fluid buildup in the body.
* **Reduces the excretion of hydrogen ions in the urine:**  Affecting the body's acid-base balance.  This effect is relevant to its use in treating metabolic alkalosis.
