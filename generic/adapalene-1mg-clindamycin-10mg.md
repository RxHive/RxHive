# Adapalene 1mg + Clindamycin 10mg

**Generic Name:** Adapalene/Clindamycin Topical Gel

**Usage:**

Adapalene/Clindamycin topical gel is a combination medication used to treat acne vulgaris (acne).  It's prescribed for mild to moderate acne that involves inflammatory lesions (pimples, papules, pustules) and comedones (blackheads and whiteheads).  The adapalene component helps prevent the formation of new comedones, while the clindamycin component reduces the bacteria (Propionibacterium acnes) that contribute to inflammation and acne breakouts.  It's typically applied once daily, at night, to the affected areas of the skin after washing and gently drying the skin.  It's crucial to follow the doctor's instructions precisely regarding application frequency and amount.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects are generally mild and may include:

* **Skin irritation:** Burning, stinging, redness, dryness, peeling, and itching at the application site. These are more common initially and usually subside with continued use.
* **Increased sun sensitivity:** The skin becomes more susceptible to sunburn.  Sunscreen use is crucial, especially during daytime.
* **Dryness:**  The skin may become excessively dry, requiring the use of a moisturizer.

Less common, but more serious side effects may include:

* **Allergic reactions:**  Rash, hives, swelling, difficulty breathing. Seek immediate medical attention if these occur.
* **Pseudomembranous colitis (rare but serious):** This is an inflammation of the colon and can be a serious side effect of oral clindamycin, although it is rare with topical application.  Symptoms include severe diarrhea, abdominal cramps, and fever.  Seek immediate medical attention if these occur.


**How it Works:**

* **Adapalene:**  This is a retinoid. It works by regulating cell turnover in the skin, preventing clogged pores (comedones) and reducing inflammation.
* **Clindamycin:** This is an antibiotic. It directly combats *Propionibacterium acnes*, the bacteria responsible for much of the inflammation associated with acne.  By reducing bacterial load, it helps to clear existing blemishes and prevent new ones.  The combination of adapalene and clindamycin offers a synergistic effect, addressing both the formation of comedones and the bacterial inflammation.
