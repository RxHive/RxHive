# Acenocoumarol 4mg

**Generic Name:** Acenocoumarol

**Usage:** Acenocoumarol is an anticoagulant (blood thinner) used to prevent blood clots from forming. It's primarily prescribed for:

* **Prevention of venous thromboembolism (VTE):** This includes deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE), often after surgery or prolonged immobilization.
* **Treatment of VTE:**  To prevent further clots from forming after a diagnosis of DVT or PE.
* **Prevention of thromboembolic complications in atrial fibrillation:**  In patients with atrial fibrillation (irregular heartbeat), acenocoumarol helps prevent clot formation that could lead to stroke.
* **Other conditions:**  May be used in other situations where blood clot prevention is necessary, as determined by a physician.

* **Bleeding:** This is the most significant risk, ranging from minor bruising and nosebleeds to more serious internal bleeding (which is a medical emergency).
* **Gastrointestinal problems:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain.
* **Skin reactions:** Rash, itching.
* **Fever:**  In rare cases.
* **Hair loss:** In rare cases.
* **Changes in liver function:**  Usually monitored through blood tests.

**Rare but serious side effects** include:  necrosis (tissue death), particularly in the skin and gastrointestinal tract; purpura fulminans (a severe form of blood clotting disorder)


**How it Works:** Acenocoumarol works by inhibiting the production of certain clotting factors in the liver. This reduces the blood's ability to clot, thereby preventing the formation of potentially dangerous blood clots.  It's a Vitamin K antagonist.
