# Aceclofenac

**Generic Name:** Aceclofenac

**Usage:** Aceclofenac is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain and inflammation caused by various conditions, including:

* Osteoarthritis:  Pain and inflammation in the joints.
* Rheumatoid arthritis:  A chronic inflammatory disorder affecting the joints.
* Ankylosing spondylitis:  A type of arthritis affecting the spine.
* Soft tissue injuries:  Pain and inflammation from sprains, strains, and other soft tissue trauma.
* Post-surgical pain:  Pain following surgical procedures.
* Dental pain:  Pain associated with dental procedures.
* Primary dysmenorrhea: Painful menstruation.


**Side Effects:**  Like all medications, aceclofenac can cause side effects.  These can range from mild to severe.  Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal:**  Heartburn, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, indigestion, ulcers (in rare cases).
* **Central Nervous System:**  Headache, dizziness, drowsiness.
* **Cardiovascular:**  Increased risk of heart attack, stroke, high blood pressure (although this risk is generally considered lower than with some other NSAIDs).  Fluid retention (edema).
* **Skin:**  Rash, itching, hives.
* **Renal:**  In rare cases, kidney problems.
* **Hepatic:** In rare cases, liver problems.

**Important Note:**  This is not an exhaustive list of side effects.  Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you experience any unusual or concerning symptoms.  Severe side effects require immediate medical attention.

**How it Works:** Aceclofenac works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which are substances in the body that cause pain, inflammation, and fever.  By reducing prostaglandin production, aceclofenac helps to relieve pain and inflammation.
