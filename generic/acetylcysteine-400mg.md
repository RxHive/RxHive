# Acetylcysteine 400mg

**Generic Name:** Acetylcysteine

**Usage:**

Acetylcysteine 400mg is used for several purposes, primarily:

* **Treatment of acetaminophen (paracetamol) overdose:** This is its most crucial use.  It works by replenishing glutathione, a liver antioxidant, which is depleted by acetaminophen toxicity.  Early administration is critical.
* **Mucous thinning:** It's used to help loosen and thin mucus in the lungs, making it easier to cough up. This is helpful in conditions like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), cystic fibrosis, and bronchitis.  It can be administered by inhalation or intravenously, depending on the condition and severity.
* **Prevention of contrast-induced nephropathy (CIN):**  Some studies suggest it may protect the kidneys from damage caused by contrast dye used in medical imaging procedures. However, its effectiveness in this area remains a subject of ongoing research and not universally accepted.


**Side Effects:**

Side effects can vary depending on the method of administration (oral, intravenous, inhalation). Common side effects include:

* **Oral:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomatitis (mouth sores), and stomach upset.  A rare but serious side effect is bronchospasm (constriction of the airways), especially in individuals with asthma or a history of respiratory problems.
* **Intravenous:** similar to oral but can also include flushing, hypotension (low blood pressure), and anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction).
* **Inhalation:** Bronchospasm, cough, and throat irritation are possible.


**How it Works:**

Acetylcysteine's mechanism of action differs slightly depending on its use:

* **Acetaminophen overdose:** It acts as a precursor to glutathione, a vital antioxidant that helps neutralize the toxic metabolite of acetaminophen (N-acetyl-p-benzoquinone imine, NAPQI) in the liver, preventing liver damage.
* **Mucus thinning:**  It breaks the disulfide bonds in mucus glycoproteins, reducing the viscosity (thickness) of mucus.  This makes it easier to expel from the respiratory tract.
