# Acetazolamide 250mg

**Generic Name:** Acetazolamide

**Usage:**

Acetazolamide is a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor used to treat several conditions, including:

* **Glaucoma:**  It reduces intraocular pressure (IOP) by decreasing the production of aqueous humor in the eye.  This is often used in open-angle glaucoma and secondary glaucoma.
* **Epilepsy (certain types):** It's used as an adjunctive therapy (along with other anti-seizure medications) for certain types of seizures, particularly petit mal (absence seizures) and myoclonic seizures in children.
* **Altitude sickness (acute mountain sickness):** It helps prevent or treat altitude sickness by increasing the excretion of bicarbonate from the kidneys, which helps to compensate for the respiratory alkalosis that can occur at high altitudes.
* **Idiopathic intracranial hypertension (pseudotumor cerebri):** It helps to reduce intracranial pressure.
* **Periodic paralysis:**  It can be used to help manage some forms of periodic paralysis.
* **Metabolic alkalosis:** It helps correct metabolic alkalosis by increasing the excretion of bicarbonate.  This is often used in a hospital setting.

**How it Works:**

Acetazolamide works by inhibiting carbonic anhydrase, an enzyme involved in the formation of aqueous humor in the eye and the regulation of acid-base balance in the body. By inhibiting this enzyme, it reduces the production of aqueous humor, lowering intraocular pressure.  It also affects the kidneys, increasing the excretion of bicarbonate and sodium, and altering the acid-base balance of the body.
