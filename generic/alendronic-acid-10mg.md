# Alendronic Acid 10mg

**Generic Name:** Alendronic Acid

**Usage:**

Alendronic acid is used to treat and prevent osteoporosis in postmenopausal women and men at increased risk of fractures.  It's also used to treat Paget's disease of bone and to prevent bone loss in men with androgen deficiency or hypogonadism.  The goal is to increase bone mineral density and reduce the risk of fractures.  It's crucial to note that alendronic acid treats osteoporosis, it doesn't cure it.  Lifestyle changes, such as a calcium-rich diet and regular weight-bearing exercise, are essential alongside medication.


**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* **Gastrointestinal issues:** These are the most common and can include heartburn, stomach pain, nausea, diarrhea, constipation, and esophageal irritation (inflammation of the esophagus).
* **Musculoskeletal pain:**  Muscle aches, joint pain, and bone pain are possible.
* **Headache:**  Headaches can occur.
* **Rare but serious side effects:**  These include atypical femoral fractures (fractures of the thigh bone that occur outside of areas of known osteoporosis), osteonecrosis of the jaw (bone death in the jaw), and esophageal ulceration (sores in the esophagus).  Severe allergic reactions are also possible, though rare.


**How it Works:**

Alendronic acid is a bisphosphonate. It works by slowing bone breakdown (resorption) and increasing bone density.  It inhibits the activity of osteoclasts, the cells responsible for breaking down bone tissue. This helps to maintain bone mass and reduces the risk of fractures.  The effects are cumulative; the longer it's taken, the greater the bone density increase.
