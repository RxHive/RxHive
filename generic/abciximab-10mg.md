# Abciximab 10mg

**Generic Name:** Abciximab

**Usage:**

Abciximab is a glycoprotein IIb/IIIa receptor antagonist used to prevent blood clots in patients undergoing procedures like percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), such as angioplasty or stent placement.  It's primarily used to reduce the risk of serious adverse cardiac events, such as death, myocardial infarction (heart attack), and urgent revascularization, in patients undergoing these procedures. It may also be used in certain unstable angina and acute coronary syndrome situations, usually in combination with other therapies.  **Important Note:** Abciximab is a prescription medication and should only be used under the direct supervision of a physician.  The specific usage will be determined by the doctor based on the individual patient's condition and risk factors.

**Side Effects:**

Common side effects include:

* Bleeding: This is the most significant risk associated with abciximab.  It can range from minor bleeding to serious, life-threatening hemorrhage.
* Thrombocytopenia (low platelet count):  A decrease in the number of platelets in the blood, increasing the risk of bleeding.
* Anemia (low red blood cell count)
* Allergic reactions (rash, itching, hives, swelling)
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Headache
* Fever


Serious side effects, though less common, can include:

* Gastrointestinal bleeding
* Intracranial hemorrhage (bleeding in the brain)
* Retroperitoneal hemorrhage (bleeding behind the abdominal cavity)
* Stroke


**How it Works:**

Abciximab is a monoclonal antibody that binds to the glycoprotein IIb/IIIa receptors on platelets. These receptors are crucial for platelet aggregation (clumping together). By blocking these receptors, abciximab prevents platelets from sticking together and forming clots, thus reducing the risk of blood clots in coronary arteries.
